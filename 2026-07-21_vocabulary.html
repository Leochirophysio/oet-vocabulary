Word 1 of 5
dysplasia
/dɪsˈpleɪ.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal development or growth of cells, tissues, or organs resulting in structural abnormalities; in paediatric physiotherapy, most commonly refers to developmental dysplasia of the hip (DDH), where the hip joint fails to form correctly in infancy.
繁體中文
發育不良；異常發育
Common Collocations
developmental dysplasia of the hip bronchopulmonary dysplasia skeletal dysplasia hip dysplasia cortical dysplasia
OET Clinical Example
"Routine neonatal screening identified left-sided developmental hip dysplasia; the infant was referred to the orthopaedic team for ultrasound-guided assessment and consideration of Pavlik harness treatment."
Related Words
dysplastic (adj) aplasia (complete absence) hyperplasia (overgrowth) hypoplasia (underdevelopment) malformation (structural abnormality)
Word 2 of 5
prematurity
/ˌprem.əˈtjʊə.rɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The condition of being born before 37 completed weeks of gestation; associated with increased risk of respiratory, neurological, gastrointestinal, and developmental complications, and requiring corrected age adjustment when assessing developmental milestones.
繁體中文
早產（狀況）；未成熟性
Common Collocations
extreme prematurity degree of prematurity sequelae of prematurity prematurity-related complications background of prematurity
OET Clinical Example
"This 14-month-old female has a background of extreme prematurity at 26 weeks gestation; developmental assessment using corrected age revealed motor skills consistent with an 11-month level, in keeping with known sequelae of prematurity."
Related Words
premature (adj) preterm (adj — born before 37 weeks) full-term (antonym — 37–42 weeks) corrected age (adjusted age for prematurity) gestation (length of pregnancy)
Word 3 of 5
mottling
/ˈmɒt.lɪŋ/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 小兒 Paediatric
Medical Definition
Patchy, irregular discolouration or marbling of the skin with alternating pale and bluish-red areas, indicating poor peripheral perfusion; a significant clinical sign in neonates and critically ill patients, often prompting urgent assessment.
繁體中文
皮膚花斑；斑駁樣皮膚改變
Common Collocations
skin mottling peripheral mottling generalised mottling mottling of the trunk progressive mottling
OET Clinical Example
"On examination, the neonate displayed generalised skin mottling and prolonged capillary refill time of four seconds, indicating poor peripheral perfusion; the medical team was notified immediately and the physiotherapy session was deferred."
Related Words
mottled (adj) livedo reticularis (net-like skin discolouration) pallor (paleness) cyanosis (blue discolouration) perfusion (blood flow to tissue)
Word 4 of 5
septicaemia
/ˌsep.tɪˈsiː.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A life-threatening condition in which bacteria enter the bloodstream and spread throughout the body, causing systemic infection and inflammation; commonly called blood poisoning. In neonates, may present with non-specific signs such as poor feeding, temperature instability, and mottling.
繁體中文
敗血症；菌血症（伴全身感染）
Common Collocations
neonatal septicaemia suspected septicaemia gram-negative septicaemia early-onset septicaemia risk of septicaemia
OET Clinical Example
"The neonate presented with poor feeding, temperature instability, and generalised irritability; blood cultures were obtained and empirical intravenous antibiotics commenced given clinical concern for early-onset neonatal septicaemia."
Related Words
sepsis (systemic inflammatory response to infection) septicaemic (adj) bacteraemia (bacteria in blood without systemic response) septic shock (severe septicaemia with organ failure) blood poisoning (lay term)
Word 5 of 5
atresia
/əˈtriː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Congenital absence or abnormal closure of a normal body opening or tubular passage; examples include oesophageal atresia (absent oesophagus), biliary atresia (absent bile ducts), and pulmonary atresia (absent pulmonary valve). Typically requires surgical correction in the neonatal period.
繁體中文
先天性閉鎖；管道閉塞（先天性）
Common Collocations
oesophageal atresia biliary atresia pulmonary atresia choanal atresia duodenal atresia
OET Clinical Example
"This three-week-old male was referred to the paediatric physiotherapy team for post-operative respiratory management following surgical repair of oesophageal atresia with tracheo-oesophageal fistula; his parents have been counselled regarding positioning and handling precautions."
Related Words
atretic (adj) stenosis (narrowing, not complete closure) fistula (abnormal connection between structures) congenital malformation (structural birth defect) patent (antonym — open/unobstructed)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 74)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

James Nguyen, a six-week-old male infant born at 28 weeks gestation (background of extreme prematurity), was referred from the neonatal intensive care unit to the paediatric physiotherapy team for post-operative respiratory care and developmental assessment following surgical repair of oesophageal atresia with tracheo-oesophageal fistula at day five of life.

On the morning of the physiotherapy review, nursing staff documented generalised skin mottling with pallor and a prolonged capillary refill time of five seconds. The senior physiotherapist suspended the planned assessment and alerted the medical team. Blood cultures were collected and empirical intravenous antibiotics commenced, given clinical suspicion of nosocomial septicaemia. The physiotherapy session was deferred pending clinical stabilisation and escalation of medical management.

Once James was medically stable, follow-up neonatal hip ultrasound performed at six weeks revealed bilateral hip dysplasia (Graf type IIa on the right, type IIb on the left); the orthopaedic team was notified and Pavlik harness fitting was scheduled.

Ongoing physiotherapy goals include post-operative airway clearance, positioning management to support feeding and pulmonary compliance, family education regarding developmental handling appropriate for corrected age, and coordination of care with orthopaedics for harness monitoring.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In paediatric referrals, always document the infant's gestational age and corrected age, relevant surgical history, and any acute clinical changes (e.g. mottling) that altered your planned intervention. This demonstrates clinical reasoning and justifies changes to the treatment plan. Use precise anatomical terms (e.g. "oesophageal atresia") rather than lay language wherever possible.
