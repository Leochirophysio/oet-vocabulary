Word 1 of 5
cardiogenic
/ˌkɑː.di.əʊˈdʒen.ɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary
Medical Definition
Originating from or caused by a disorder of the heart; used to describe conditions where the heart is the primary source of pathology.
繁體中文
心源性的；心因性的
Common Collocations
cardiogenic shock cardiogenic pulmonary oedema cardiogenic embolism cardiogenic syncope cardiogenic dyspnoea
OET Clinical Example
"The patient was admitted with cardiogenic pulmonary oedema secondary to decompensated left ventricular failure and has now been medically stabilised pending referral for supervised pulmonary rehabilitation."
Related Words
cardiac (adj — of the heart) cardiological (adj) myocardial (adj — of heart muscle) non-cardiogenic (antonym) cardiomyopathy (heart muscle disease)

pleural
/ˈplʊə.rəl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary
Medical Definition
Relating to the pleura — the thin, two-layered membrane that lines the chest cavity and encloses each lung; pertaining to the space or structures between these layers.
繁體中文
胸膜的；肋膜的
Common Collocations
pleural effusion pleural rub pleural cavity pleural drainage pleural empyema
OET Clinical Example
"Chest imaging confirmed a moderate right-sided pleural effusion, which is considered to be contributing significantly to the patient's progressive exertional dyspnoea and reduced exercise tolerance."
Related Words
pleura (noun — the membrane itself) pleurisy (inflammation of the pleura) pleurodesis (surgical adhesion procedure) pleuritis (formal — inflammation) thoracocentesis (draining pleural fluid)
Word 3 of 5
anasarca
/ˌæn.əˈsɑː.kə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 心肺 Cardiopulmonary
Medical Definition
Severe, generalised oedema involving the subcutaneous tissues throughout the entire body — including the limbs, trunk, and sometimes internal organs; typically a sign of advanced cardiac, renal, or hepatic failure.
繁體中文
全身性水腫；全身浮腫
Common Collocations
anasarca secondary to severe anasarca anasarca in heart failure develop anasarca anasarca with ascites
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs Clarke presented with anasarca secondary to end-stage renal failure, with generalised pitting oedema extending from the bilateral lower limbs to the sacral region and abdominal wall, significantly limiting her functional mobility."
Related Words
oedema (localised or generalised swelling) ascites (fluid accumulation in abdomen) lymphoedema (lymphatic swelling) anasarcous (adj) pitting oedema (leaves an indent on pressure)
Word 4 of 5
hyperhidrosis
/ˌhaɪ.pəˌhaɪˈdrəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormally excessive sweating that exceeds what is physiologically required for thermoregulation; may be localised (e.g. palms, axillae, feet) or generalised, and can be primary (idiopathic) or secondary to an underlying medical condition.
繁體中文
多汗症；過度出汗
Common Collocations
palmar hyperhidrosis axillary hyperhidrosis primary hyperhidrosis hyperhidrosis secondary to generalised hyperhidrosis
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported significant palmar hyperhidrosis that was adversely affecting his ability to grip therapeutic equipment during sessions and maintain adherence to the prescribed home exercise programme."
Related Words
diaphoresis (profuse sweating, often disease-related) hidrosis (sweating, general term) hypohidrosis (reduced sweating) anhidrosis (absence of sweating) hyperhidrotic (adj)
Word 5 of 5
haematemesis
/ˌhiː.məˈtem.ɪ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The vomiting of blood or blood-stained material originating from the upper gastrointestinal tract (oesophagus, stomach, or duodenum); an important clinical sign indicating upper GI haemorrhage.
繁體中文
吐血；嘔血
Common Collocations
frank haematemesis haematemesis and melaena episode of haematemesis haematemesis secondary to haematemesis requiring transfusion
OET Clinical Example
"Please note that this patient has a documented history of haematemesis related to peptic ulcer disease; non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs are therefore contraindicated, and analgesic options should be discussed with the medical team prior to commencing treatment."
Related Words
haemoptysis (blood coughed from airway — key distinction) melaena (dark tarry stools from upper GI bleed) haemorrhage (excessive bleeding) haematuria (blood in urine) haematemesis (British) / hematemesis (American)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 64)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr Harold Singh, a 72-year-old male with a background of ischaemic cardiomyopathy, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and chronic kidney disease stage 3, presented to the acute medical unit following a ten-day admission for decompensated heart failure.

On physiotherapy assessment, he demonstrated anasarca with 3+ pitting oedema extending bilaterally from the lower limbs to the sacral region, consistent with significant fluid overload. Auscultation revealed reduced air entry at both lung bases, and recent chest imaging confirmed bilateral pleural effusions, greater on the right. His exercise tolerance was markedly reduced; he was unable to ambulate more than 5 metres without rest, reporting cardiogenic dyspnoea and profound fatigue at minimal exertion.

Of note, the patient also reported an episode of haematemesis six months prior, attributed to peptic ulcer disease and now resolved; this renders non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs contraindicated for pain management. The patient also experiences hyperhidrosis of the palms, which has limited his ability to grip assistive devices effectively.

Goals of physiotherapy include graded mobilisation within haemodynamic parameters, patient education regarding fluid and activity management, and preparation for referral to a community cardiac rehabilitation programme upon medical stabilisation.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting contraindications in referral letters, be explicit — name the condition, the contraindicated treatment, and the reason. For example: "NSAIDs are contraindicated secondary to a prior history of haematemesis" gives the receiving clinician all the information they need to act safely.
