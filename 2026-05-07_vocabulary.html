equinus
/ɪˈkwaɪ.nəs/ adjective / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal 神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
A foot deformity in which the foot is held in permanent plantarflexion (toes pointing downward), preventing normal heel strike during gait; frequently associated with spastic cerebral palsy, stroke, and other upper motor neurone conditions.
繁體中文
馬蹄足（足部跖屈畸形）
Common Collocations
equinus deformity equinus gait equinus contracture bilateral equinus spastic equinus
OET Clinical Example
"Kaito, a 6-year-old with spastic diplegia secondary to cerebral palsy, presents with bilateral equinus deformity affecting his gait pattern and limiting safe independent ambulation on uneven terrain."
Related Words
plantarflexion (foot movement) equinovarus (combined deformity) ankle-foot orthosis / AFO (treatment) toe walking (lay description) calcaneus (opposite position)
Word 2 of 5
pyrexia
/paɪˈrek.si.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Elevation of body temperature above the normal range (typically >37.5°C); a common systemic response to infection, inflammation, or tissue injury, mediated by pyrogens acting on the hypothalamus.
繁體中文
發燒；發熱
Common Collocations
low-grade pyrexia high-grade pyrexia persistent pyrexia pyrexia of unknown origin post-operative pyrexia
OET Clinical Example
"The patient developed a low-grade pyrexia of 38.2°C on day two post-operatively; physiotherapy was deferred pending medical review and blood culture results."
Related Words
fever (lay term) febrile (adj — having a fever) afebrile (antonym — no fever) hyperthermia (heat-related, non-infective) pyrexial (adj)
Word 3 of 5
bradycardia
/ˌbræd.ɪˈkɑː.di.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormally slow heart rate, defined as fewer than 60 beats per minute in adults; may be physiological (e.g. in trained athletes) or pathological, causing symptoms such as dizziness, fatigue, and syncope.
繁體中文
心跳過緩；心動過緩
Common Collocations
sinus bradycardia symptomatic bradycardia relative bradycardia bradycardia at rest exercise-induced bradycardia
OET Clinical Example
"Mobilisation was suspended when the patient developed symptomatic bradycardia during the sit-to-stand transfer, with heart rate dropping to 44 bpm and associated light-headedness; the treating physiotherapist notified the ward nurse immediately."
Related Words
tachycardia (antonym — fast heart rate) bradycardic (adj) arrhythmia (irregular rhythm) heart rate / HR (clinical shorthand) pacemaker (treatment for severe cases)
Word 4 of 5
pallor
/ˈpæl.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal paleness or loss of colour from the skin or mucous membranes, typically indicating reduced blood flow, anaemia, shock, or vasovagal response; assessed clinically at the face, conjunctivae, and nail beds.
繁體中文
面色蒼白；膚色蒼白
Common Collocations
facial pallor marked pallor pallor and diaphoresis conjunctival pallor sudden onset pallor
OET Clinical Example
"During the initial mobilisation attempt, the patient exhibited marked pallor, diaphoresis, and reported feeling faint; the session was terminated and the patient returned to supine with legs elevated."
Related Words
pallid (adj) cyanosis (blue discolouration) erythema (redness — opposite) anaemia (common cause) vasovagal response (mechanism)

Medical Definition
An abnormally large head circumference, defined as a measurement greater than two standard deviations above the mean for age and sex; causes include hydrocephalus, megalencephaly, metabolic storage disorders, and benign familial variation.
繁體中文
巨頭症；頭圍過大
Common Collocations
benign familial macrocephaly progressive macrocephaly macrocephaly with hydrocephalus isolated macrocephaly macrocephaly on centile chart
OET Clinical Example
"The referring paediatrician documented progressive macrocephaly with a head circumference tracking above the 99th percentile and requested physiotherapy assessment in the context of associated gross motor developmental delay."
Related Words
microcephaly (antonym — small head) macrocephalic (adj) hydrocephalus (common cause) head circumference / HC (measurement) plagiocephaly (asymmetric flattening)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 18)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Aroha, a 72-year-old female with a background of severe aortic stenosis and iron-deficiency anaemia, was referred to the inpatient physiotherapy service for post-operative rehabilitation following elective aortic valve replacement.

On initial assessment, the patient demonstrated marked pallor of the conjunctivae and nail beds, consistent with her known anaemia. Vital signs monitoring during the initial sit-to-stand transfer revealed symptomatic bradycardia, with heart rate dropping from 58 bpm at rest to 41 bpm on standing, accompanied by dizziness and diaphoresis. The session was terminated and the patient returned to supine with legs elevated; the ward nurse was notified immediately.

The patient also reported a low-grade pyrexia of 37.8°C earlier that morning, which had been documented by nursing staff. In light of these findings, physiotherapy was deferred pending cardiology review and optimisation of the patient's haemodynamic status.

Goals upon medical clearance include progressive seated exercise, lower limb strengthening, and a graded return to ambulation with close haemodynamic monitoring throughout.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting an adverse event during physiotherapy, clearly record the specific observation (e.g. "HR dropped to 41 bpm"), the patient's symptoms (e.g. "dizziness and diaphoresis"), the action taken (e.g. "session terminated, returned to supine"), and who was notified. This protects the patient and demonstrates professional accountability.
