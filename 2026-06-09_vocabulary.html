somnolence
/ˈsɒm.nə.ləns/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Excessive drowsiness or sleepiness; a state of abnormal, difficult-to-resist drowsiness that may indicate neurological compromise, medication side effects, or systemic illness.
繁體中文
嗜睡；過度困倦
Common Collocations
post-ictal somnolence daytime somnolence drug-induced somnolence profound somnolence somnolence following TBI
OET Clinical Example
"The patient demonstrated marked somnolence following commencement of high-dose opioid analgesia, necessitating close neurological monitoring and a review of the prescribed regimen."
Related Words
somnolent (adj) drowsiness (lay term) obtundation (deeper impairment) stupor (more severe) hypersomnia (excessive sleep)

hypertonia
/ˌhaɪ.pəˈtəʊ.ni.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological 骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormally increased muscle tone or tension; a condition in which muscles resist passive movement and are in a state of excessive stiffness, most commonly seen following upper motor neuron lesions such as stroke or spinal cord injury.
繁體中文
肌張力過高；肌肉張力亢進
Common Collocations
upper limb hypertonia bilateral hypertonia hypertonia secondary to stroke manage hypertonia hypertonia associated with spasticity
OET Clinical Example
"Physiotherapy was requested for the management of lower limb hypertonia secondary to a recent cerebrovascular accident, with the primary goals of preventing contracture formation and improving functional mobility."
Related Words
hypotonia (antonym — low tone) spasticity (velocity-dependent hypertonia) rigidity (non-velocity-dependent hypertonia) hypertonic (adj) tone (general term)

osteotomy
/ˌɒs.tiˈɒt.ə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A surgical procedure in which a bone is cut to realign, shorten, or lengthen it, or to correct a structural deformity; commonly performed at the knee, hip, or foot to redistribute mechanical load or restore normal alignment.
繁體中文
截骨術；骨切除術
Common Collocations
high tibial osteotomy corrective osteotomy peri-acetabular osteotomy valgus-producing osteotomy post-osteotomy rehabilitation
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Thompson is a 52-year-old male referred for physiotherapy rehabilitation following a right high tibial osteotomy performed three weeks ago to address medial compartment osteoarthritis and varus malalignment."
Related Words
arthroplasty (joint replacement) osteoplasty (bone reshaping) corrective surgery (general) tibial osteotomy femoral osteotomy
Word 4 of 5
synovial
/sɪˈnəʊ.vi.əl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Relating to or produced by the synovium; the synovial membrane lines the inner surface of joint capsules and tendon sheaths, secreting synovial fluid that lubricates articular cartilage and provides joint nutrition.
繁體中文
滑液的；滑膜的
Common Collocations
synovial fluid synovial joint synovial membrane synovial inflammation synovial hypertrophy
OET Clinical Example
"Ultrasound-guided aspiration of the right knee was performed, yielding 15 mL of turbid synovial fluid consistent with inflammatory arthropathy; the sample was sent for microbiological analysis and crystal identification."
Related Words
synovium (noun — the membrane) synovitis (inflammation of synovium) intra-articular (within the joint) joint capsule bursa (similar fluid-filled sac)

refractory

Medical Definition
Resistant to treatment or management; used to describe a condition, symptom, or patient that does not respond satisfactorily to standard therapeutic interventions, necessitating escalation or alternative management strategies.
繁體中文

Related Words
intractable (synonym) treatment-resistant (synonym) recalcitrant (synonym) responsive (antonym) refractory period (cardiac/neural)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 49)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Park, a 58-year-old male with a background of bilateral knee osteoarthritis, is referred to your physiotherapy department following a left high tibial osteotomy performed six weeks ago to correct varus malalignment.

Pre-operatively, Mr. Park had undergone twelve months of conservative management including physiotherapy, activity modification, and oral analgesia; however, his pain remained refractory to these measures, precipitating the surgical intervention. On initial post-operative assessment, mild hypertonia was noted in the left quadriceps, likely attributable to pain inhibition and prolonged weight-bearing restriction rather than any neurological cause.

Imaging has also confirmed a residual synovial effusion in the operative knee, which is being managed conservatively with elevation and compression. Mr. Park is otherwise alert and cooperative, with no evidence of post-anaesthetic somnolence at the time of assessment.

Physiotherapy goals include progressive weight-bearing mobilisation, restoration of knee range of motion, lower limb strengthening, and patient education regarding activity pacing and warning signs of post-operative complications.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters following orthopaedic procedures, always document the indication for surgery (e.g. "refractory to conservative management"), current functional status, and any concurrent findings (e.g. synovial effusion, hypertonia) that may influence the rehabilitation plan. Use precise anatomical and clinical language rather than general descriptors.
