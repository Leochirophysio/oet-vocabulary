microcephaly
/ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈsɛf.ə.li/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A neurological condition in which the head circumference is significantly smaller than expected for a child's age and sex, indicating abnormal or incomplete brain development; frequently associated with intellectual disability and global developmental delay.
繁體中文
小頭症
Common Collocations
congenital microcephaly severe microcephaly postnatal microcephaly microcephaly and developmental delay microcephaly associated with infection
OET Clinical Example
"James, a 10-month-old male with a confirmed diagnosis of congenital microcephaly, was referred to paediatric physiotherapy for assessment and intervention to address significant global developmental delay, including poor head control and absent antigravity movements."
Related Words
macrocephaly (antonym — enlarged head) microcephalic (adj) craniosynostosis (differential) hydrocephalus (differential) cephalic index

coryza
/kəˈraɪ.zə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the mucous membranes lining the nasal cavities, characterised by nasal discharge, congestion, and sneezing; commonly associated with upper respiratory tract infections and frequently encountered in paediatric patients.
繁體中文
鼻炎；流鼻水（傷風）
Common Collocations
acute coryza coryza and pyrexia viral coryza coryza with productive cough presenting with coryza
OET Clinical Example
"Mia, a 4-year-old, presented with a five-day history of coryza, low-grade pyrexia, and a productive cough; physiotherapy assessment was requested to assist with airway clearance as symptoms had progressed to involve the lower respiratory tract."
Related Words
rhinitis (medical synonym) rhinorrhoea (runny nose) coryzal (adj) URTI (upper respiratory tract infection) nasal congestion
Word 3 of 5
purpura
/ˈpɜː.pjʊ.rə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A skin condition characterised by purple or reddish-brown discolouration caused by bleeding from small blood vessels beneath the skin; a non-blanching rash that is a critical clinical sign potentially indicating serious underlying pathology such as septicaemia, vasculitis, or clotting disorders.
繁體中文
紫癜
Common Collocations
non-blanching purpura purpuric rash Henoch–Schönlein purpura idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura petechial purpura
OET Clinical Example
"During the physiotherapy session, a new non-blanching purpuric rash was observed bilaterally on the child's lower limbs; the session was immediately suspended, the treating medical team was notified, and urgent review was arranged."
Related Words
petechiae (smaller punctate spots) ecchymosis (larger bruising) purpuric (adj) thrombocytopenia (low platelets — common cause) vasculitis (inflammation of blood vessels)

incontinence
/ɪnˈkɒn.tɪ.nəns/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The involuntary loss of bladder or bowel control resulting in unintended leakage of urine or faeces; in children, may reflect delayed sphincter maturation, neurological involvement, or pelvic floor dysfunction and is a common referral reason to paediatric physiotherapy.
繁體中文
失禁
Common Collocations
urinary incontinence faecal incontinence nocturnal incontinence stress incontinence functional incontinence
OET Clinical Example
"Oliver, a 6-year-old boy, was referred to physiotherapy with a six-month history of nocturnal urinary incontinence and daytime urgency; conservative pelvic floor rehabilitation and bladder training were commenced in coordination with the paediatric continence nurse."
Related Words
enuresis (urinary incontinence in children) encopresis (faecal incontinence in children) incontinent (adj) continent (antonym) continence (noun) pelvic floor dysfunction

regression
/rɪˈɡrɛʃ.ən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
In developmental paediatrics, the loss of previously acquired skills or return to an earlier stage of functioning, encompassing motor, communication, or social abilities; a clinically significant finding that may indicate neurological, psychological, or systemic medical conditions requiring urgent investigation.
繁體中文
退化；發展技能退步
Common Collocations
developmental regression regression of skills motor regression regression following illness regression of milestones
OET Clinical Example
"Parents report a marked regression in Ethan's gross motor skills over the preceding fortnight, with loss of independent walking that had been achieved at 14 months; urgent neurological review has been requested to exclude a progressive underlying condition."
Related Words
regress (verb) regressive (adj) developmental deterioration (synonym) milestone loss skill loss
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 60)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Dear Paediatric Neurology Team,

I am writing to refer Liam Chen, a 20-month-old male with a known diagnosis of congenital microcephaly, for urgent review. His parents have reported a significant regression in gross motor skills over the past three weeks, with loss of independent standing that had been previously achieved at 16 months.

He has recently recovered from a febrile illness characterised by coryza, productive cough, and pyrexia lasting seven days. Of note, a non-blanching purpuric rash was observed over his lower limbs during today's physiotherapy session; the session was suspended immediately and the on-call medical registrar was notified. Liam is currently awaiting full blood count and coagulation screen results.

His parents also report episodes of nocturnal urinary incontinence, which represents a regression from previously achieved continence. Physiotherapy rehabilitation will be deferred pending your review and medical clearance. Please do not hesitate to contact our department should further information be required.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting a child safeguarding or urgent clinical concern (such as a non-blanching rash or skill regression), state clearly: what you observed, when you observed it, what action you took immediately, and what has been arranged. Passive constructions such as "the team was notified" and "the session was suspended" are appropriate in formal clinical letters.
