Medical Definition

systolic
/sɪˈstɒl.ɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to or occurring during systole — the phase of the cardiac cycle during which the ventricles contract and eject blood into the aorta and pulmonary artery. The upper number in a blood pressure reading represents systolic pressure.
繁體中文
收縮期的
Common Collocations
systolic blood pressure systolic murmur systolic dysfunction systolic heart failure systolic ejection fraction
OET Clinical Example
"On arrival to the emergency department, the patient's systolic blood pressure was recorded at 82 mmHg, which failed to improve adequately following a 500 mL crystalloid bolus, prompting referral to the intensive care unit."
Related Words
systole (noun — contraction phase) diastolic (antonym — relaxation phase) ejection fraction (cardiac output measure) hypertension (elevated BP) hypotension (low BP)

Medical Definition
The surgical or therapeutic destruction and removal of tissue, an organ, or a body part; in cardiology, the targeted destruction of abnormal electrical pathways in the heart responsible for arrhythmias, most commonly using radiofrequency energy or extreme cold (cryoablation).
繁體中文

Related Words
ablate (verb) ablative (adj) cardioversion (electrical reversion) arrhythmia (abnormal rhythm) cryoablation (cold-based ablation)

Medical Definition
Relating to or involving the diaphragm — the dome-shaped musculotendinous partition separating the thoracic and abdominal cavities, which serves as the primary muscle of inspiration. Used to describe breathing technique, hernia type, or nerve involvement.

Related Words

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 76)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Daniel Park, a 61-year-old male with a known history of ischaemic heart disease and hypertension, was admitted to the cardiology unit on 18 July following an episode of sustained ventricular tachycardia. His admission blood pressure was 90/58 mmHg, with a systolic component that failed to respond to initial pharmacological management.

Echocardiography on Day 2 of admission identified a small pericardial effusion, with fluid noted within the pericardium estimated at 150 mL; this was monitored closely and did not progress to haemodynamic compromise. He subsequently underwent successful catheter ablation for his recurrent ventricular tachycardia on 22 July, with restoration and maintenance of sinus rhythm confirmed on 24-hour Holter monitoring.

Mr. Park was referred to cardiopulmonary physiotherapy for post-procedure rehabilitation. On assessment, he demonstrated reduced respiratory efficiency with excessive accessory muscle use. Diaphragmatic breathing retraining was commenced, with the patient achieving satisfactory technique by the second session. He was discharged on 25 July with a home exercise programme and instructions to monitor his systolic blood pressure twice daily.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting cardiac procedures in referral letters, specify the procedure type (e.g. "catheter ablation"), the indication (e.g. "ventricular tachycardia"), and the outcome (e.g. "restoration of sinus rhythm"). Avoid vague terms like "heart procedure" or "something for the rhythm."
