Medical Definition
An inability to recognise objects, people, sounds, or sensations despite intact sensory organ function and normal intelligence; results from damage to specific cortical association areas, typically following stroke or brain injury.

Medical Definition
Partial or incomplete paralysis; muscular weakness due to nerve damage or neurological disease, distinguished from plegia (complete loss of motor function) by the preservation of some voluntary movement.

Word 3 of 5
spondylolisthesis
/ˌspɒn.dɪ.ləʊˈlɪs.θɪ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A spinal condition in which one vertebra slips forward over the vertebra immediately below it; most commonly occurring at the L4–L5 or L5–S1 level, and graded I–IV according to the degree of slippage. May cause low back pain, buttock pain, and lower limb neurological symptoms.
繁體中文
脊椎滑脫症
Common Collocations
lumbar spondylolisthesis Grade I spondylolisthesis isthmic spondylolisthesis degenerative spondylolisthesis symptomatic spondylolisthesis
OET Clinical Example
"Lumbar MRI confirmed a Grade II L4–L5 spondylolisthesis with moderate bilateral neural foraminal narrowing, consistent with the patient's reported buttock pain radiating to the posterior thighs and reduced walking tolerance of fewer than 100 metres."
Related Words
spondylolysis (pars stress fracture) anterolisthesis (forward slip) retrolisthesis (backward slip) spondylosis (degenerative changes) spinal instability

Medical Definition
The process of bone formation; clinically most relevant as heterotopic ossification, the pathological development of bone within soft tissues such as muscle or periarticular structures following trauma, surgery, spinal cord injury, or acquired brain injury.
繁體中文
骨化（作用）；骨質化

OET Clinical Example
"Serial radiographs identified periarticular heterotopic ossification around the right hip, a recognised complication following the patient's recent traumatic brain injury, which was contributing to a progressive restriction in hip range of motion and impeding rehabilitation progress."
Related Words
ossify (verb) ossified (adj) calcification (calcium deposits) osteogenesis (bone growth) myositis ossificans (muscle)
Word 5 of 5
ipsilateral
/ˌɪp.sɪˈlæt.ər.əl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Situated on, pertaining to, or affecting the same side of the body as a given point of reference (e.g. a lesion or injury); the anatomical opposite of contralateral. Essential for describing the laterality of neurological signs and musculoskeletal symptoms relative to a known pathology.
繁體中文
同側的
Common Collocations
ipsilateral weakness ipsilateral limb ipsilateral cerebellar signs ipsilateral hip pain ipsilateral facial droop
OET Clinical Example
"Following a right L4–L5 disc prolapse, the patient reported ipsilateral lower limb pain radiating to the right foot, with ipsilateral ankle dorsiflexion weakness graded MRC 3/5, consistent with L5 nerve root compromise."
Related Words
contralateral (opposite side — antonym) bilateral (both sides) unilateral (one side) homolateral (synonym — less common) ipsilaterally (adverb)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 28)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr David Kim, a 47-year-old male office worker, was referred to the outpatient physiotherapy department following MRI confirmation of a Grade II L4–L5 spondylolisthesis with right-sided neural foraminal compromise.

He presented with a six-week history of low back pain radiating into the ipsilateral right lower limb along the L4–L5 dermatomal distribution. Lower limb neurological assessment revealed ipsilateral ankle dorsiflexion paresis graded MRC 3/5 and diminished sensation over the right dorsal foot, consistent with L5 nerve root involvement. Knee and ankle reflexes were intact bilaterally.

Of note, follow-up imaging from the treating orthopaedic surgeon flagged early periarticular ossification at the right facet joints, likely secondary to chronic mechanical irritation, which may further limit spinal mobility if left unaddressed.

Physiotherapy goals include lumbar stabilisation exercises, neural mobilisation for the affected L4–L5 nerve root, patient education regarding load management and posture, and progressive strengthening to address the ipsilateral lower limb paresis. Referral for surgical review is recommended if neurological deficits fail to improve within eight weeks of conservative management.

📌 OET Writing Tip: Always specify laterality precisely using "ipsilateral" or "contralateral" rather than simply "same side" or "other side." In referral letters, pair neurological findings with a grading system (e.g. MRC scale) and link symptoms to the confirmed imaging diagnosis for maximum clinical clarity.
