Medical Definition

Medical Definition
Describing a symptom or event that occurs in sudden, brief attacks or episodes, reaching peak intensity rapidly and typically resolving abruptly; applied to coughing, pain, dyspnoea, arrhythmias, and other clinical phenomena.

Related Words
paroxysm (noun — sudden attack) episodic (similar meaning) intermittent (recurring but less abrupt) PND (paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea) acute onset

desaturation
/ˌdiː.sætʃ.əˈreɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A fall in blood oxygen saturation (SpO₂), typically measured by pulse oximetry; a clinically significant indicator of respiratory compromise during exercise, at rest, or during sleep.
繁體中文
血氧去飽和（氧飽和度下降）
Common Collocations
oxygen desaturation exercise-induced desaturation nocturnal desaturation significant desaturation desaturation on exertion
OET Clinical Example
"During the six-minute walk test, the patient demonstrated significant oxygen desaturation from 96% at rest to 82% at peak exertion, indicating exercise-induced hypoxaemia and a requirement for supervised rehabilitation with supplemental oxygen."
Related Words
SpO₂ (peripheral oxygen saturation) hypoxaemia (low arterial oxygen) pulse oximetry (measurement method) saturate / saturation (base form) supplemental oxygen (intervention)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 43)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs. Nguyen Thi Lan, a 72-year-old female with a background of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (GOLD Stage III) and recently diagnosed left ventricular failure, was referred to the physiotherapy outpatient department for pulmonary rehabilitation assessment.

Her GP notes documented paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea on two occasions in the past fortnight, requiring her to sit upright for 30 minutes before symptoms resolved. On initial physiotherapy assessment, resting SpO₂ was 91% on room air, consistent with mild hypoxaemia at rest.

A six-minute walk test was conducted with continuous pulse oximetry monitoring. The patient demonstrated significant oxygen desaturation to 79% at the three-minute mark, necessitating early cessation of the test. Supplemental oxygen at 2 L/min via nasal prongs was commenced, with SpO₂ recovering to 90% within five minutes of rest.

Additionally, the patient reported odynophagia when taking oral medications, which she attributed to recent changes in her tablet formulation. A referral to the speech language therapy team for a swallowing assessment was initiated in parallel.

Physiotherapy goals include a supervised, oxygen-titrated exercise programme, breathing retraining, and energy conservation education to optimise functional capacity within safe desaturation limits.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting oxygen desaturation, always specify the baseline SpO₂, the lowest recorded value, and the activity or context in which it occurred (e.g. "desaturation to 79% at 3 minutes during 6MWT"). This precision reflects real clinical handover language and scores highly in OET writing tasks.
