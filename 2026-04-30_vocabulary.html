radiculopathy
/ˌræd.ɪ.kjʊˈlɒp.ə.θi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Disease or dysfunction of one or more spinal nerve roots, typically caused by compression from a herniated disc or bony spur, producing pain, weakness, or altered sensation that radiates along the nerve's distribution.
繁體中文
神經根病變
Common Collocations
cervical radiculopathy lumbar radiculopathy L5 radiculopathy bilateral radiculopathy radiculopathy secondary to disc herniation
OET Clinical Example
"MRI of the lumbar spine confirmed L5–S1 disc herniation consistent with right-sided radiculopathy, characterised by shooting pain radiating from the buttock to the dorsum of the right foot."
Related Words
radicular pain (adj form) nerve root compression (synonym) myelopathy (spinal cord involvement) sciatica (L4–S1 specific) dermatome (nerve root territory)

impingement
/ɪmˈpɪndʒ.mənt/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The mechanical compression or trapping of soft tissue — most commonly a tendon or bursa — between bony structures, producing pain, a painful arc of movement, and functional restriction. Most frequently described in the shoulder (subacromial space).
繁體中文
夾擠症候群；撞擊徵
Common Collocations
shoulder impingement subacromial impingement impingement syndrome neural impingement impingement sign positive
OET Clinical Example
"The patient was referred with a six-month history of right shoulder impingement, presenting with a painful arc between 70° and 120° of abduction and a positive Hawkins–Kennedy test bilaterally."
Related Words
impinge (verb) rotator cuff (commonly affected structure) bursitis (associated condition) painful arc (clinical sign) decompression (surgical treatment)

paraesthesia
/ˌpær.ɪsˈθiː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormal sensation in the skin — typically described as tingling, prickling, or "pins and needles" — occurring without obvious external stimulus; may be transient or persistent and is commonly associated with nerve compression or peripheral nerve dysfunction.
繁體中文
感覺異常；麻刺感
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported intermittent paraesthesia along the medial border of the left forearm and into the fourth and fifth digits, consistent with a C8 nerve root distribution and exacerbated by sustained cervical flexion."
Related Words
paraesthetic (adj) paresthesia (American spelling) numbness / hypoaesthesia (reduced sensation) anaesthesia (absence of sensation) dysaesthesia (unpleasant abnormal sensation)
Word 4 of 5
antalgic
/ænˈtæl.dʒɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Describing a posture, movement pattern, or gait adopted involuntarily to minimise pain; in gait analysis, an antalgic pattern involves a shortened stance phase on the painful side as the patient minimises weight-bearing time to reduce discomfort.
繁體中文
止痛性的（為減輕疼痛而採取的姿勢或步態）
Common Collocations
antalgic gait antalgic lean antalgic posture antalgic scoliosis marked antalgic pattern
OET Clinical Example
"On observational gait analysis, the patient demonstrated a marked antalgic gait pattern with a significantly shortened stance phase on the left lower limb, consistent with his reported left hip pain and reduced weight-bearing tolerance."
Related Words
analgesic (pain-relieving, related root) Trendelenburg gait (different gait pattern) pain-avoidance posture (lay description) compensatory gait (broader category) gait deviation (clinical term)

stenosis
/stɪˈnəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal narrowing of a canal, passage, or hollow structure in the body; in a musculoskeletal context, most commonly refers to narrowing of the spinal canal or intervertebral foramina, compressing neural structures and producing pain, weakness, and neurogenic symptoms in the limbs.
繁體中文
狹窄；管腔縮小
Common Collocations
spinal stenosis lumbar stenosis cervical stenosis foraminal stenosis moderate / severe stenosis
OET Clinical Example
"MRI findings are consistent with moderate lumbar spinal stenosis at L3–L4, producing bilateral lower limb heaviness and neurogenic symptoms that limit comfortable walking distance to approximately 80 metres."
Related Words
stenotic (adj) narrowing (lay term) foraminal narrowing (related descriptor) myelopathy (cord compression sequel) neurogenic claudication (common presentation)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 12)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 58-year-old male office worker, was referred to your physiotherapy outpatient clinic by his general practitioner for assessment and management of lower back and right leg symptoms of approximately four months' duration.

MRI of the lumbar spine completed two weeks prior confirmed moderate lumbar spinal stenosis at L4–L5, with associated foraminal narrowing producing right-sided radiculopathy. The patient reported constant aching in the right buttock with intermittent paraesthesia extending to the dorsum of the right foot, rated 6/10 at rest and 9/10 on prolonged standing or walking.

Observational gait analysis on initial assessment revealed a pronounced antalgic gait pattern, with markedly reduced stance phase on the right and a flexed lumbar posture adopted to decompress the affected nerve root. Walking tolerance was limited to approximately 70 metres on level ground before onset of leg heaviness necessitated rest.

Goals of physiotherapy include neural desensitisation, graded walking rehabilitation, lumbar stabilisation exercises, and education regarding posture and activity modification to reduce ongoing impingement of the affected nerve root.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting neurological symptoms in a referral, always specify the distribution (e.g. "dorsum of the right foot"), the nature (e.g. "paraesthesia" vs "pain"), and aggravating/relieving factors (e.g. "worse on prolonged standing, relieved by flexion"). This precision demonstrates clinical reasoning and is rewarded in OET writing tasks.
