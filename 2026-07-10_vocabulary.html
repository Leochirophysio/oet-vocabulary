Medical Definition

Word 2 of 5
isometric
/ˌaɪ.səˈmɛt.rɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Describing a type of muscle contraction in which the muscle generates tension without changing its length and therefore without producing joint movement; used in early rehabilitation when joint loading or movement is contraindicated.
繁體中文
等長收縮的；靜態肌肉收縮
Common Collocations
isometric contraction isometric exercise isometric strengthening isometric holds isometric quadriceps exercise
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chen was commenced on isometric quadriceps and hip abductor exercises in the immediate post-operative period to maintain muscle activation whilst minimising stress on the surgical repair."
Related Words
isotonic (movement-based contraction) isokinetic (constant-velocity) concentric (muscle shortening) eccentric (muscle lengthening) static exercise (lay term)

dislocation
/ˌdɪs.ləˈkeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Complete loss of contact between the articular surfaces of a joint, in which the bones are fully displaced from their normal anatomical position; typically caused by trauma and requiring prompt reduction to restore joint alignment.
繁體中文
脫臼；關節脫位
Common Collocations
shoulder dislocation anterior dislocation recurrent dislocation first-episode dislocation joint dislocation
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Patel was referred following a first-episode anterior shoulder dislocation sustained during sport; closed reduction was performed in the emergency department and he was subsequently referred for post-immobilisation rehabilitation."
Related Words
subluxation (partial dislocation) reduction (restoring alignment) dislocate (verb) relocation (synonym for reduction) recurrence (re-dislocation)

plyometric
/ˌplaɪ.əˈmɛt.rɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Describing exercises that exploit the stretch-shortening cycle, involving rapid eccentric loading of a muscle immediately followed by a concentric contraction to develop explosive power and neuromuscular control; a key component of sport-specific rehabilitation.
繁體中文
增強式的；爆發力訓練的
Common Collocations
plyometric training plyometric programme plyometric exercises plyometric loading single-leg plyometrics
OET Clinical Example
"Following successful completion of the strength and neuromuscular phases, Miss Tran progressed to a plyometric programme incorporating double-leg box jumps and lateral hops in preparation for return to competitive netball."
Related Words
stretch-shortening cycle (mechanism) eccentric (loading phase) explosive training (lay term) reactive neuromuscular training jump training (synonym)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 65)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Nguyen, a 24-year-old recreational footballer, was referred to our physiotherapy service following a right anterior shoulder dislocation sustained during a weekend match eight weeks ago. Closed reduction was performed in the emergency department on the day of injury and the limb was subsequently immobilised in a sling for three weeks.

On initial assessment, Mr. Nguyen demonstrated marked right scapular dyskinesis with impaired lower trapezius and serratus anterior activation, alongside rotator cuff weakness graded 3/5 on manual muscle testing. There was no evidence of recurrent instability at this stage.

A graduated rehabilitation programme was initiated, commencing with isometric rotator cuff and periscapular strengthening exercises performed in pain-free ranges before progressing to dynamic isotonic loading. Concurrently, a proprioceptive retraining protocol was implemented to address neuromuscular deficits secondary to the injury.

Following eight weeks of structured rehabilitation, Mr. Nguyen has met the criteria for advanced sport-specific training and has been progressed to a plyometric programme incorporating single-leg landing tasks, lateral bounds, and overhead throwing drills. A graded return-to-football plan has been agreed upon, with full clearance anticipated within four weeks subject to satisfactory performance in functional testing.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting rehabilitation progression in referral letters, specify the phase of rehabilitation (e.g. isometric → isotonic → plyometric), functional criteria met at each stage, and an anticipated timeline for return to activity. Avoid vague language such as "doing well" — use measurable outcomes instead.
