Word 1 of 5
myelitis
/ˌmaɪ.əˈlaɪ.tɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the spinal cord, which can impair both motor and sensory function below the level of the lesion; may be caused by infection, autoimmune disease, or demyelination.
繁體中文
脊髓炎
Common Collocations
transverse myelitis acute myelitis viral myelitis myelitis secondary to MS cervical myelitis
OET Clinical Example
"Mr Chen was referred to the neurological rehabilitation unit following an acute episode of transverse myelitis at the T6 level, resulting in bilateral lower limb weakness and impaired bladder control."
Related Words
myelopathy (non-inflammatory cord disease) demyelination (myelin sheath damage) encephalomyelitis (brain + cord) myelitic (adj) spinal cord injury (SCI)
Word 2 of 5
cerebrovascular
/ˌser.ɪ.brəʊˈvæs.kjʊ.lə/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
Relating to the blood vessels that supply the brain; used to describe conditions or events involving disruption of blood flow to cerebral tissue.
繁體中文
腦血管的
Common Collocations
cerebrovascular accident (CVA) cerebrovascular disease cerebrovascular event cerebrovascular risk factors cerebrovascular insufficiency
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs Kim has a significant history of cerebrovascular disease, having sustained two ischaemic events over the past three years, and was referred for physiotherapy following her most recent CVA six weeks ago."
Related Words
CVA / stroke (clinical abbreviation) TIA (transient ischaemic attack) ischaemia (reduced blood flow) haemorrhagic (bleeding type) carotid stenosis
Word 3 of 5
anosmia
/æˈnɒz.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Complete absence of the sense of smell, which may result from nasal obstruction, head trauma, viral infection, or damage to cranial nerve I (olfactory nerve).
繁體中文
嗅覺喪失
Common Collocations
post-viral anosmia anosmia following head injury persistent anosmia anosmia and ageusia bilateral anosmia
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported persistent anosmia following a COVID-19 infection four months ago; olfactory function has not returned, and she was referred to neurology for further evaluation of cranial nerve I involvement."
Related Words
hyposmia (partial loss of smell) ageusia (loss of taste) olfactory (adj — relating to smell) anosmic (adj) cranial nerve I
Word 4 of 5
self-efficacy
/ˌself ˈef.ɪ.kə.si/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
A patient's belief in their own capacity to successfully perform the behaviours needed to manage their health condition; a key predictor of rehabilitation outcomes and treatment adherence.
繁體中文
自我效能感
Common Collocations
low self-efficacy improve self-efficacy pain self-efficacy self-efficacy for exercise self-efficacy beliefs
OET Clinical Example
"On assessment, Mrs Patel demonstrated low self-efficacy regarding her ability to complete her home exercise programme independently; motivational interviewing strategies were incorporated into subsequent sessions to address this barrier."
Related Words
confidence (lay term) motivation (driving force) autonomy (self-direction) self-management (patient skill) locus of control
Word 5 of 5
empowerment
/ɪmˈpaʊ.ə.mənt/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
The process of enabling patients to take an active role in managing their own health; supporting individuals to develop the knowledge, skills, and confidence to make informed decisions about their care.
繁體中文
賦能；促進患者自主能力
Common Collocations
patient empowerment promote empowerment empowerment strategies empower the patient empowerment through education
OET Clinical Example
"A central goal of the discharge plan was the empowerment of Mr Garcia to independently manage his chronic low back pain through a structured home exercise programme, activity pacing, and a self-monitoring symptom diary."
Related Words
empower (verb) autonomy (patient's right to self-determine) self-efficacy (belief in one's ability) patient-centred care shared decision-making
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 73)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr Tran Van Duc, a 42-year-old male, was referred to the community neurological rehabilitation service following an admission under neurology with confirmed transverse myelitis at the C8–T1 level, thought to be secondary to a recent viral illness.

His past medical history is notable for cerebrovascular disease; he sustained a minor ischaemic event eighteen months ago with full recovery. On the current admission, presenting features included bilateral upper limb weakness, impaired fine motor coordination, and reduced sensation below the level of the lesion. He also reported anosmia of approximately six weeks' duration, which predated the onset of motor symptoms and was attributed to the initial viral infection; referral to ENT has been arranged.

Psychosocially, Mr Tran expressed significant distress regarding his prognosis and demonstrated markedly low self-efficacy for independent self-care, citing fear of falls and uncertainty about his recovery trajectory. A key aim of physiotherapy is the empowerment of Mr Tran through structured goal-setting, graded functional tasks, and education regarding neuroplasticity and expected rehabilitation timelines.

Please review and advise regarding ongoing neurological rehabilitation, including gait retraining, upper limb function, and strategies to support patient self-management.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters involving neurological conditions, always specify the level of the lesion (e.g. "C8–T1"), the suspected aetiology (e.g. "secondary to viral illness"), and the patient's psychosocial status (e.g. low self-efficacy, fear of falls). This gives the receiving clinician a complete clinical picture and justifies the referral goals.
