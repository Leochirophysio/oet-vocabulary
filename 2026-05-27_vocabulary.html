Word 1 of 5
murmur
/ˈmɜː.mə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormal heart sound caused by turbulent blood flow through the cardiac valves or chambers, detected on auscultation. Classified by timing (systolic or diastolic), intensity (grades I–VI), and location.
繁體中文
心臟雜音
Common Collocations
cardiac murmur systolic murmur diastolic murmur grade III murmur heart murmur
OET Clinical Example
"On auscultation, a grade III/VI systolic murmur was detected at the left sternal border with radiation to the axilla; the patient has been referred to cardiology for echocardiographic assessment."
Related Words
bruit (vascular turbulence) thrill (palpable murmur) auscultation valvular (adj) stenosis / regurgitation

wheeze
/wiːz/ noun / verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A continuous, high-pitched whistling breath sound produced by air passing through narrowed or partially obstructed airways; most commonly heard on expiration and associated with bronchospasm, asthma, or COPD.
繁體中文
喘鳴音；哮鳴音
Common Collocations
expiratory wheeze audible wheeze bilateral wheeze polyphonic wheeze wheeze on auscultation
OET Clinical Example
"Assessment revealed a persistent bilateral expiratory wheeze throughout both lung fields, with reduced air entry at the right base; the patient's bronchodilator technique was reviewed and corrected prior to discharge."
Related Words
wheezing (noun/gerund) stridor (inspiratory, upper airway) rhonchi (low-pitched) bronchospasm adventitious breath sounds

Medical Definition

Word 4 of 5
rhonchi
/ˈrɒŋ.kaɪ/ noun (plural) 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Low-pitched, continuous adventitious breath sounds caused by air moving through secretions or a narrowed lumen in the large bronchi; typically more prominent on expiration and may partially clear with coughing. Singular: rhonchus.
繁體中文
低音性囉音；鼾音
Common Collocations
coarse rhonchi bilateral rhonchi expiratory rhonchi widespread rhonchi rhonchi on auscultation
OET Clinical Example
"Coarse bilateral rhonchi were audible throughout the lung fields on auscultation, partially clearing with coughing, indicating retained secretions in the central airways; chest physiotherapy was commenced."
Related Words
rhonchus (singular) crackles / crepitations (fine or coarse) wheeze (high-pitched) adventitious breath sounds secretions (cause)

precordial
/prɪˈkɔː.di.əl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to or situated in the precordium — the anterior region of the thorax overlying the heart. Used clinically to describe chest pain location, cardiac examination findings, and ECG lead placement.
繁體中文
心前區的；心前區
Common Collocations
precordial pain precordial chest precordial thrill precordial examination precordial leads
OET Clinical Example
"The patient described a sharp precordial pain that worsened on deep inspiration and when lying supine, improving when leaning forwards; pericarditis was suspected and urgent cardiology review was arranged."
Related Words
precordium (noun — the region) retrosternal (behind the sternum) substernal (beneath the sternum) pericardial (relating to pericardium) mediastinal
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 36)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Tran, a 72-year-old retired construction worker with a 45-year smoking history and established COPD (GOLD Stage III), was reviewed by the physiotherapy team following admission for an acute respiratory exacerbation.

On auscultation, coarse bilateral rhonchi were noted throughout the lung fields, partially clearing on coughing, indicating retained secretions in the central airways. An intermittent expiratory wheeze was also audible at the right base, consistent with bronchoconstriction. Peripheral examination revealed bilateral digital clubbing, consistent with his longstanding hypoxaemia.

Cardiac auscultation identified a grade II/VI systolic murmur at the left sternal border; the treating physician was notified and an echocardiogram was subsequently arranged. The patient additionally reported intermittent precordial discomfort during episodes of productive coughing, which the team documented and monitored throughout the admission.

Physiotherapy management focused on airway clearance techniques including active cycle of breathing, gravity-assisted positioning for postural drainage, and graduated mobilisation with supplemental oxygen to improve functional capacity and secretion clearance.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting breath sounds, always specify quality (coarse/fine), distribution (bilateral/unilateral, zone), and timing (inspiratory/expiratory). For murmurs, include grade, timing, and location. Precise descriptors demonstrate clinical credibility in OET referral letters.
