haemoptysis
/hɪˈmɒp.tɪ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The coughing up of blood or blood-stained mucus originating from the respiratory tract, particularly the lungs or bronchi; a serious sign requiring urgent clinical investigation.
繁體中文
咳血
Common Collocations
frank haemoptysis streaky haemoptysis massive haemoptysis recurrent haemoptysis haemoptysis on exertion
OET Clinical Example
"The patient was referred urgently following two episodes of frank haemoptysis over the preceding 48 hours, each producing approximately 30 mL of blood-stained sputum, and a chest CT has been arranged as a priority."
Related Words
haematemesis (vomiting blood — different source) haemorrhage (bleeding) epistaxis (nosebleed) haemoptyse (verb — rare) blood-stained sputum (clinical description)

orthopnoea
/ˌɔː.θɒpˈniːə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Breathlessness that occurs when lying flat, relieved by sitting upright or elevating the head; commonly associated with left heart failure or severe chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. Clinically quantified by the number of pillows required for comfortable sleep.
繁體中文
端坐呼吸（平臥時呼吸困難）
Common Collocations
two-pillow orthopnoea three-pillow orthopnoea severe orthopnoea orthopnoea at night reports orthopnoea
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported three-pillow orthopnoea and was unable to sleep in the supine position, confirming a significant deterioration in his cardiac function since his last review three weeks ago."
Related Words
orthopnoeic (adj) dyspnoea (breathlessness — broader term) paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea / PND platypnoea (breathless when upright — opposite) supine (lying flat)

bronchospasm
/ˈbrɒŋ.kəʊ.spæz.əm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Sudden involuntary contraction of the smooth muscle in the walls of the bronchi and bronchioles, causing airway narrowing, wheezing, and difficulty breathing; a hallmark feature of asthma and exercise-induced reactive airway disease.
繁體中文
支氣管痙攣
Common Collocations
exercise-induced bronchospasm acute bronchospasm severe bronchospasm reversible bronchospasm bronchospasm on exertion
OET Clinical Example
"The patient experienced exercise-induced bronchospasm during the functional capacity assessment, requiring administration of salbutamol via metered-dose inhaler; symptoms resolved within ten minutes and the session was not resumed."
Related Words
bronchospastic (adj) bronchoconstriction (synonym) bronchodilation (antonym) bronchodilator (treatment — e.g. salbutamol) wheezing (audible sign)

spirometry
/spaɪˈrɒm.ɪ.tri/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A pulmonary function test that measures the volume and flow rate of air that can be inhaled and exhaled; used to diagnose and monitor obstructive and restrictive respiratory conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
繁體中文
肺量計測定；肺功能檢查
Common Collocations
perform spirometry spirometry results pre-bronchodilator spirometry post-bronchodilator spirometry spirometry findings
OET Clinical Example
"Pre-operative spirometry revealed a forced expiratory volume in one second (FEV1) of 62% predicted with a FEV1/FVC ratio of 0.59, indicating moderate obstructive impairment and informing the anaesthetic team's risk assessment."
Related Words
spirometer (noun — device) spirometric (adj) FEV1 (forced expiratory volume in 1 second) FVC (forced vital capacity) peak flow (simpler bedside test)

Medical Definition
Profuse or abnormal sweating; may occur in response to systemic illness, acute cardiac events, fever, autonomic dysfunction, or physiological stress during exercise testing; an important clinical warning sign requiring immediate assessment.

Related Words
diaphoretic (adj / noun) perspiration (lay term) hyperhidrosis (chronic excessive sweating condition) pallor (often co-occurs) autonomic response
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 8)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Tran, a 72-year-old retired engineer with a background of ischaemic heart disease and moderate chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), was reviewed in the cardiopulmonary physiotherapy clinic following a recent hospital admission for acute exacerbation.

On assessment, Mr. Tran reported three-pillow orthopnoea preventing restful sleep in the supine position, and described two episodes of haemoptysis producing small quantities of blood-streaked sputum over the preceding week, for which he had been referred for urgent respiratory review.

Bedside spirometry demonstrated a post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC ratio of 0.58 with an FEV1 of 64% predicted, consistent with moderate obstructive impairment. During a six-minute walk test, the patient developed marked diaphoresis and chest tightness at 180 metres, with oxygen saturation declining to 88% on room air, necessitating early termination of the assessment. A brief episode of audible wheeze was noted, suggestive of exertional bronchospasm, which resolved following administration of salbutamol via metered-dose inhaler.

It is recommended that physiotherapy management focus on breathing retraining, positioning strategies to alleviate orthopnoea, and carefully graduated activity pacing, pending further investigation of the haemoptysis by the respiratory team.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting cardiopulmonary symptoms, always quantify where possible — report pillow number for orthopnoea, volume and frequency for haemoptysis, SpO2 values for desaturation, and distance or time for exercise tolerance. Specific data strengthens the clinical handover and demonstrates professional accuracy.
