herniation
/ˌhɜːniˈeɪʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The protrusion of an organ or tissue through an abnormal opening in its surrounding wall or cavity; most commonly used in clinical practice to describe displacement of intervertebral disc material (nucleus pulposus) into the spinal canal, where it may compress adjacent neural structures.
繁體中文
突出；疝出（椎間盤突出）
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"MRI of the lumbar spine confirmed a central disc herniation at L4/5, with moderate compression of the right L5 nerve root, accounting for the patient's reported right-sided radiculopathy and lower limb weakness."
Related Words
herniate (verb) prolapse (synonym — disc prolapse) extrusion (severe herniation beyond annulus) sequestration (free disc fragment) radiculopathy (nerve root consequence)

calcification
/ˌkæl.sɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The abnormal deposition of calcium salts in soft tissues, tendons, or bursae, resulting in hardening of the affected structure. May be associated with chronic inflammation, degenerative changes, or metabolic disturbance, and is typically identified on plain radiograph or ultrasound.
繁體中文
鈣化
Common Collocations
calcific tendinopathy rotator cuff calcification soft tissue calcification ectopic calcification calcification on imaging
OET Clinical Example
"Plain radiograph of the right shoulder demonstrated a significant area of calcification within the supraspinatus tendon, consistent with calcific tendinopathy; extracorporeal shockwave therapy has been recommended as first-line management."
Related Words
calcify (verb) calcified (adj) heterotopic ossification (bone formation in soft tissue) calcific tendinopathy decalcification (loss of calcium)

hypomobility
/ˌhaɪ.pəʊ.məˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Reduced range of movement at a joint or spinal segment, below the normal physiological range; may result from capsular tightness, periarticular fibrosis, muscular guarding, degenerative joint changes, or prolonged immobilisation.
繁體中文
關節活動度減少；低活動性
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Assessment of the thoracic spine revealed marked segmental hypomobility at T4–T6, with associated paraspinal muscle guarding; manual therapy techniques and graded mobility exercises have been incorporated into the treatment plan."
Related Words
hypermobility (antonym — excessive range) stiffness (lay term) restricted range of motion (clinical) capsular pattern (pattern of restriction) joint mobilisation (treatment)
Word 4 of 5
prehabilitation
/ˌpriː.həˌbɪl.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A structured programme of exercise, physical conditioning, and patient education undertaken prior to surgery or an anticipated period of physical decline, with the aim of improving the patient's baseline functional capacity and optimising postoperative recovery outcomes.
繁體中文
術前復健；預先復健
Common Collocations
prehabilitation programme prehabilitation prior to joint replacement cardiac prehabilitation oncological prehabilitation enrol in prehabilitation
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Thompson has been enrolled in a six-week prehabilitation programme ahead of his scheduled total knee arthroplasty, targeting quadriceps strengthening, cardiovascular conditioning, and education regarding post-surgical weight-bearing restrictions."
Related Words
rehabilitation (post-surgical counterpart) preoperative physiotherapy functional optimisation deconditioning (what prehab aims to prevent) prehab (abbreviation)
Word 5 of 5
malleolus
/məˈliː.ə.ləs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Either of the two rounded bony prominences at the distal end of the lower leg forming the ankle mortise: the medial malleolus (distal tibia) and the lateral malleolus (distal fibula). These are key anatomical landmarks in lower limb assessment and are commonly involved in ankle fractures and ligamentous injuries.
繁體中文
踝骨；踝突（內踝 / 外踝）
Common Collocations
medial malleolus lateral malleolus bimalleolar fracture tenderness over the malleolus malleolar avulsion
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented with point tenderness directly over the lateral malleolus following an inversion ankle injury sustained during sport; Ottawa Ankle Rules criteria were met and plain radiography was arranged to exclude fracture."
Related Words
malleolar (adj) bimalleolar (involving both malleoli) ankle mortise (the ankle joint space) tibia / fibula (parent bones) trimalleolar fracture (includes posterior malleolus)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 75)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Sutherland, a 44-year-old warehouse supervisor, was referred to physiotherapy following confirmation of a central disc herniation at L4/5 on MRI, with moderate compression of the right L5 nerve root. He presented with a six-week history of right-sided lumbar and lower limb pain, rated 7/10 at worst on the numerical rating scale.

On initial assessment, significant hypomobility of the lumbar spine was noted in all planes, with active flexion limited to 35° (normal approximately 80°) and extension restricted to 8° due to pain and protective muscle guarding. Neurological examination revealed diminished sensation along the right L5 dermatomal distribution and mild weakness of extensor hallucis longus (grade 4/5 on MRC scale).

Of note, Mr. Sutherland is also scheduled for a right total hip arthroplasty in eight weeks' time. He has therefore been enrolled in a concurrent prehabilitation programme targeting hip abductor and quadriceps strengthening, aerobic conditioning, and education regarding post-operative weight-bearing precautions. He sustained a minor inversion ankle injury last month and reports residual tenderness over the lateral malleolus, though Ottawa Ankle Rules were negative at the time of injury and he is currently weight-bearing without restriction.

Physiotherapy goals include progressive lumbar stabilisation, neural mobilisation for the right L5 radiculopathy, and optimisation of functional capacity prior to arthroplasty.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In referral and handover letters, specify imaging findings (e.g. "confirmed on MRI at L4/5"), objective measures (e.g. "flexion limited to 35°"), and functional impact (e.g. "restricted to 8° due to pain"). Pair clinical findings with their consequences to guide the receiving clinician efficiently.
