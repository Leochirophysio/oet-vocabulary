Medical Definition

pronation

骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A rotational movement in which the forearm turns so that the palm faces downward or backward; in the lower limb, the inward rolling of the foot and ankle during weight-bearing, resulting in a lowered medial arch.

OET Clinical Example

Related Words
supination (antonym — outward rotation) pronate (verb) pronator (noun — muscle) overpronation / hyperpronation pes planus (flat foot)

capsulitis
/ˌkæpsjʊˈlaɪtɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the fibrous capsule surrounding a joint, resulting in pain, stiffness, and restricted range of motion; adhesive capsulitis (frozen shoulder) is the most clinically common presentation, involving progressive capsular fibrosis.
繁體中文

Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Mrs. Nguyen was diagnosed with adhesive capsulitis of the right shoulder, presenting with a six-month history of progressive stiffness and night pain, with active elevation restricted to seventy-five degrees and external rotation to ten degrees."
Related Words
frozen shoulder (lay term) periarthritis (surrounding joint inflammation) capsular pattern (restricted ROM pattern) glenohumeral joint (affected joint) fibrosis (underlying process)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 23)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 58-year-old male, was referred to your outpatient physiotherapy department following right total knee arthroplasty performed six weeks ago. His post-operative recovery was complicated by a moderate intramuscular haematoma over the anterior thigh, which has largely resolved on serial review but contributed to a delayed commencement of active rehabilitation.

Pre-operatively, MRI had confirmed end-stage osteoarthritis with associated medial meniscus degeneration and significant joint space narrowing. Gait assessment at referral revealed residual antalgic gait with reduced cadence, and biomechanical review noted excessive foot pronation bilaterally, which the referring orthopaedic surgeon has requested be addressed concurrently to optimise lower limb alignment post-arthroplasty.

Of note, Mr. Park also has a background history of right shoulder adhesive capsulitis, currently in the resolving phase, which may limit his ability to use axillary crutches for extended periods during gait re-education.

Goals of physiotherapy include quadriceps and hip abductor strengthening, progressive weight-bearing mobilisation, correction of lower limb biomechanics, and patient education regarding activity modification and joint protection strategies.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters involving post-surgical patients, always note post-operative complications (e.g. haematoma), pre-operative pathology (e.g. meniscus degeneration), current functional status (e.g. antalgic gait), and any comorbidities that affect treatment (e.g. capsulitis limiting crutch use). This gives the receiving clinician a complete clinical picture.
