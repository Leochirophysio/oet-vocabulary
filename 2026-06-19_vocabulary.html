anomia
/æˈnəʊ.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A form of aphasia in which the patient has specific difficulty retrieving the names of objects, people, or places, despite intact understanding and ability to describe the item; also called anomic aphasia or word-finding difficulty.
繁體中文
命名障礙（失名症）
Common Collocations
severe anomia anomia secondary to stroke anomic aphasia word-finding difficulty persistent anomia
OET Clinical Example
"On language assessment, the patient demonstrated significant anomia, being unable to name common household objects despite intact comprehension, consistent with a left hemisphere lesion identified on recent MRI."
Related Words
aphasia (broader term) anomic aphasia (specific type) dysphasia (mild form) word-finding difficulty (lay term) anomic (adj)
Word 2 of 5
myoclonus
/maɪˈɒk.lə.nəs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A sudden, brief, involuntary muscle jerk or twitch affecting one or more muscles; may occur in isolation in healthy individuals (e.g. hypnic jerks on falling asleep) or as a sign of neurological disease, metabolic disturbance, or medication toxicity.
繁體中文
肌陣攣
Common Collocations
nocturnal myoclonus myoclonic jerks cortical myoclonus multifocal myoclonus myoclonus at rest
OET Clinical Example
"During physiotherapy assessment, episodes of myoclonus were observed in the bilateral lower limbs, particularly on initiation of movement, necessitating close monitoring and modified task progression during gait retraining."
Related Words
myoclonic (adj) clonus (rhythmic, sustained) tremor (rhythmic oscillation) fasciculation (muscle fibre twitch) asterixis (flapping tremor)
Word 3 of 5
chorea
/kəˈriː.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A hyperkinetic movement disorder characterised by random, rapid, irregular, involuntary movements that flow unpredictably from one body part to another, resembling a dance-like pattern; commonly associated with Huntington's disease, rheumatic fever (Sydenham's chorea), or medications.
繁體中文
舞蹈症
Common Collocations
Huntington's chorea choreiform movements Sydenham's chorea chorea gravidarum progressive chorea
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presents with progressive chorea predominantly affecting the bilateral upper limbs and trunk, which significantly impairs her functional independence, safety during transfers, and ability to self-care."
Related Words
choreiform (adj — chorea-like) hemiballismus (large-amplitude chorea) athetosis (slow writhing movements) hyperkinesia (excessive movement) dystonia (sustained muscle contractions)
Word 4 of 5
autonomy
/ɔːˈtɒn.ə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
A patient's right and capacity to make informed, voluntary decisions about their own healthcare and treatment, free from undue pressure or coercion; one of the four core principles of biomedical ethics (alongside beneficence, non-maleficence, and justice).
繁體中文
自主性（患者自主決策權）
Common Collocations
patient autonomy respect autonomy preserve autonomy functional autonomy promote autonomy
OET Clinical Example
"In accordance with the principle of patient autonomy, treatment goals and management options were discussed thoroughly with Mr. Patel, and his expressed preferences regarding the frequency and setting of physiotherapy sessions were incorporated into the care plan."
Related Words
autonomous (adj) informed consent (practical expression) shared decision-making paternalism (antonym — clinician overrides patient) self-determination
Word 5 of 5
psychoeducation
/ˌsaɪ.kəʊˌedʒ.ʊˈkeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A structured therapeutic approach in which clinicians educate patients and their families about a medical condition, its mechanisms, treatment options, and self-management strategies, with the aim of improving understanding, reducing fear, and empowering active participation in care; in physiotherapy this frequently includes pain neuroscience education (PNE).
繁體中文
心理教育／衛教（病患教育）
Common Collocations
pain neuroscience psychoeducation group psychoeducation structured psychoeducation psychoeducation session psychoeducation programme
OET Clinical Example
"A psychoeducation session was conducted to address the patient's catastrophic beliefs surrounding chronic low back pain, incorporating pain neuroscience education to reduce fear-avoidance and facilitate graded return to activity."
Related Words
pain neuroscience education / PNE patient education (broader term) health literacy self-management cognitive-behavioural approach
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 59)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs Nguyen, a 71-year-old woman with a confirmed diagnosis of Huntington's disease (early-to-moderate stage), was referred by her neurologist for inpatient physiotherapy following a recent unwitnessed fall at home. She was accompanied by her daughter, who acts as her primary carer.

On presentation, Mrs Nguyen demonstrated progressive chorea predominantly affecting the bilateral upper limbs, trunk, and oro-facial musculature, with associated postural instability and a wide-based, irregular gait pattern. Involuntary episodes of myoclonus were also observed at rest and on initiation of voluntary movement, further compromising her balance and functional mobility during transfers and ambulation.

Cognitive assessment revealed mild-to-moderate impairment, including significant anomia for common objects and reduced short-term recall. Communication was adapted accordingly, with simplified language, visual cues, and closed-ended questions used throughout the assessment to optimise Mrs Nguyen's participation and ensure accurate history-taking.

A structured psychoeducation session was conducted with both Mrs Nguyen and her daughter, addressing falls risk, safe transfer techniques, appropriate assistive device use, and home environment modifications. In line with the principle of patient autonomy, Mrs Nguyen's strongly expressed preference to remain at home for as long as possible was central to goal-setting discussions, and the physiotherapy management plan was tailored accordingly to optimise functional independence and carer confidence.

Ongoing physiotherapy goals include: (1) improving postural stability and reducing fall risk through targeted balance and strengthening exercises; (2) optimising upper limb function for activities of daily living despite progressive choreiform movements; and (3) continuing psychoeducation to prepare the family for anticipated functional decline and future care planning needs.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters for patients with complex neurological conditions, document specific movement disorder characteristics (distribution, triggers, severity), note communication adaptations used during assessment, and always record the patient's own goals and preferences to demonstrate respect for patient autonomy.
