paraplegia
/ˌpær.əˈpliː.dʒi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Complete or partial loss of motor and sensory function in both lower limbs, typically resulting from spinal cord injury or disease at the thoracic, lumbar, or sacral level.
繁體中文
截癱；下半身癱瘓
Common Collocations
traumatic paraplegia complete paraplegia incomplete paraplegia spastic paraplegia paraplegia secondary to injury
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chen, a 32-year-old male, sustained a T6 vertebral fracture in a motor vehicle accident, resulting in complete paraplegia and loss of all sensation below the level of the lesion."
Related Words
paraplegic (adj/noun) hemiplegia (one side) tetraplegia (all four limbs) monoplegia (one limb) spinal cord injury (SCI)
Word 2 of 5
neuralgia
/njʊˈræl.dʒə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Intense, often burning or lancinating pain that follows the course of a nerve; arises from nerve irritation or damage rather than from the tissue the nerve supplies.
繁體中文
神經痛
Common Collocations
trigeminal neuralgia postherpetic neuralgia intercostal neuralgia occipital neuralgia neuralgic pain
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reports a six-week history of lancinating neuralgia radiating along the right T4 dermatomal distribution, consistent with postherpetic neuralgia following a recent herpes zoster infection."
Related Words
neuralgic (adj) neuropathy (nerve disease) radiculopathy (nerve root) allodynia (pain from non-painful stimulus) nociceptive pain
Word 3 of 5
vestibular
/vɛˈstɪb.jʊ.lər/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Relating to the vestibule of the inner ear and its role in detecting head movement, maintaining balance, and coordinating eye movements; disorders cause dizziness, vertigo, and postural instability.
繁體中文
前庭的（與平衡系統相關）
Common Collocations
vestibular dysfunction vestibular rehabilitation vestibular neuritis central vestibular disorder vestibular assessment
OET Clinical Example
"Following a diagnosis of benign paroxysmal positional vertigo, the patient was referred for vestibular rehabilitation, including canalith repositioning manoeuvres and progressive balance retraining."
Related Words
vestibule (noun) vertigo (symptom) nystagmus (eye sign) labyrinthitis (inner ear inflammation) proprioception (related sense)
Word 4 of 5
validate
/ˈvæl.ɪ.deɪt/ verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
In clinical communication, to explicitly acknowledge and affirm a patient's feelings, pain, or concerns as understandable and legitimate, thereby building therapeutic alliance and trust.
繁體中文
確認（患者感受）；認可
Common Collocations
validate the patient's concerns validate the patient's pain validate feelings validate and acknowledge validate the patient's experience
OET Clinical Example
"The physiotherapist took time to validate Mr. Patel's concerns regarding the anticipated duration of his rehabilitation, ensuring he felt heard before providing education on expected recovery milestones."
Related Words
validation (noun) invalidate (antonym) acknowledge empathise rapport
Word 5 of 5
escalate
/ˈɛs.kə.leɪt/ verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
To refer a patient to a higher level of care, or to alert a senior clinician or team, due to deterioration in the patient's condition or a clinical concern that exceeds the current practitioner's scope of practice.
繁體中文
升級醫療處理；轉介至更高層級照護
Common Collocations
escalate care escalate concerns escalate to the medical team escalate management escalate if symptoms worsen
OET Clinical Example
"The physiotherapist ceased the treatment session and advised the nursing staff to escalate the patient's care to the on-call medical officer, as oxygen saturation had dropped to 88% on room air during mobilisation."
Related Words
escalation (noun) de-escalate (antonym) refer (clinical) triage handover
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 45)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Nguyen, a 28-year-old male, was admitted to the spinal rehabilitation unit following a T10 burst fracture sustained during a construction site accident two weeks ago, resulting in complete paraplegia with no motor or sensory function below the level of injury.

During today's physiotherapy session, Mr. Nguyen reported intermittent burning neuralgia radiating across both thighs, consistent with neuropathic pain arising at the level of the lesion. He expressed significant distress regarding his prognosis and was visibly anxious. The treating physiotherapist took time to validate his concerns, acknowledging the profound impact of his injury before proceeding with goal setting.

Concurrent to his spinal management, Mr. Nguyen also reported new-onset dizziness and imbalance during transfers, prompting a vestibular screening assessment. Results were inconclusive in the context of his injury, and the treating physiotherapist elected to escalate these findings to the consulting neurologist for further evaluation.

He is referred to your outpatient spinal rehabilitation service for wheelchair mobility training, upper limb strengthening, and ongoing neuralgic pain management as an outpatient.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters for patients with neurological conditions, clearly state the level and completeness of injury (e.g. "complete paraplegia at T10"), any complicating symptoms (e.g. neuropathic pain, vestibular changes), and the reason for escalation where relevant. Demonstrating patient-centred communication (e.g. validating concerns) also reflects OET Speaking criteria.
