ankylosis
/ˌæŋ.kɪˈləʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal stiffening or fusion of a joint, resulting in complete or partial loss of movement; may be bony (osseous) or fibrous, and caused by disease, injury, or surgical intervention.
繁體中文
關節強直
Common Collocations
bony ankylosis fibrous ankylosis complete ankylosis post-traumatic ankylosis spinal ankylosis
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Kumar was referred to physiotherapy following radiological confirmation of bony ankylosis of the left ankle secondary to longstanding rheumatoid arthritis, presenting with complete loss of joint mobility and compensatory gait changes."
Related Words
ankylotic (adj) arthrodesis (surgical fusion) ankylosing spondylitis joint fusion contracture (soft-tissue restriction)

Medical Definition
Inflammation of the tendons and periosteum at the epicondyle of the humerus; lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow) involves the outer elbow, whilst medial epicondylitis (golfer's elbow) involves the inner elbow.
繁體中文
上髁炎
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Mrs. Tan was referred with a nine-month history of lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow, exacerbated by repetitive keyboard use at work and unresponsive to a six-week course of NSAIDs and activity modification."

lymphoedema
/ˌlɪm.fəˈdiː.mə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Chronic swelling of a limb or body region caused by impaired lymphatic drainage, leading to accumulation of protein-rich fluid in the interstitial tissues; classified as primary (congenital) or secondary (acquired, e.g. post-surgical or post-radiation).
繁體中文
淋巴水腫
Common Collocations
primary lymphoedema secondary lymphoedema lower limb lymphoedema lymphoedema management breast-cancer-related lymphoedema
OET Clinical Example
"Ms. Nguyen presents with stage II secondary lymphoedema of the left upper limb following axillary lymph node dissection for breast cancer, and has been referred for complete decongestive therapy including manual lymphatic drainage and compression bandaging."
Related Words
oedema (general swelling) lymphoedematous (adj) lipoedema (fat-related swelling) lymphangitis (lymph vessel inflammation) manual lymphatic drainage

induration

Signs & Symptoms 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Abnormal hardening or increased firmness of body tissue, typically detected on palpation; caused by inflammation, fibrosis, chronic pressure, or cellular infiltration.
繁體中文
組織硬化 / 硬結
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example

Related Words
indurated (adj) fibrosis (fibrous tissue formation) sclerosis (hardening of tissue) hardening (lay term) softening (antonym)

骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of skeletal muscle tissue, which may be acute or chronic; arising from infection, autoimmune disease, trauma, or medication; presenting with pain, swelling, weakness, and tenderness on palpation.
繁體中文

Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"The patient was referred with progressive proximal muscle weakness and tenderness over six weeks; preliminary investigations have raised the possibility of autoimmune myositis, and physiotherapy assessment of functional capacity and muscle strength is requested pending specialist review."
Related Words
myopathy (muscle disease, broader) polymyositis (systemic autoimmune) dermatomyositis (with skin involvement) myositis ossificans (bone in muscle) rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 40)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Wong, a 45-year-old office administrator, was referred to your physiotherapy department with a primary diagnosis of chronic lateral epicondylitis of the right elbow, unresponsive to conservative management over the past eight months.

On clinical assessment, the physiotherapist noted palpable induration over the lateral epicondyle region, consistent with localised peritendinous fibrosis secondary to repetitive strain. Mr. Wong reported significant pain on resisted wrist extension and gripping, graded 7/10 on the numeric pain rating scale, with marked functional limitation affecting his ability to perform sustained keyboard tasks and carry objects.

A secondary concern was raised regarding mild distal upper limb lymphoedema, noted following a prior surgical procedure to the right axilla; this has been managed with compression sleeves, though the swelling remains persistent.

Please assess and commence an individualised rehabilitation programme addressing pain management, progressive loading of the wrist extensors, and ongoing monitoring of the lymphoedema in collaboration with the treating lymphoedema nurse.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting findings in referral letters, use precise clinical descriptors: specify location (e.g. "lateral epicondyle"), quality (e.g. "palpable induration"), and functional impact (e.g. "limiting sustained keyboard tasks"). This demonstrates professional clinical language and aids the receiving therapist.
