periosteum
/ˌper.iˈɒs.ti.əm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The dense fibrous membrane that covers the outer surface of all bones except at joint surfaces; it contains blood vessels and nerves essential for bone nourishment, pain sensation, and fracture repair.

骨膜

callus
/ˈkæl.əs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A mass of new bony and fibrocartilaginous tissue that forms around the ends of a fractured bone as part of the healing process; also refers to a thickened area of skin caused by repeated friction or pressure.

OET Clinical Example

Related Words
callous (adj — hardened) callosity (skin thickening) fracture remodelling ossification non-union (failed healing)
Word 3 of 5
avascular
/eɪˈvæs.kjʊ.lə/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Lacking blood vessels or having an inadequate blood supply; tissues that are avascular heal poorly and are susceptible to necrosis, as seen in cartilage, the meniscus, and certain regions of bone following vascular disruption.
繁體中文
無血管的；缺乏血液供應的
Common Collocations
avascular necrosis avascular zone avascular cartilage avascular femoral head avascular tissue
OET Clinical Example
"MRI of the left hip confirmed avascular necrosis of the femoral head, and the patient was referred urgently to the orthopaedic team for consideration of core decompression prior to further collapse."
Related Words
avascularity (noun) vascular (antonym) ischaemia (reduced blood flow) necrosis (tissue death) osteonecrosis (bone death)

diathermy
/ˈdaɪ.ə.θɜː.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A therapeutic modality that uses high-frequency electromagnetic waves or electric currents to generate heat within deep body tissues; used in physiotherapy to relieve pain, reduce muscle spasm, improve circulation, and promote tissue healing.
繁體中文
透熱療法
Common Collocations
shortwave diathermy pulsed diathermy microwave diathermy therapeutic diathermy surgical diathermy
OET Clinical Example
"A course of pulsed shortwave diathermy was initiated targeting the lumbar paraspinal musculature, aiming to reduce deep tissue muscle spasm and improve extensibility prior to manual therapy and exercise rehabilitation."
Related Words
thermotherapy (heat treatment) electrotherapy ultrasound therapy TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation) hyperthermia
Word 5 of 5
fasciotomy
/ˌfæʃ.iˈɒt.ə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A surgical procedure in which the fascia — the fibrous connective tissue sheath surrounding muscles — is incised to relieve dangerously elevated pressure within a muscle compartment; the primary treatment for acute compartment syndrome.
繁體中文
筋膜切開術
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"The patient was referred to the physiotherapy department following emergency fasciotomy to the right lower leg for acute compartment syndrome, requiring wound oedema management, ankle range-of-motion restoration, and a graded progressive weight-bearing programme."
Related Words
fascia (noun) compartment syndrome decompression fascial release fasciotomy scar
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 51)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Taufa, a 34-year-old male rugby player, was admitted to the orthopaedic ward following a crush injury to the right lower leg sustained during a tackle. Intracompartmental pressure measurements confirmed acute compartment syndrome, and he underwent emergency fasciotomy to the anterior and deep posterior compartments of the right leg within six hours of injury.

Post-operatively, imaging of the right tibia revealed a non-displaced stress fracture at the mid-diaphysis with early callus formation visible on the lateral view. The orthopaedic surgeon noted the importance of protecting the periosteum during wound closure to preserve the vascularity essential for bone healing.

Of note, a prior MRI of the right hip — obtained three months earlier for unrelated pain — had demonstrated early avascular necrosis of the femoral head, which will require ongoing monitoring by the orthopaedic team.

Mr. Taufa has been referred to physiotherapy for post-fasciotomy rehabilitation. Initial goals include oedema reduction, restoration of ankle dorsiflexion range, and a progressive weight-bearing programme. A course of pulsed diathermy to the calf musculature has been requested by the surgical team to address residual muscle spasm and promote deep tissue healing prior to active strengthening.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting post-surgical referrals, specify the procedure performed (e.g. "emergency fasciotomy"), the indication (e.g. "acute compartment syndrome"), and the current rehabilitation goals. Linking each goal to a measurable functional outcome (e.g. "restoration of ankle dorsiflexion") demonstrates clinical reasoning in your OET writing response.
