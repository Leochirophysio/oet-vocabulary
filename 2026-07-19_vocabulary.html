Word 1 of 5
remodelling
/ˌriːˈmɒd.əl.ɪŋ/ noun / verb (gerund) 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The continuous physiological process by which bone or connective tissue is broken down (resorption) and rebuilt (formation) in response to mechanical loading, injury, or metabolic demand; a key phase in fracture healing and surgical recovery.
繁體中文
重塑（骨骼／組織重塑）
Common Collocations
bone remodelling tissue remodelling adaptive remodelling cortical remodelling remodelling phase
OET Clinical Example
"Serial radiographs at eight weeks post-fixation demonstrate progressive cortical remodelling at the fracture site, consistent with an appropriate healing response to axial loading."
Related Words
remodel (verb) resorption (breakdown phase) ossification (bone formation) Wolff's Law (mechanical adaptation) osteogenesis (bone growth)
Word 2 of 5
allograft
/ˈæl.ə.ɡrɑːft/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Tissue or bone transplanted from a genetically non-identical donor of the same species; commonly used in orthopaedic surgery for ligament reconstruction (e.g. ACL), bone defect filling, or cartilage repair when autogenous tissue is unavailable or insufficient.
繁體中文
同種異體移植物；異體移植
Common Collocations
bone allograft tendon allograft allograft reconstruction allograft versus autograft structural allograft
OET Clinical Example
"The patient underwent anterior cruciate ligament reconstruction using a tibialis anterior tendon allograft, and has been referred for a structured rehabilitation programme commencing at six weeks post-operatively."
Related Words
autograft (from same patient) xenograft (cross-species graft) graft (general term) allogeneic (adj — same species) reconstruction (noun)
Word 3 of 5
ganglion
/ˈɡæŋ.ɡli.ən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A benign, fluid-filled cystic swelling arising from a tendon sheath or joint capsule, most commonly presenting on the dorsal aspect of the wrist; may vary in size, cause localised discomfort or pressure symptoms, and occasionally resolve spontaneously.
繁體中文
腱鞘囊腫；神經節囊腫
Common Collocations
dorsal ganglion wrist ganglion ganglion cyst recurrent ganglion excision of ganglion
OET Clinical Example
"A firm, fluctuant ganglion cyst was identified on the dorsal aspect of the right wrist, measuring approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, with no associated neurovascular compromise or restriction of active range of motion."
Related Words
ganglia (plural) cyst (general term) fluctuant (adj — fluid-filled) aspiration (drainage treatment) excision (surgical removal)
Word 4 of 5
meniscectomy
/ˌmen.ɪˈsek.tə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Surgical removal of part (partial meniscectomy) or all (total meniscectomy) of the meniscus of the knee, typically performed arthroscopically to address meniscal tears that are not amenable to primary repair, aiming to relieve mechanical pain and locking.
繁體中文
半月板切除術
Common Collocations
partial meniscectomy arthroscopic meniscectomy total meniscectomy post-meniscectomy rehabilitation meniscectomy versus meniscal repair
OET Clinical Example
"Following arthroscopic partial meniscectomy of the medial compartment, the patient has been referred for physiotherapy to address residual quadriceps inhibition and restore full weight-bearing tolerance over the next four to six weeks."
Related Words
meniscus (noun — the structure) meniscal repair (alternative surgery) arthroscopy (surgical approach) chondroprotection (cartilage preservation) medial / lateral compartment
Word 5 of 5
chondroplasty
/ˈkɒn.drə.plæs.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
A surgical procedure to repair or smooth damaged articular cartilage, performed arthroscopically by shaving or contouring fibrillated or irregular cartilage surfaces within a joint; aims to reduce mechanical symptoms and slow articular deterioration.
繁體中文
軟骨整形術；軟骨修復術
Common Collocations
arthroscopic chondroplasty chondroplasty of the knee chondroplasty versus microfracture cartilage chondroplasty post-chondroplasty rehabilitation
OET Clinical Example
"The patient underwent arthroscopic chondroplasty to address grade III chondral lesions on the medial femoral condyle, and physiotherapy is now recommended to restore range of motion and facilitate graded return to weight-bearing activity."
Related Words
chondromalacia (cartilage softening) articular cartilage (target tissue) microfracture (deeper cartilage repair) fibrillation (cartilage fraying) arthroplasty (joint replacement)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 72)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Carlos Reyes, a 32-year-old tradesman, is referred for postoperative physiotherapy following arthroscopic partial meniscectomy of the right knee, performed three weeks ago for a complex medial meniscal tear. Intra-operatively, the surgeon also performed localised chondroplasty to grade II articular surface changes on the medial femoral condyle, and excised a small intra-articular ganglion identified within the posterior joint recess.

The patient is currently partially weight-bearing with a single forearm crutch and reports ongoing medial joint line tenderness with mild residual effusion. Active knee flexion is limited to 95 degrees. Ongoing bone remodelling at the chondral repair site is expected over the coming three to six months; the surgeon has requested that physiotherapy prioritise quadriceps activation and range-of-motion restoration prior to commencing progressive loading exercises.

Goals of physiotherapy include full restoration of active range of motion, progressive strengthening of the lower limb musculature, and a graduated return to full weight-bearing and occupational duties. Please review and advise regarding suitability for hydrotherapy-based rehabilitation at this stage.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing post-operative referral letters, specify the exact procedure performed (e.g. "arthroscopic partial meniscectomy"), the current functional status (e.g. "partially weight-bearing with single crutch"), and specific physiotherapy goals. Avoid vague phrases such as "needs rehab" or "surgery went well" — precision signals clinical competence in OET Writing tasks.
