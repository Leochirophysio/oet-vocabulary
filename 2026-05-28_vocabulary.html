iontophoresis
/aɪˌɒn.tə.fəˈriː.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A therapeutic technique that uses a low-level direct electrical current to drive ionised medication transcutaneously into underlying soft tissue; commonly employed for localised anti-inflammatory drug delivery without systemic side effects.

Related Words

nociceptive
/ˌnəʊ.sɪˈsep.tɪv/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Relating to or describing pain that arises from actual or potential tissue damage, detected by specialised peripheral sensory receptors called nociceptors; characterised by a clear mechanical pattern and localisation, and distinct from neuropathic pain.
繁體中文
傷害感受性的；傷害性的
Common Collocations
nociceptive pain nociceptive stimulus nociceptive pathway nociceptive input nociceptive processing
OET Clinical Example
"The patient's pain profile is consistent with nociceptive pain, characterised by a clear mechanical pattern, localisation to the lumbar region, and relief with positional change and simple analgesia."
Related Words
nociception (noun) nociceptor (receptor) neuropathic (different pain type) central sensitisation pain classification

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 37)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs. Chan, a 34-year-old office worker, was referred to physiotherapy by her general practitioner with a three-month history of bilateral patellofemoral pain syndrome and concurrent work-related cervical strain.

Her knee pain is consistent with nociceptive pain, aggravated by prolonged sitting, stair use, and kneeling, with significant relief noted following activity modification and simple analgesia. Examination of the right ankle, injured in a sporting incident six months prior, demonstrated residual ligamentous laxity with a positive anterior drawer test, suggesting incomplete rehabilitation of the lateral ligament complex.

Occupational history revealed that Mrs. Chan spends eight to ten hours per day at a desktop workstation with a suboptimal setup. An ergonomic workstation assessment was conducted during the initial consultation, identifying a monitor positioned below eye level, inadequate lumbar support, and no structured rest breaks. Recommended modifications have been provided in writing.

Physiotherapy management will include quadriceps strengthening targeting patellofemoral mechanics, progressive ankle proprioceptive exercises to address the ligamentous insufficiency, and implementation of the ergonomic recommendations. The patient has been educated on activity pacing and self-monitoring of symptom response.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting pain type, specify the pattern (mechanical vs. non-mechanical), aggravating and relieving factors, and functional impact. For nociceptive pain, noting what relieves it (rest, position, analgesia) is as important as what provokes it — this guides treatment planning and demonstrates clinical reasoning in your referral letter.
