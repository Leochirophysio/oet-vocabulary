haemothorax
/ˌhiː.məʊˈθɔː.ræks/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The accumulation of blood in the pleural cavity — the space between the lung and the chest wall — usually resulting from trauma, surgery, or pathological processes such as malignancy or pulmonary infarction.
繁體中文
血胸
Common Collocations
traumatic haemothorax massive haemothorax haemothorax drainage post-operative haemothorax haemopneumothorax
OET Clinical Example
"Following blunt chest trauma sustained in a motor vehicle accident, chest radiograph confirmed a right-sided haemothorax; intercostal drain insertion was performed and physiotherapy referral initiated for subsequent respiratory rehabilitation."
Related Words
pneumothorax (air in pleural space) haemopneumothorax (blood + air) pleural effusion (fluid — not blood) thoracocentesis (drainage procedure) intercostal drain
Word 2 of 5
ischaemia
/ɪˈskiː.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An inadequate supply of oxygenated blood to a body part or organ due to obstruction or constriction of blood vessels, resulting in tissue hypoxia and potential infarction if prolonged.
繁體中文
缺血；局部貧血
Common Collocations
myocardial ischaemia peripheral ischaemia cerebral ischaemia limb ischaemia ischaemic changes
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented with signs consistent with critical limb ischaemia, including absent pedal pulses, pallor, and rest pain in the left foot; urgent vascular review was arranged prior to commencing any physiotherapy intervention."
Related Words
ischaemic (adj) ischemia (American spelling) infarction (tissue death from ischaemia) hypoperfusion (reduced blood flow) reperfusion (restoration of blood flow)
Word 3 of 5
arrhythmia
/əˈrɪð.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Any abnormality in the rate, regularity, or origin of the heart's electrical impulses, encompassing both tachyarrhythmias (excessively fast) and bradyarrhythmias (excessively slow), as well as irregular rhythms such as atrial fibrillation.
繁體中文
心律不整；心律失常
Common Collocations
cardiac arrhythmia ventricular arrhythmia exercise-induced arrhythmia arrhythmia management arrhythmia monitoring
OET Clinical Example
"Exercise testing was deferred pending cardiology clearance, as the patient had a documented history of exercise-induced arrhythmia and had experienced two syncopal episodes during moderate physical exertion in the preceding month."
Related Words
dysrhythmia (synonym) arrhythmic (adj) tachyarrhythmia (fast) bradyarrhythmia (slow) fibrillation (chaotic rhythm) palpitation (subjective symptom)

malaise
/məˈleɪz/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A non-specific feeling of general discomfort, unease, or illness — often described as "feeling unwell" — that is difficult to pinpoint and frequently accompanies systemic infections, chronic disease, or post-viral conditions.
繁體中文
倦怠感；全身不適
Common Collocations
general malaise profound malaise post-viral malaise persistent malaise malaise and fatigue
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported persistent malaise and profound fatigue over the preceding four weeks following a confirmed viral illness, representing a significant decline from her prior level of independent function."
Related Words
fatigue (tiredness) lethargy (sluggishness/drowsiness) asthenia (weakness) constitutional symptoms (broader term) post-exertional malaise — PEM (ME/CFS)

pruritus
/pruːˈraɪ.təs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The medical term for itching — an unpleasant skin sensation that provokes an urge to scratch. May be localised or generalised, and can indicate dermatological, systemic (e.g. hepatic, renal), or neuropathic causes.
繁體中文
搔癢症；瘙癢
Common Collocations
generalised pruritus nocturnal pruritus intractable pruritus pruritus of the skin pruritus secondary to
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented with generalised pruritus that was most severe at night, resulting in disrupted sleep and secondary skin excoriation over the forearms and lower legs; dermatology referral was initiated to exclude a systemic cause."
Related Words
pruritic (adj — e.g. pruritic rash) itch / itching (lay term) antipruritic (treatment) neuropathic pruritus (nerve-related) excoriation (skin damage from scratching)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 22)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Tane, a 57-year-old male with a background of peripheral vascular disease and paroxysmal atrial fibrillation, was referred to the cardiopulmonary physiotherapy team for rehabilitation following a six-day inpatient admission.

On admission, he had presented with signs of critical left lower limb ischaemia — absent dorsalis pedis pulse, pallor, and rest pain — which resolved following percutaneous angioplasty. His hospital course was further complicated by a documented episode of ventricular arrhythmia on day three, managed medically with antiarrhythmic therapy; telemetry monitoring had since been stable.

In addition, Mr. Tane reported persistent malaise and marked fatigue throughout his admission, which the team attributed to deconditioning compounded by prolonged bed rest. He also described generalised pruritus that had begun three days post-procedure, thought to be a reaction to his new anticoagulant medication; dermatology review was pending.

Physiotherapy goals include progressive ambulation training with cardiac monitoring, patient education regarding activity pacing given his cardiac history, and close observation for any recurrence of limb ischaemia during exercise. Liaison with the cardiology team is recommended prior to commencing any aerobic conditioning.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting a complex cardiac patient, specify the type of arrhythmia (e.g. "ventricular arrhythmia"), the distribution of ischaemia (e.g. "left lower limb"), and the management already received. Always note which investigations are pending and flag any precautions for the receiving team before intervention commences.
