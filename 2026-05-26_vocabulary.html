Medical Definition

OET Clinical Example

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 35)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Chen, a 36-year-old office administrator, was referred to the physiotherapy department by his general practitioner with an eight-week history of right-sided sciatica secondary to confirmed discogenic pathology at L4/5.

MRI of the lumbar spine demonstrated a right posterolateral disc herniation at L4/5 with moderate nerve root compression. The patient reported sharp, radiating pain from the right buttock to the lateral calf, rated 7/10 at its worst, aggravated by prolonged sitting and relieved partially by walking. He also noted intermittent pins and needles in the right foot.

In addition to his acute presentation, Mr. Chen carries a background diagnosis of fibromyalgia syndrome, which has contributed to a heightened pain experience and significant sleep disturbance. Neurological screening revealed reduced right ankle plantar flexion strength (4/5 MRC) and a diminished right Achilles reflex, consistent with S1 nerve root involvement.

Physiotherapy management will focus on neural mobilisation, lumbar stabilisation, postural education, and a graded activity programme, with close monitoring of neurological signs. The referring physician has requested physiotherapy review within two weeks to reassess radicular symptoms and functional progress.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When describing spinal conditions, always specify the level (e.g. "L4/5"), the side (e.g. "right-sided"), and the neurological findings (e.g. reflex, power grade, dermatomal pattern). This gives the receiving clinician a clear clinical picture without requiring them to re-examine the patient.
