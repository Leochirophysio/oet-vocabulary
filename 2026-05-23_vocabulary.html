jaundice
/ˈdʒɔːn.dɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A yellow discolouration of the skin, sclerae, and mucous membranes caused by elevated bilirubin levels in the blood (hyperbilirubinaemia). Commonly seen in neonates (physiological jaundice) and in hepatic or biliary disease.
繁體中文
黃疸
Common Collocations
neonatal jaundice physiological jaundice obstructive jaundice haemolytic jaundice jaundiced sclera
OET Clinical Example
"The neonate presented with visible jaundice on day 3 of life, with a total serum bilirubin of 248 µmol/L; phototherapy was commenced and the infant was reviewed by the neonatal team."
Related Words
icterus (clinical synonym) jaundiced (adj) hyperbilirubinaemia scleral icterus phototherapy (treatment)
Word 2 of 5
retraction
/rɪˈtræk.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An inward drawing of the skin between or below the ribs, at the sternal notch, or in the supraclavicular fossae during inspiration, indicating markedly increased respiratory effort and use of accessory muscles. A key sign of respiratory distress, particularly in neonates and young children.
繁體中文
胸壁凹陷（呼吸困難徵象）
Common Collocations
subcostal retraction intercostal retraction sternal retraction supraclavicular retraction marked retraction
OET Clinical Example
"The infant presented with marked subcostal and intercostal retractions, nasal flaring, and a respiratory rate of 72 breaths per minute, consistent with severe respiratory distress requiring urgent escalation."
Related Words
recession (British clinical term) indrawing (lay term) accessory muscle use nasal flaring respiratory distress
Word 3 of 5
meconium
/mɪˈkəʊ.ni.əm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The first faecal material passed by a newborn infant, composed of materials ingested during foetal development (e.g. amniotic fluid, intestinal secretions, lanugo). Meconium staining of the amniotic fluid indicates foetal distress and is associated with meconium aspiration syndrome.
繁體中文
胎便；胎糞
Common Collocations
meconium staining meconium-stained liquor thick meconium meconium aspiration syndrome passage of meconium
OET Clinical Example
"Thick meconium-stained amniotic fluid was noted at rupture of membranes, prompting immediate neonatal resuscitation team attendance at delivery and subsequent referral to the neonatal unit for monitoring."
Related Words
amniotic fluid foetal distress neonatal resuscitation meconium ileus (neonatal bowel obstruction) lanugo
Word 4 of 5
tinnitus
/tɪˈnaɪ.təs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A perception of sound — commonly ringing, buzzing, hissing, or roaring — in one or both ears in the absence of an external acoustic stimulus. May be subjective (heard only by the patient) or objective (audible to the examiner) and is often associated with hearing loss or vestibular pathology.
繁體中文
耳鳴
Common Collocations
persistent tinnitus bilateral tinnitus high-pitched tinnitus tinnitus associated with hearing loss subjective tinnitus
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported persistent high-pitched tinnitus in the right ear, worse in quiet environments, which was significantly disrupting sleep and concentration; she was referred to audiology for formal assessment."
Related Words
vertigo (vestibular symptom) hearing loss / sensorineural deafness ototoxicity (drug-induced) Menière's disease hyperacusis (sound sensitivity)
Word 5 of 5
rickets
/ˈrɪk.ɪts/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A disease of infants and young children characterised by impaired mineralisation of growing bone, most commonly due to vitamin D, calcium, or phosphate deficiency. Clinical features include bowing of the weight-bearing limbs, widening and cupping of the growth plates, rachitic rosary, and delayed fontanelle closure.
繁體中文
佝僂病；軟骨病
Common Collocations
nutritional rickets vitamin D deficiency rickets rachitic rosary bowing deformity renal rickets
OET Clinical Example
"The 14-month-old male was referred to the paediatric orthopaedic team with radiological evidence of nutritional rickets, demonstrating cupped and frayed metaphyses at the wrists and progressive bowing of the lower limbs."
Related Words
osteomalacia (adult equivalent) rachitic (adj) vitamin D deficiency hypocalcaemia ossification (bone mineralisation)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 32)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Master Kai Tane, a six-week-old male infant, was born at 38+2 weeks gestation via emergency lower segment caesarean section following identification of thick meconium-stained amniotic fluid and foetal heart rate decelerations. Neonatal resuscitation was required at delivery; the infant was intubated and transferred to the neonatal intensive care unit.

On day 3 of life, the infant developed progressive jaundice with a total serum bilirubin peaking at 263 µmol/L, requiring 36 hours of double phototherapy. Bilirubin levels subsequently normalised and phototherapy was discontinued.

On day 5, respiratory assessment revealed mild subcostal retractions at rest, a persistent oxygen requirement of 0.3 L/min via nasal cannulae, and a respiratory rate of 54 breaths per minute. The clinical findings were consistent with evolving chronic lung disease of prematurity; a physiotherapy referral was made for airway clearance techniques and supportive positioning.

He is now being reviewed at the six-week developmental clinic. Parents report normal feeding and developmental progress. No concerns regarding skeletal deformity or features consistent with rickets were identified on examination.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In neonatal referral letters, always document gestational age, birth mode, and the immediate neonatal period clearly (e.g. "required resuscitation at delivery"). When describing respiratory signs, specify the location of retractions (subcostal, intercostal), the oxygen requirement, and the respiratory rate — these details demonstrate clinical precision expected in OET Writing Band B+.
