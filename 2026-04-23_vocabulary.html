arthralgia
/ɑːˈθræl.dʒə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Pain in one or more joints, without visible signs of inflammation; a symptom rather than a diagnosis in its own right.
繁體中文
關節痛
Common Collocations
bilateral arthralgia migratory arthralgia arthralgia of the knee inflammatory arthralgia arthralgia on weight-bearing
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reports bilateral arthralgia of the knee joints, aggravated by prolonged standing and stair climbing, with a pain score of 6/10 on a numerical rating scale."
Related Words
arthritis (with inflammation) myalgia (muscle pain) arthralgic (adj) polyarthralgia (multiple joints) joint pain (lay term)
Word 2 of 5
tendinopathy
/ˌten.dɪˈnɒp.ə.θi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A clinical syndrome of tendon pain, swelling, and impaired function, resulting from overuse or degenerative change; encompasses both tendinitis and tendinosis.
繁體中文
肌腱病變
Common Collocations
Achilles tendinopathy patellar tendinopathy rotator cuff tendinopathy chronic tendinopathy insertional tendinopathy
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Hassan presents with a six-week history of right Achilles tendinopathy, with localised tenderness 2–3 cm proximal to the calcaneal insertion and pain on single-leg heel raise."
Related Words
tendinitis (acute inflammation) tendinosis (degenerative) tendon (anatomy) tenosynovitis (tendon sheath) overuse injury
Word 3 of 5
bursitis
/bɜːˈsaɪ.tɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of a bursa — a small fluid-filled sac that reduces friction between bones, tendons, and muscles near joints; commonly affects the shoulder, elbow, hip, and knee.
繁體中文
滑囊炎
Common Collocations
subacromial bursitis trochanteric bursitis olecranon bursitis septic bursitis chronic bursitis
OET Clinical Example
"Imaging confirmed subacromial bursitis of the right shoulder, consistent with the patient's reported pain on shoulder abduction beyond 60 degrees and tenderness to palpation over the greater tuberosity."
Related Words
bursa (anatomy — fluid sac) bursae (plural) impingement syndrome synovitis (joint lining inflammation) tendinopathy (tendon disorder)

effusion
/ɪˈfjuː.ʒən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal accumulation of fluid within a body cavity or joint space; in musculoskeletal practice, most commonly refers to a joint effusion indicating intra-articular pathology.
繁體中文
積液；關節積液
Common Collocations
joint effusion knee effusion moderate effusion pleural effusion haemarthrosis (blood effusion)
OET Clinical Example
"Assessment of the right knee revealed a moderate effusion with a positive ballottement test, and range of motion was limited to 0–90 degrees of flexion secondary to pain and swelling."
Related Words
haemarthrosis (blood in joint) synovial fluid aspiration (draining the fluid) oedema (tissue swelling) ballottement test (clinical sign)
Word 5 of 5
claudication
/ˌklɔː.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Cramp-like pain or limping caused by inadequate blood supply to the muscles, typically occurring in the legs during exercise and relieved by rest; may also refer to neurogenic claudication from spinal stenosis.
繁體中文
跛行；間歇性跛行
Common Collocations
intermittent claudication neurogenic claudication vascular claudication claudication distance claudication on walking
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reports intermittent claudication of the left calf, with onset after walking approximately 50 metres on flat ground; symptoms resolve within two minutes of rest."
Related Words
claudicant (adj/noun — one who claudicates) peripheral arterial disease (PAD) spinal stenosis (neurogenic cause) ischaemia (reduced blood supply) claudication distance (how far before pain)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 5)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 62-year-old male retired postal worker, was referred to the physiotherapy outpatient clinic by his GP for assessment and management of bilateral lower limb symptoms.

He presents with a two-month history of bilateral arthralgia of the knee joints, rated 5/10 at rest and 8/10 on weight-bearing activities. Ultrasound examination demonstrated a moderate knee effusion on the right, with evidence of early degenerative change bilaterally.

In addition, the patient reports intermittent claudication of the left calf with a claudication distance of approximately 80 metres; this has been referred to vascular surgery for further investigation. He also has a background of right shoulder subacromial bursitis, currently managed conservatively.

Physiotherapy aims to optimise lower limb function, provide hydrotherapy and land-based strengthening within pain-free range, and educate the patient on joint protection strategies and activity modification.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting musculoskeletal symptoms, always include location (bilateral knee), severity (5/10 at rest), aggravating factors (weight-bearing), and relevant investigation findings (ultrasound). This mirrors real referral letter structure and scores well on OET Writing criteria.
