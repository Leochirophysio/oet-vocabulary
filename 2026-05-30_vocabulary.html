hyperbilirubinaemia
/ˌhaɪ.pə.ˌbɪl.ɪ.ˌruː.bɪˈniː.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormally elevated level of bilirubin in the bloodstream, most commonly presenting in neonates as visible jaundice; may be physiological (resolving within days) or pathological, requiring phototherapy or exchange transfusion.
繁體中文
高膽紅素血症
Common Collocations
neonatal hyperbilirubinaemia physiological hyperbilirubinaemia pathological hyperbilirubinaemia severe hyperbilirubinaemia treat hyperbilirubinaemia
OET Clinical Example
"Baby Mitchell, born at 37+2 weeks gestation, was commenced on phototherapy on day 2 of life for neonatal hyperbilirubinaemia; physiotherapy input was requested to monitor positional tolerance and support developmental handling."
Related Words
jaundice (clinical sign) bilirubin (the substance) hyperbilirubinaemic (adj) kernicterus (severe complication) phototherapy (treatment)

diplegia
/daɪˈpliː.dʒi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological 小兒 Paediatric
Medical Definition
Paralysis or significant motor weakness affecting corresponding parts on both sides of the body; in cerebral palsy, spastic diplegia most commonly involves the lower limbs with relative sparing of the upper limbs.
繁體中文
雙側癱瘓；雙癱
Common Collocations
spastic diplegia lower limb diplegia diplegia secondary to bilateral diplegia manage diplegia
OET Clinical Example
"Liam, aged 4, has been diagnosed with spastic diplegia secondary to periventricular leukomalacia and is receiving twice-weekly physiotherapy to improve his gait pattern and functional mobility on level surfaces."
Related Words
hemiplegia (one side) tetraplegia (all four limbs) monoplegia (one limb) diplegic (adj) cerebral palsy (common cause)
Word 3 of 5
petechiae
/pɪˈtiː.ki.iː/ noun (plural) 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Small (1–2 mm), flat, pinpoint haemorrhages on the skin or mucous membranes that do not blanch on pressure; a clinically important sign that may indicate thrombocytopaenia, vasculitis, or serious infection such as meningococcaemia.
繁體中文
瘀點；點狀出血
Common Collocations
non-blanching petechiae petechiae on the trunk petechiae and purpura note petechiae widespread petechiae
OET Clinical Example
"On assessment, the child presented with high-grade pyrexia and widespread non-blanching petechiae across the trunk and lower limbs; urgent medical review was requested and physiotherapy was deferred pending investigation."
Related Words
petechia (singular) petechial (adj) purpura (larger lesions) ecchymosis (bruising) thrombocytopaenia (cause)

hydrocephalus
/ˌhaɪ.drəʊˈsɛf.ə.ləs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological 小兒 Paediatric
Medical Definition
An abnormal accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid within the cerebral ventricles, leading to raised intracranial pressure; in infants it may cause progressive head enlargement, whereas in older children and adults it presents with headache, vomiting, and altered conscious state.
繁體中文
腦積水
Common Collocations
congenital hydrocephalus shunted hydrocephalus communicating hydrocephalus hydrocephalus secondary to manage hydrocephalus
OET Clinical Example
"Emma, aged 6 months, has been referred to the physiotherapy department following insertion of a ventriculoperitoneal shunt for congenital hydrocephalus; current treatment goals include facilitating head control and supporting developmental milestone acquisition."
Related Words
macrocephaly (enlarged head) ventriculomegaly (enlarged ventricles) VP shunt (treatment) intracranial pressure (consequence) hydrocephalic (adj)
Word 5 of 5
rigidity
/rɪˈdʒɪd.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Increased muscle tone characterised by resistance throughout the entire range of passive movement, independent of movement velocity; distinguished from spasticity (velocity-dependent). Presents as lead-pipe rigidity or cogwheel rigidity (with superimposed tremor), classically associated with Parkinson's disease.
繁體中文
強直；肌肉僵硬
Common Collocations
cogwheel rigidity lead-pipe rigidity truncal rigidity bilateral rigidity rigidity of the upper limbs
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Tran, aged 72, presents with bilateral upper limb cogwheel rigidity and bradykinesia consistent with Parkinson's disease; physiotherapy is aimed at improving mobility, reducing falls risk, and maintaining functional independence."
Related Words
spasticity (velocity-dependent, contrast) hypertonia (increased tone, broader) cogwheel (type with tremor) lead-pipe (uniform type) rigid (adj)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 39)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Dear Dr. Patel,

I am writing to refer Oscar Chen, a 5-year-old male with a background of congenital hydrocephalus managed with a ventriculoperitoneal shunt, for ongoing physiotherapy review.

Oscar was diagnosed with spastic diplegia at 18 months of age following periventricular leukomalacia identified on MRI. He currently ambulates independently over short distances indoors but requires a posterior walker for community mobility. His lower limb tone is increased bilaterally, with scissoring gait pattern noted on assessment.

Of note, Oscar presented to our department last week with a febrile illness. On examination, non-blanching petechiae were identified across his anterior chest wall, prompting immediate referral to the emergency department. Physiotherapy was suspended pending medical clearance, which has since been obtained.

Current physiotherapy goals include improving gait quality, strengthening hip abductors and ankle dorsiflexors, and monitoring shunt function through regular neurological screening. Please find enclosed the most recent functional assessment and school physiotherapy reports.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In paediatric referral letters, always document the child's background diagnosis, current functional level, and any acute clinical concerns (such as non-blanching petechiae) that altered the treatment plan. Use passive constructions sparingly — prefer active, concise clinical language.
