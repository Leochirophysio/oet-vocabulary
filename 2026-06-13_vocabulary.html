hypothermia
/ˌhaɪ.pəˈθɜː.mi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormally low core body temperature below 35°C, occurring when heat loss exceeds heat production; a medical emergency particularly dangerous in neonates, who have limited thermoregulatory capacity.
繁體中文
低體溫症
Common Collocations
mild hypothermia neonatal hypothermia risk of hypothermia hypothermia on admission therapeutic hypothermia
OET Clinical Example
"The neonate was admitted with a core temperature of 34.1°C; hypothermia management was commenced immediately, including skin-to-skin contact and radiant warming, and physiotherapy assessment was deferred until haemodynamic stability was confirmed."
Related Words
hyperthermia (antonym — abnormally high temperature) normothermia (normal body temperature) hypothermic (adjective) thermoregulation (temperature control) pyrexia (fever)

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 53)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Oliver Chen, a male infant born at 36 weeks gestation, was admitted to the neonatal unit for monitoring following an uneventful delivery. On admission, a core temperature of 34.8°C was recorded, confirming hypothermia; warming measures including skin-to-skin contact and a radiant warmer were commenced, and the infant's temperature normalised within four hours.

Subsequent examination by the paediatric team identified mild hepatomegaly, with the liver edge palpable approximately 2 cm below the right costal margin. Palpation of the neck also revealed bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy, with nodes of approximately 1 cm bilaterally. Both findings were communicated to the senior registrar, and an abdominal ultrasound and full blood count were requested to determine the underlying aetiology.

The infant remained alert and well-perfused throughout. Once clinically stable, a neonatal physiotherapy assessment was completed, including evaluation of tone, posture, and feeding. Positioning recommendations and developmental handling advice were provided to the parents, with review scheduled at two weeks of age.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In paediatric referral letters, always document gestational age at birth, exact measurements for clinical findings (e.g. "liver edge palpable 2 cm below the costal margin"), and actions taken before transfer. Avoid vague terms like "a bit enlarged" — precise, objective language is essential in OET writing tasks.
