synovitis
/ˌsɪn.əˈvaɪ.tɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the synovial membrane lining a joint, causing pain, warmth, swelling, and reduced range of motion. May be acute or chronic, and can occur secondary to injury, infection, or inflammatory arthritis.
繁體中文
滑膜炎
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Ultrasound findings confirmed active synovitis of the right knee, with hypertrophied synovial tissue and increased vascularity on Doppler imaging, consistent with early rheumatoid arthritis."
Related Words
synovial membrane (anatomy) synovial fluid tenosynovitis (tendon sheath) arthritis (broader) effusion (joint fluid)

debridement
/dɪˈbriːd.mənt/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The surgical or mechanical removal of devitalised, contaminated, or foreign material from a wound to promote healing and prevent infection. Methods include sharp, enzymatic, autolytic, and mechanical debridement.
繁體中文
清創術
Common Collocations
wound debridement surgical debridement sharp debridement enzymatic debridement debridement of necrotic tissue
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chen was referred to the wound care clinic for debridement of the chronic pressure ulcer over the sacral region, as conservative management had failed to reduce the necrotic burden over four weeks."
Related Words
debrided (adj) wound care (clinical) necrotic tissue (indication) curettage (technique) granulation (healing stage)
Word 3 of 5
kyphosis
/kaɪˈfəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Excessive outward (posterior) curvature of the thoracic spine, producing a rounded or hunched upper back. May be postural, structural (Scheuermann's disease), or secondary to vertebral compression fractures or osteoporosis.
繁體中文
脊椎後凸（駝背）
Common Collocations
thoracic kyphosis postural kyphosis Scheuermann's kyphosis mild / moderate / severe kyphosis kyphotic posture
OET Clinical Example
"Postural assessment revealed a marked thoracic kyphosis of approximately 55 degrees, accompanied by forward head posture and bilateral scapular winging, which was attributed to prolonged sedentary work."
Related Words
kyphotic (adj) hyperkyphosis (excessive) lordosis (anterior curve, in list) scoliosis (lateral curve, in list) flat back deformity (antonym)
Word 4 of 5
hypertrophy
/haɪˈpɜː.trə.fi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An increase in the size of an organ or tissue due to enlargement of its constituent cells rather than an increase in cell number. In musculoskeletal physiotherapy, refers to the increase in muscle fibre size in response to progressive resistance training.
繁體中文
肥大（增生）
Common Collocations
muscle hypertrophy cardiac hypertrophy compensatory hypertrophy left ventricular hypertrophy hypertrophy training
OET Clinical Example
"The rehabilitation programme was designed to promote muscle hypertrophy of the lower limb extensors following prolonged disuse, with progressive resistance training introduced from week three at 60–75% of one-repetition maximum."
Related Words
atrophy (antonym — in list) hypertrophied (adj) hyperplasia (cell number increase) sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) resistance training (stimulus)
Word 5 of 5
traction
/ˈtræk.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A therapeutic or orthopaedic technique in which a sustained or intermittent pulling force is applied to a body part — most commonly the cervical or lumbar spine — to relieve nerve root compression, reduce pain, or restore joint alignment.
繁體中文
牽引（治療）
Common Collocations
cervical traction lumbar traction mechanical traction intermittent traction sustained traction
OET Clinical Example
"Intermittent mechanical traction was applied to the lumbar spine at a force of 40% of body weight over eight sessions, resulting in significant reduction of right-sided radicular symptoms and improved straight leg raise."
Related Words
distraction (joint mobilisation) decompression (spinal) traction force (measurement) manual traction (technique) cervical collar (alternative)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 9)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 52-year-old male office worker, was referred to your physiotherapy outpatient department by his general practitioner with a three-month history of chronic low back pain radiating into the right lower limb, consistent with L4/L5 nerve root irritation confirmed on MRI.

On postural assessment, a pronounced thoracic kyphosis was noted, estimated at 60 degrees by Cobb angle measurement, accompanied by a compensatory lumbar hyperlordosis. The patient reported that his posture had worsened progressively since commencing full-time sedentary work twelve years ago.

Muscle testing revealed bilateral gluteal weakness graded 3+/5, with marked inhibition of the deep spinal stabilisers. A supervised programme of progressive resistance exercise was prescribed with the goal of achieving lower limb muscle hypertrophy and restoring lumbopelvic control.

In addition, a course of intermittent mechanical traction was commenced at 35% of body weight for ten-minute sessions, three times per week, targeting relief of the right-sided radicular symptoms. Following initial assessment, mild synovitis of the right facet joints at L4/L5 was also identified on ultrasound, and a referral for guided corticosteroid injection was made concurrently.

Please note that the patient has an open wound over the right heel secondary to a previous diabetic ulcer; debridement of this wound was completed by the district nursing team last week, and the site is currently in the granulation phase. Weight-bearing exercise has been modified accordingly to offload the affected foot.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting spinal curvature, always specify the region (thoracic, lumbar, cervical) and, where possible, the degree of curvature. For traction, record the force applied (as % body weight or kg), mode (intermittent vs. sustained), and session duration. This level of precision is expected in OET referral and handover writing.
