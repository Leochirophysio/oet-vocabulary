Medical Definition

Medical Definition

cardioversion
/ˌkɑː.di.əʊˈvɜː.ʒən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A procedure used to restore a normal heart rhythm in patients with certain arrhythmias, achieved either by delivering a controlled, synchronised electrical shock (DC cardioversion) or through pharmacological agents (chemical cardioversion).
繁體中文
心臟電擊復律（心律轉復）
Common Collocations
DC cardioversion electrical cardioversion pharmacological cardioversion synchronised cardioversion successful cardioversion
OET Clinical Example
"Following an unsuccessful pharmacological cardioversion, Mr. Chen underwent synchronised DC cardioversion under procedural sedation, with restoration of sinus rhythm confirmed on post-procedure ECG monitoring."
Related Words
defibrillation (unsynchronised shock) atrial fibrillation (common indication) sinus rhythm (target rhythm) arrhythmia cardiovert (verb)

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 41)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 58-year-old male with a background of hypertension and persistent atrial fibrillation, was admitted to the cardiology ward following a two-day history of worsening dyspnoea and pleuritic right-sided chest pain.

A CT pulmonary angiogram confirmed a right-sided pulmonary embolism, and anticoagulation therapy was commenced without delay. He was also noted to have developed sharp anterior chest pain radiating to the left shoulder, and echocardiography subsequently demonstrated features consistent with pericarditis, with a small pericardial effusion measuring 8 mm.

Regarding his atrial fibrillation, the cardiology team elected to proceed with elective DC cardioversion once therapeutic anticoagulation had been maintained for a minimum of four weeks, with the aim of restoring sinus rhythm and improving functional capacity.

Physiotherapy was consulted to assist with early mobilisation and respiratory management. Airway clearance techniques were initiated, and a fibreoptic bronchoscopy was performed by the respiratory team to aspirate retained secretions from the right lower lobe. Post-procedurally, adequate haemostasis was confirmed and the patient tolerated the procedure well.

Goals of physiotherapy include progressive ambulation, breathing exercises, and patient education regarding anticoagulation precautions and early warning signs of re-embolism.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In cardiopulmonary referrals, specify the imaging or test that confirmed the diagnosis (e.g. "CT pulmonary angiogram confirmed"), the intervention performed (e.g. "DC cardioversion planned"), and the patient's functional response (e.g. "tolerated the procedure well"). Precise procedural language demonstrates clinical fluency.
