Medical Definition
A condition of abnormally low muscle tone — the resting tension or resistance to passive stretch in a muscle. Commonly observed in infants and children, and frequently associated with neurological, genetic, or metabolic conditions.

/ˌskəʊ.liˈəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical

Medical Definition
An abnormal lateral (sideways) curvature of the spine, often combined with vertebral rotation. Classified by aetiology (idiopathic, congenital, neuromuscular) and quantified in degrees using the Cobb angle method.
繁體中文
脊椎側彎

OET Clinical Example

Related Words
kyphosis (forward curvature) lordosis (exaggerated inward curvature) scoliotic (adj) Cobb angle (measurement method) spinal deformity

Medical Definition
A condition in which the head is tilted or rotated to one side due to spasm, shortening, or fibrosis of the sternocleidomastoid muscle. May be congenital (present at birth) or acquired following injury, infection, or cervical pathology.

Related Words
wry neck (lay term) cervical dystonia (related) sternocleidomastoid (SCM) head tilt facial asymmetry (associated finding)

Medical Definition
In paediatric medicine, a developmental milestone is a functional skill or age-specific behaviour that most children achieve within a predictable timeframe. Motor milestones (e.g. rolling, sitting, standing, walking) are key markers used to identify neurological and musculoskeletal developmental delay.

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 4)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

James, a 20-month-old male, was referred to the paediatric physiotherapy department by his general practitioner following parental concern regarding delayed motor milestones. His mother reported that he had not yet achieved independent standing or taken any steps, despite these skills being expected between 12 and 15 months of age.

On initial assessment, James demonstrated generalised hypotonia with notably reduced antigravity activity in the lower limbs and poor truncal stability when supported in sitting. Postural screening identified significant shoulder and pelvic asymmetry, with a mild compensatory thoracolumbar curve noted on forward flexion.

In addition, left-sided congenital muscular torticollis was confirmed, with a 25-degree restriction of right cervical rotation. Cranial asymmetry (right occipital flattening consistent with plagiocephaly) was present, attributed to a prolonged positional preference secondary to the torticollis.

Physiotherapy goals include: neurodevelopmental facilitation of weight-bearing and independent standing, core and lower limb strengthening to address hypotonia, cervical stretching and active repositioning strategies for the torticollis, and parent education regarding therapeutic handling and milestone-supportive play activities.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In paediatric referrals, always specify the child's chronological age, the expected age range for the milestone, and the degree of restriction in measurable terms (e.g. "25-degree limitation of cervical rotation"). Avoid vague terms such as "behind" or "not developing normally."
