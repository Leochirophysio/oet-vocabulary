Medical Definition
Complete paralysis of one entire side of the body — including the arm, leg, and sometimes the face — most commonly caused by a stroke or traumatic brain injury affecting the contralateral cerebral hemisphere.

神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormal skin sensation — such as tingling, numbness, prickling, or a "pins and needles" feeling — typically caused by nerve compression, damage, or irritation, without an apparent physical stimulus.
繁體中文
感覺異常（麻木、刺痛感）

OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported persistent paraesthesia in the right hand and forearm, following a C6 dermatomal distribution, which was aggravated by sustained overhead activities and relieved by resting the arm in a supported position."
Related Words

Word 3 of 5
spasticity
/spæˈstɪs.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A velocity-dependent increase in muscle tone characterised by resistance to passive stretch, caused by damage to the upper motor neurone pathways of the brain or spinal cord. Commonly assessed using the Modified Ashworth Scale.
繁體中文
痙攣性肌張力過高
Common Collocations
lower limb spasticity spasticity management grade spasticity post-stroke spasticity spasticity of the flexors
OET Clinical Example
"Physiotherapy was commenced to address significant lower limb spasticity, with tone graded at 2 on the Modified Ashworth Scale in the bilateral hip adductors and knee flexors, limiting passive range of motion and functional transfers."
Related Words
spastic (adj) hypertonia (elevated muscle tone — broader term) clonus (rhythmic involuntary muscle contraction) rigidity (different type of increased tone — uniform) Modified Ashworth Scale (MAS — grading tool)
Word 4 of 5
clarify
/ˈklær.ɪ.faɪ/ verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
To make something easier to understand by providing further explanation or detail; in clinical communication, used to check a patient's understanding, ask them to explain further, or to provide clearer instructions about a procedure or home programme.
繁體中文
澄清、說明清楚
Common Collocations
clarify the instructions clarify any concerns could you clarify clarify the diagnosis clarify what you mean
OET Clinical Example
"Before concluding the session, the physiotherapist took time to clarify each step of the home exercise programme, checking that the patient was able to demonstrate the exercises correctly and felt confident to perform them independently."
Related Words
clarification (noun) elaborate (synonym — to expand in more detail) explain (synonym) paraphrase (restate to confirm understanding) ambiguous (antonym context — unclear)
Word 5 of 5
consent
/kənˈsɛnt/ noun / verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Voluntary agreement or permission given by a patient (or their legal representative) to undergo a medical assessment, procedure, or treatment after receiving adequate information about its nature, purpose, benefits, and potential risks. "Informed consent" is a fundamental ethical and legal requirement in healthcare.
繁體中文
同意、知情同意
Common Collocations
informed consent obtain consent written consent verbal consent consent form
OET Clinical Example
"Prior to commencing the manual therapy component of the session, informed consent was obtained from the patient, with verbal and written explanation provided regarding the procedure, its intended benefits, and the associated risks."
Related Words
informed consent (compound noun — core clinical term) assent (agreement by a minor or person with limited capacity) capacity (legal ability to consent) autonomy (patient's right to decide) refuse / decline (antonym — patient's right to withhold)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 3)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 58-year-old male, was admitted to the neurological rehabilitation unit following a left-sided haemorrhagic stroke, resulting in dense right-sided hemiplegia with complete loss of voluntary movement in the right upper and lower limbs.

On initial physiotherapy assessment, informed consent was obtained prior to commencing evaluation. Significant lower limb spasticity was noted in the right hip adductors and knee flexors, graded 2 on the Modified Ashworth Scale, which was impeding passive range of motion and functional positioning in bed. The patient also reported intermittent paraesthesia in the right forearm and hand, described as a persistent "pins and needles" sensation that was present at rest.

The treating physiotherapist took care to clarify the short-term rehabilitation goals with both Mr. Park and his family, using simple language and visual aids to ensure mutual understanding of the planned programme. Goals include spasticity management via positioning and stretching, facilitation of bed mobility, and assisted transfers to a wheelchair.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting neurological patients, always specify the side affected (e.g. "right-sided hemiplegia"), the severity grading (e.g. "MAS grade 2 spasticity"), and the functional impact (e.g. "impeding passive range of motion"). In OET Speaking, using phrases like "I'd like to clarify..." and "Before we begin, I need to obtain your consent..." demonstrates professionalism and scores highly on the communication criteria.
