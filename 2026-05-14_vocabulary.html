Word 1 of 5
neuroplasticity
/ˌnjʊərəʊplæsˈtɪs.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
The capacity of the brain and nervous system to reorganise its structure, function, and neural connections in response to learning, experience, or injury; the biological basis for rehabilitation-driven recovery.
繁體中文
神經可塑性
Common Collocations
promote neuroplasticity neuroplasticity-based rehabilitation cortical neuroplasticity experience-dependent neuroplasticity harness neuroplasticity
OET Clinical Example
"The rehabilitation programme was designed to harness neuroplasticity through high-repetition, task-specific upper limb training, with the aim of facilitating motor recovery following the patient's left-sided cerebrovascular accident."
Related Words
neuroplastic (adj) cortical reorganisation synaptic plasticity motor relearning neurological recovery
Word 2 of 5
vertigo
/ˈvɜː.tɪ.ɡəʊ/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A false sensation of rotational movement — either the patient spinning or their surroundings rotating — arising from dysfunction of the vestibular system or central nervous system pathways.
繁體中文
眩暈
Common Collocations
benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV) vertigo on head movement episodes of vertigo central vertigo persistent vertigo
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported recurrent episodes of vertigo provoked by rolling to the right in bed, consistent with benign paroxysmal positional vertigo; the Dix-Hallpike test was positive on the right side."
Related Words
vertiginous (adj) dizziness (broader term) vestibular dysfunction nystagmus (associated sign) disequilibrium
Word 3 of 5
apraxia
/eɪˈpræk.si.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A neurological disorder characterised by the inability to perform learned, purposeful movements on command, despite intact muscle strength, sensation, and comprehension; caused by damage to cortical motor planning areas.
繁體中文
失用症
Common Collocations
ideomotor apraxia limb apraxia gait apraxia oral apraxia apraxia of speech
OET Clinical Example
"Physiotherapy assessment identified ideomotor apraxia affecting the left upper limb, impairing the patient's ability to perform tool-use gestures on command despite preserved grip strength and sensation."
Related Words
apraxic (adj) dyspraxia (milder form) motor planning deficit aphasia (language deficit) dysarthria (speech motor disorder)

rapport
/ræˈpɔː/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
A harmonious, trusting relationship between a clinician and patient, characterised by mutual respect, open communication, and empathy; essential for effective therapeutic engagement and treatment adherence.
繁體中文
融洽關係；信任關係
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"The treating physiotherapist established good rapport with the patient during the initial consultation, which facilitated open discussion of his concerns regarding returning to sport following his anterior cruciate ligament reconstruction."
Related Words
therapeutic alliance trust (lay term) patient-centred care empathy concordance (treatment agreement)

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 24)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Nguyen, a 54-year-old male, was referred to the outpatient physiotherapy department following a right middle cerebral artery infarct sustained six weeks ago. He was transferred from the inpatient rehabilitation unit with residual left-sided motor deficits.

Assessment revealed ideomotor apraxia of the left upper limb, limiting his ability to perform self-care tasks on command despite adequate muscle tone and preserved sensation. He also reported intermittent vertigo on rapid head movements, which has been investigated and attributed to secondary benign paroxysmal positional vertigo; a canalith repositioning procedure is planned.

The treating physiotherapist has established good rapport with Mr. Nguyen and his family, and provided reassurance that the rehabilitation programme — designed to harness neuroplasticity through task-specific, high-repetition upper limb training — offers meaningful potential for continued motor recovery over the coming months.

Goals include: reduction of apraxic errors in bilateral upper limb tasks, resolution of positional vertigo, improved independence in activities of daily living, and safe community ambulation without assistive devices.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing neurological referrals, specify the type of deficit (e.g. "ideomotor apraxia" rather than "movement problems"), the functional impact (e.g. "limiting self-care tasks"), and the rehabilitation rationale (e.g. "to harness neuroplasticity"). Precise terminology demonstrates clinical credibility and aids the receiving clinician.
