神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The absence or marked poverty of voluntary movement; a cardinal feature of Parkinson's disease characterised by difficulty initiating movement and a global reduction in spontaneous motor activity.
繁體中文

Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example

Related Words

Medical Definition

Word 5 of 5
confabulation
/kənˌfæb.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The spontaneous production of fabricated, distorted, or misinterpreted memories about oneself or the world, without the conscious intention to deceive; occurs in conditions affecting memory such as Korsakoff's syndrome, dementia, or traumatic brain injury.
繁體中文
虛談（記憶填補）
Common Collocations
spontaneous confabulation provoked confabulation confabulation in Korsakoff's syndrome confabulation following brain injury confabulation in dementia
OET Clinical Example
"The team noted episodes of confabulation during the patient's history-taking, with the patient providing detailed but inconsistent accounts of recent events, suggesting significant memory impairment; family corroboration was sought to obtain an accurate clinical history."
Related Words
confabulate (verb) confabulatory (adj) amnesia (memory loss — related) Korsakoff's syndrome (common cause) anosognosia (unawareness of deficit)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 38)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Tran, a 74-year-old male, was admitted to the neurology ward following a witnessed fall at home. CT imaging confirmed a right frontal contusion consistent with mild traumatic brain injury. He is now medically stable and has been referred to physiotherapy for comprehensive rehabilitation assessment.

Formal assessment of cognition using the Addenbrooke's Cognitive Examination-III revealed a score of 68/100, indicating moderate cognitive impairment across domains of memory, attention, and executive function. Notably, the treating team documented frequent episodes of confabulation during history-taking; the patient provided plausible but demonstrably inaccurate accounts of his pre-admission level of function. Collateral history was subsequently obtained from his daughter.

On physiotherapy assessment, Mr. Tran demonstrated reduced initiation of movement consistent with mild akinesia, with a shuffling gait pattern and difficulty commencing transfers without verbal cueing. Lower limb pain was identified as neurogenic in origin, attributed to pre-existing lumbar stenosis rather than acute musculoskeletal injury.

Throughout the assessment, the physiotherapist maintained an empathetic approach, acknowledging Mr. Tran's visible distress and taking additional time to deliver instructions in short, simple steps to accommodate his cognitive impairment. Goals of rehabilitation include improving safe mobility, cognitive cueing strategies for gait initiation, and carer education regarding safe handling.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting patients with cognitive impairment, specify the assessment tool and score (e.g. "ACE-III: 68/100"), note when collateral history was sought and why, and describe how the clinician adapted their communication approach. These details reflect professional, patient-centred practice expected at OET Band B and above.
