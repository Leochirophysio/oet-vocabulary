hyperalgesia
/ˌhaɪ.pər.æl.ˈdʒiː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
An abnormally heightened sensitivity to pain in which stimuli that would normally cause mild discomfort produce an exaggerated or disproportionate pain response; may indicate peripheral or central sensitisation.
繁體中文
痛覺過敏
Common Collocations
primary hyperalgesia secondary hyperalgesia central hyperalgesia hyperalgesia to touch opioid-induced hyperalgesia
OET Clinical Example
"The patient demonstrated marked hyperalgesia throughout the right lower limb, responding with disproportionate pain to light palpation of the affected area, which is consistent with central sensitisation requiring a pain neuroscience education approach."
Related Words
allodynia (pain from non-painful stimulus) hypoalgesia (reduced pain sensitivity) hyperalgesic (adj) central sensitisation nociception (pain signalling)

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 33)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Nguyen, a 62-year-old retired tradesman, was referred to the physiotherapy outpatient department by his orthopaedic surgeon for conservative management prior to planned right knee replacement surgery.

Mr. Nguyen has a ten-year history of bilateral knee osteoarthritis, confirmed on plain radiograph with significant medial joint space narrowing and subchondral sclerosis bilaterally. He reports constant myalgia throughout both thighs and calves, rated 5/10 at rest and 8/10 on weight-bearing activity, significantly limiting his functional mobility and ability to complete activities of daily living independently.

On clinical examination, a bilateral knee varus deformity was noted, more pronounced on the right. There was also extensive resolving ecchymosis over the right medial knee following a recent fall. Palpation of the peri-patellar region elicited a marked hyperalgesia response disproportionate to the pressure applied, suggesting a component of central sensitisation that will require a pain neuroscience education component within the rehabilitation programme.

Goals of physiotherapy include quadriceps and hip abductor strengthening, gait retraining to reduce varus thrust during ambulation, patient education regarding load management, and pre-operative optimisation of lower limb strength and range of motion.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting pain in referral letters, specify the location, rating (e.g. 5/10 at rest), aggravating factors, and functional impact. Distinguish between nociceptive pain (e.g. osteoarthritis) and central sensitisation features (e.g. hyperalgesia) as they require different management approaches.
