fasciitis
/ˌfæʃ.ɪˈaɪ.tɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the fascia — the fibrous connective tissue that envelops muscles and other body structures. Most commonly encountered in physiotherapy as plantar fasciitis, affecting the thick band of tissue on the sole of the foot.
繁體中文
筋膜炎
Common Collocations
plantar fasciitis chronic fasciitis necrotising fasciitis fasciitis of the heel recurrent fasciitis
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Nguyen was referred for physiotherapy management of confirmed plantar fasciitis, presenting with sharp heel pain of six months' duration, most severe during the first steps in the morning and after prolonged periods of sitting."
Related Words
fascia (noun — the tissue itself) fascial (adj) myofascial (muscle + fascia) plantar fasciitis (most common type) necrotising fasciitis (severe infection)
Word 2 of 5
eccentric
/ɪkˈsɛn.trɪk/ adjective / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
In exercise physiology, describing a muscle contraction in which the muscle generates force while simultaneously lengthening under load. Eccentric exercises are widely used in physiotherapy for tendinopathy rehabilitation, such as the Alfredson protocol for Achilles tendinopathy.
繁體中文
離心性（肌肉收縮）
Common Collocations
eccentric contraction eccentric exercise eccentric loading eccentric strengthening programme eccentric phase
OET Clinical Example
"A structured eccentric loading programme targeting the Achilles tendon was commenced in accordance with the Alfredson protocol, comprising three sets of 15 repetitions performed twice daily on a declined board over a 12-week period."
Related Words
concentric (antonym — muscle shortens) isometric (no length change) plyometric (explosive) eccentric overload tendinopathy (common indication)

acupuncture
/ˈæk.jʊ.pʌŋk.tʃə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
A therapeutic technique involving the insertion of fine sterile needles into specific anatomical points on the body to relieve pain and promote healing. In physiotherapy practice, this often takes the form of Western medical acupuncture or dry needling, targeting myofascial trigger points.
繁體中文

Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Following informed consent, acupuncture was administered to the bilateral upper trapezius and cervical paraspinal musculature, targeting identified trigger points, with the patient reporting a 40% reduction in pain intensity in the 24 hours post-treatment."
Related Words
dry needling (physiotherapy-specific term) trigger point therapy electro-acupuncture acupuncturist (practitioner) needling (verb form)
Word 4 of 5
electromyography
/ɪˌlek.trəʊ.maɪˈɒɡ.rə.fi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
A diagnostic and investigative technique that records the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles; used to assess neuromuscular function, identify muscle activation patterns, diagnose neuropathies and myopathies, and evaluate the efficacy of rehabilitation interventions. Commonly abbreviated as EMG.
繁體中文
肌電圖檢查（EMG）
Common Collocations
surface electromyography needle electromyography EMG findings electromyography study electromyography assessment
OET Clinical Example
"Surface electromyography was utilised during the gait analysis to assess tibialis anterior and gastrocnemius activation timing, with findings demonstrating delayed muscle onset consistent with the reported functional instability."
Related Words
EMG (abbreviation) electromyographic (adj) nerve conduction study / NCS electrophysiology biofeedback (clinical application)
Word 5 of 5
perturbation
/ˌpɜː.tɜːˈbeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
An unexpected or sudden displacement force applied to a patient during balance and proprioceptive rehabilitation to challenge the neuromuscular control system. Perturbation training improves reactive postural responses and is used extensively in functional rehabilitation following lower limb injury or neurological conditions.
繁體中文
擾動訓練（平衡干擾）
Common Collocations
perturbation training balance perturbation perturbation-based rehabilitation controlled perturbation postural perturbation
OET Clinical Example
"Perturbation-based balance training was incorporated into the rehabilitation programme from week three, with the patient progressing from supported perturbation on a stable surface to unsupported perturbation on an unstable platform by week six."
Related Words
perturb (verb) perturbatory (adj) proprioception (sense being trained) balance training (broader category) reactive postural response
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 44)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Tran Van Minh, a 45-year-old male warehouse supervisor, was referred to the physiotherapy department by his GP for management of chronic plantar fasciitis of six months' duration, with concomitant right ankle instability following a recurrent inversion injury.

On initial assessment, marked tenderness was elicited at the medial calcaneal tuberosity bilaterally, consistent with established plantar fasciitis. He reported that pain was most severe during the first 10–15 steps upon waking and following periods of prolonged sitting, and was limiting his ability to complete full shifts at work.

A structured rehabilitation programme was initiated, incorporating eccentric calf-strengthening using the Alfredson protocol (three sets of 15 repetitions, twice daily on a declined board) to address the underlying tendinopathic component. From week two, perturbation-based balance training was introduced on an unstable platform to address the proprioceptive deficit associated with the recurrent ankle instability. Acupuncture was additionally administered to the gastrocnemius and soleus musculature, targeting identified trigger points, with the patient reporting a 35% reduction in pain intensity following the initial session.

Surface electromyography was utilised during functional gait assessment to quantify tibialis anterior and peroneal activation patterns, confirming delayed muscle onset responses consistent with the reported instability. These findings will inform the progression of the neuromuscular retraining component of the programme.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting procedures in referral letters, specify the protocol used (e.g. "Alfredson protocol"), dosage (e.g. "three sets of 15 repetitions, twice daily"), and objective outcome measures (e.g. "35% reduction in pain intensity") rather than vague descriptors. This demonstrates clinical reasoning and facilitates continuity of care.
