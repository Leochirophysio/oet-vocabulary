infarction
/ɪnˈfɑːk.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The death of tissue in a localised area of the body caused by an obstruction of its blood supply, resulting in ischaemia and subsequent necrosis; most commonly refers to myocardial infarction when a coronary artery is occluded.
繁體中文
梗塞（組織壞死）
Common Collocations
myocardial infarction acute infarction cerebral infarction pulmonary infarction subendocardial infarction
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chan was admitted to the coronary care unit following a confirmed acute myocardial infarction; he has been medically stabilised and is now referred for graded cardiac rehabilitation commencing at low intensity."
Related Words
ischaemia (insufficient blood supply) necrosis (tissue death) infarct (noun — the dead tissue area) infarcted (adj) MI (abbreviation)

oxygenation
/ˌɒk.sɪ.dʒɪˈneɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The process of supplying or enriching body tissues with oxygen; monitored clinically via pulse oximetry (SpO₂) or arterial blood gas analysis (PaO₂), and optimised through supplemental oxygen therapy, positioning, and breathing exercises.
繁體中文
氧合（血氧供應）
Common Collocations
adequate oxygenation impaired oxygenation oxygenation status tissue oxygenation optimise oxygenation
OET Clinical Example
"Oxygenation was maintained at SpO₂ 94% on 2 L/min supplemental oxygen at rest; however, the patient demonstrated significant desaturation to 84% on exertion during the six-minute walk test, necessitating cessation of the assessment."
Related Words
desaturation (antonym — fall in SpO₂) hypoxia (insufficient oxygen in tissue) oxygenate (verb) oxygenated (adj) SpO₂ / oxygen saturation (measurement)

bronchodilator
/ˌbrɒŋ.kəʊˈdaɪ.leɪ.tər/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A medication that relaxes and widens the bronchial smooth muscle, increasing airflow to the lungs; used to treat conditions such as asthma and COPD; may be short-acting (SABA, e.g. salbutamol) or long-acting (LABA, e.g. salmeterol).
繁體中文
支氣管擴張劑
Common Collocations
short-acting bronchodilator long-acting bronchodilator inhaled bronchodilator bronchodilator therapy administer a bronchodilator
OET Clinical Example
"Prior to commencing pulmonary rehabilitation exercise sessions, the patient was instructed to use his short-acting bronchodilator to optimise airway patency and reduce the risk of exercise-induced bronchospasm."
Related Words
bronchospasm (condition it treats) bronchoconstriction (opposite effect) bronchodilation (noun — the effect) salbutamol / ventolin (SABA example) beta-agonist (drug class)

pericardiocentesis
/ˌper.ɪˌkɑː.di.əʊ.senˈtiː.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A medical procedure in which a needle or catheter is inserted through the chest wall into the pericardial sac to aspirate excess fluid (pericardial effusion); performed under echocardiographic or fluoroscopic guidance to relieve cardiac tamponade and restore haemodynamic stability.
繁體中文
心包穿刺術
Common Collocations
emergency pericardiocentesis ultrasound-guided pericardiocentesis perform pericardiocentesis therapeutic pericardiocentesis post-pericardiocentesis care
OET Clinical Example
"Following urgent echocardiography confirming a large pericardial effusion with haemodynamic compromise, the cardiology team performed emergency pericardiocentesis; the patient subsequently stabilised and was transferred to the cardiac ward for ongoing monitoring."
Related Words
tamponade (complication requiring it) pericardial effusion (fluid drained) pericarditis (common cause of effusion) thoracocentesis (analogous pleural procedure) pericardiectomy (surgical removal of pericardium)

anticoagulation
/ˌæn.ti.kəʊˌæɡ.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The use of anticoagulant medications to inhibit blood clotting, reducing the risk of thromboembolic events; commonly prescribed after myocardial infarction, pulmonary embolism, atrial fibrillation, or joint replacement surgery, and has direct implications for physiotherapy intervention planning.
繁體中文
抗凝血治療
Common Collocations
therapeutic anticoagulation anticoagulation therapy initiate anticoagulation systemic anticoagulation anticoagulation management
OET Clinical Example
"The patient is currently maintained on therapeutic anticoagulation with warfarin following a confirmed pulmonary embolism six weeks ago; please note that this has implications for manual therapy and exercise intensity during physiotherapy rehabilitation."
Related Words
anticoagulant (adj/noun — the drug) coagulation (clotting process) thrombosis (condition prevented) warfarin / heparin (drug examples) INR (monitoring measure)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 62)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 58-year-old male with a background of COPD (GOLD Stage III) and a recent non-ST-elevation myocardial infarction (NSTEMI) three weeks ago, has been referred to the cardiopulmonary physiotherapy team for graded rehabilitation.

He is currently maintained on therapeutic anticoagulation with rivaroxaban following a concurrent deep vein thrombosis identified on Doppler ultrasound at the time of admission. This has been taken into account in the exercise prescription, with contact sports and high-impact activities contraindicated for the duration of treatment.

Prior to each rehabilitation session, the patient is instructed to use his short-acting bronchodilator (salbutamol 200 mcg via metered-dose inhaler) to optimise airway patency and minimise the risk of exercise-induced bronchospasm. Oxygenation is monitored continuously throughout exercise using pulse oximetry, with sessions modified or ceased if SpO₂ falls below 88% on room air.

Goals of physiotherapy include progressive aerobic conditioning within safe cardiopulmonary parameters, patient education regarding symptom monitoring, and optimisation of functional capacity ahead of planned outpatient cardiac and pulmonary rehabilitation programmes.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters for patients on anticoagulation, always state the specific agent (e.g. warfarin, rivaroxaban), the indication (e.g. DVT, AF), and any clinical implications for the receiving therapist (e.g. contraindications to manual techniques or impact activities). This demonstrates precise, safe clinical handover language valued highly in OET Writing.
