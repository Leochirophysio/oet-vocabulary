encephalitis
/ɪnˌsɛf.əˈlaɪ.tɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the brain parenchyma, most commonly caused by viral infection (e.g. herpes simplex virus) or autoimmune processes. Presents with fever, altered consciousness, seizures, and focal neurological deficits; a medical emergency requiring urgent investigation and treatment.
繁體中文
腦炎
Common Collocations
viral encephalitis autoimmune encephalitis herpes simplex encephalitis acute encephalitis encephalitis with seizures
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Patel was admitted to the neurology ward with suspected autoimmune encephalitis, presenting with a one-week history of behavioural change, fever, and two witnessed tonic-clonic seizures; MRI brain demonstrated T2 hyperintensity in the bilateral medial temporal lobes."
Related Words
encephalitic (adj) encephalopathy (brain dysfunction, broader) meningitis (inflammation of meninges) meningoencephalitis (combined) cerebritis (focal brain inflammation)
Word 2 of 5
meningism
/ˈmɛn.ɪn.dʒɪz.əm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A clinical syndrome characterised by neck stiffness (nuchal rigidity), photophobia, and headache, suggesting irritation of the meninges. May occur in meningitis, subarachnoid haemorrhage, or encephalitis. Assessed by Kernig's and Brudzinski's signs on neurological examination.
繁體中文
腦膜刺激症
Common Collocations
signs of meningism positive meningism meningism on examination meningism without confirmed infection nuchal rigidity / meningism
OET Clinical Example
"On neurological examination, Mrs. Chen demonstrated marked meningism with positive Kernig's and Brudzinski's signs alongside severe photophobia; she was referred urgently to the emergency department for lumbar puncture and further management."
Related Words
meningismus (alternative term) nuchal rigidity (key feature) photophobia (feature) Kernig's sign (test) meninges (anatomical site)
Word 3 of 5
dysdiadochokinesia
/ˌdɪs.daɪˌæd.ə.kəʊ.kɪˈniː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Impaired ability to perform rapid, rhythmic alternating movements (such as forearm pronation and supination), indicative of cerebellar dysfunction. Tested clinically by asking the patient to rapidly alternate between slapping the palm and dorsum of the hand on the thigh.
繁體中文
輪替運動障礙
Common Collocations
bilateral dysdiadochokinesia dysdiadochokinesia on testing cerebellar dysdiadochokinesia dysdiadochokinesia of the upper limbs dysdiadochokinesia and intention tremor
OET Clinical Example
"Cerebellar function testing revealed bilateral dysdiadochokinesia and intention tremor in the upper limbs, consistent with the patient's diagnosis of spinocerebellar ataxia; physiotherapy was commenced to address coordination, balance, and upper limb functional deficits."
Related Words
adiadochokinesia (complete inability) diadochokinesia (normal alternating movement) dysmetria (overshooting on reaching) ataxia (broader cerebellar incoordination) cerebellar signs (clinical category)
Word 4 of 5
cruciate
/ˈkruː.si.ɪt/ adjective / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Cross-shaped; in orthopaedics, refers to the cruciate ligaments of the knee — the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) and posterior cruciate ligament (PCL) — which cross within the joint and provide anteroposterior and rotational stability to the knee.
繁體中文
十字形的；十字韌帶
Common Collocations
anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) posterior cruciate ligament (PCL) cruciate ligament tear cruciate reconstruction cruciate-deficient knee
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Thompson sustained a complete rupture of the anterior cruciate ligament during a rugby tackle, confirmed on MRI; he has been referred to the orthopaedic surgeon for consideration of cruciate ligament reconstruction, with pre-operative physiotherapy commenced to restore full range of motion and quadriceps strength."
Related Words
ACL / PCL (abbreviations) collateral ligament (medial / lateral) ligamentous laxity Lachman test (ACL assessment) pivot shift (ACL instability sign)
Word 5 of 5
synovectomy
/ˌsɪn.əˈvɛk.tə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
Surgical excision of the synovial membrane lining a joint, performed to treat conditions in which the synovium is chronically inflamed and hypertrophied, such as rheumatoid arthritis or pigmented villonodular synovitis, when conservative management has failed.
繁體中文
滑膜切除術
Common Collocations
arthroscopic synovectomy open synovectomy total synovectomy post-synovectomy rehabilitation synovectomy of the knee
OET Clinical Example
"Following unsuccessful conservative management of rheumatoid arthritis affecting the right knee, Mrs. Ahmed underwent arthroscopic synovectomy and has been referred to physiotherapy for post-operative rehabilitation, focusing on restoring range of motion, reducing joint effusion, and rebuilding quadriceps strength."
Related Words
synovium / synovial membrane (tissue removed) synovitis (indication for surgery) arthroscopy (surgical approach) rheumatoid arthritis (common indication) arthroplasty (more extensive joint procedure)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 70)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Liu, a 45-year-old male software engineer with no significant past medical history, was admitted to the neurology ward following a one-week history of confusion, low-grade fever, and progressive behavioural change. Initial assessment revealed meningism with nuchal rigidity and marked photophobia. Lumbar puncture findings were consistent with autoimmune encephalitis, and the patient was commenced on intravenous immunoglobulin and high-dose corticosteroids.

On neurological examination by the physiotherapist, Mr. Liu demonstrated bilateral dysdiadochokinesia in the upper limbs, intention tremor, and truncal ataxia, indicating significant cerebellar involvement. He was unable to maintain standing balance unsupported and required maximum assistance for all transfers.

He has been referred to the inpatient neurorehabilitation team for assessment and commencement of a structured rehabilitation programme. Treatment goals include improvement of upper limb coordination, restoration of safe independent mobility, and patient and family education regarding expected recovery trajectory.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing neurological referral letters, specify the neurological signs elicited (e.g. "bilateral dysdiadochokinesia and intention tremor"), the functional impact (e.g. "unable to maintain standing balance unsupported"), and the aetiology (e.g. "autoimmune encephalitis"). Avoid vague terms such as "brain problems" or "coordination issues" — precise clinical language is essential for OET Writing Task B.
