dysarthria
/dɪsˈɑː.θri.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A motor speech disorder caused by weakness, incoordination, or paralysis of the muscles used for speaking, resulting in slurred, slow, or otherwise unclear speech. It arises from neurological damage rather than a language-processing deficit.
繁體中文
構音障礙
Common Collocations
spastic dysarthria severe dysarthria dysarthria following stroke dysarthric speech mild dysarthria
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented with marked dysarthria following a left-hemisphere CVA, rendering speech difficult to understand; referral to speech-language therapy has been arranged as part of the multidisciplinary rehabilitation programme."
Related Words
dysarthric (adj) aphasia (language loss) dysphasia (language impairment) anarthria (severe — no speech) slurred speech (lay term)
Word 2 of 5
spasticity
/spæˈstɪs.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A condition of velocity-dependent increased muscle tone resulting from an upper motor neurone lesion, characterised by stiffness and resistance to passive movement. It differs from rigidity in that the resistance is speed-dependent (the "clasp-knife" phenomenon).
繁體中文
痙攣性（肌張力增高）
Common Collocations
lower limb spasticity spasticity management spasticity graded on the Modified Ashworth Scale spasticity following TBI significant spasticity
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Huang presented with significant bilateral lower limb spasticity graded 3 on the Modified Ashworth Scale, which was impeding transfer training and gait rehabilitation; botulinum toxin injection has been discussed with the treating neurologist."
Related Words
spastic (adj) hypertonicity (increased tone) rigidity (lead-pipe resistance) clonus (rhythmic spasm) flaccidity (antonym — low tone)

paraesthesia
/ˌpær.ɪsˈθiː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormal skin sensation — such as tingling, burning, numbness, or "pins and needles" — occurring without apparent external cause. It typically indicates nerve compression, irritation, or damage, and may be transient or chronic.
繁體中文
感覺異常（麻刺感）
Common Collocations
intermittent paraesthesia peripheral paraesthesia paraesthesia radiating to the hand burning paraesthesia paraesthesia on waking
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported intermittent paraesthesia radiating from the right buttock to the calf, consistent with L4/L5 nerve root irritation, which worsened with prolonged sitting and was partially relieved by lumbar extension exercises."
Related Words
paraesthetic (adj) anaesthesia (no sensation) hypoaesthesia (reduced sensation) dysaesthesia (unpleasant sensation) pins and needles (lay term)
Word 4 of 5
lordosis
/lɔːˈdəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
An excessive inward (anterior) curvature of the lumbar or cervical spine. Mild lordosis is a normal feature of spinal alignment; however, excessive or pathological lordosis is associated with lower back pain, anterior pelvic tilt, and altered movement mechanics.
繁體中文
脊椎前凸（前彎過大）
Common Collocations
lumbar lordosis cervical lordosis excessive lordosis loss of lumbar lordosis increased lordosis
OET Clinical Example
"Postural assessment revealed marked lumbar lordosis with associated anterior pelvic tilt and shortened hip flexors, which was identified as a contributing factor to the patient's chronic non-specific lower back pain."
Related Words
lordotic (adj) kyphosis (posterior thoracic curve) scoliosis (lateral curve) neutral spine alignment anterior pelvic tilt (associated finding)
Word 5 of 5
palpation
/pælˈpeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A clinical examination technique in which the clinician uses their hands or fingers to feel structures of the body, assessing for tenderness, temperature, texture, swelling, muscle tone, and anatomical abnormalities. One of the four core physical examination methods alongside inspection, percussion, and auscultation.
繁體中文
觸診
Common Collocations
soft tissue palpation tenderness on palpation palpation of the spine palpation reveals deep palpation
OET Clinical Example
"Palpation of the lumbar paraspinal musculature elicited localised tenderness at L3–L4 with palpable muscle guarding, most pronounced on the right side, consistent with the patient's reported pain distribution."
Related Words
palpate (verb) palpable (adj — can be felt) inspection (visual examination) percussion (tapping technique) auscultation (listening technique)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 7)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Huang, a 52-year-old male with a two-year history of relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis, was referred to the outpatient physiotherapy department for a comprehensive gait rehabilitation programme following a recent relapse with residual motor and sensory deficits.

On initial assessment, the patient demonstrated significant bilateral lower limb spasticity, graded 2–3 on the Modified Ashworth Scale, which was impeding his walking speed and foot clearance during the swing phase. He reported intermittent paraesthesia affecting both feet and the anterior aspects of his lower legs, described as a persistent burning sensation that worsened with physical fatigue.

Palpation of the lumbar spine revealed no focal bony tenderness; however, postural assessment identified increased lumbar lordosis with associated anterior pelvic tilt, likely contributing to the compensatory gait pattern observed on treadmill analysis. The patient also exhibited mild dysarthria, consistent with prior brainstem involvement documented in his neurology notes, and has an established referral to speech-language therapy.

Goals of physiotherapy include spasticity management through a graded stretching and positioning programme, progressive gait training with appropriate assistive devices, postural correction to address the lumbar lordosis, and coordination with the multidisciplinary team regarding symptom monitoring and fatigue management strategies.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting neurological signs, always specify the assessment scale used (e.g. "Modified Ashworth Scale"), the body region affected (e.g. "bilateral lower limb spasticity"), and the functional impact (e.g. "impeding gait rehabilitation"). This level of precision is essential for OET Writing Band B and above.
