✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 1)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs. Li Wei, a 68-year-old female with a background of chronic heart failure (NYHA Class III), was referred to your physiotherapy department for rehabilitation following a recent acute exacerbation requiring hospitalisation.

On initial assessment, she presented with bilateral peripheral oedema to the lower limbs, graded 2+ bilaterally, and reported ongoing exertional dyspnoea limiting her to walking fewer than 15 metres before requiring rest. She denied palpitations at rest but noted occasional episodes during light household activities.

Physical examination revealed marked quadriceps atrophy bilaterally, consistent with prolonged deconditioning and reduced activity levels over the preceding two months. She was currently unable to ambulate without a wheeled walking frame and required supervision due to safety concerns regarding her balance.

Goals of physiotherapy include progressive ambulation training, lower limb strengthening to address muscle atrophy, and patient education regarding energy conservation techniques and symptom monitoring.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In referral letters, always specify grade/severity of symptoms (e.g. "2+ pitting oedema"), functional impact (e.g. "limiting to 15 metres"), and current assist level (e.g. "with walking frame under supervision"). Avoid vague language like "very bad" or "a lot of difficulty."
