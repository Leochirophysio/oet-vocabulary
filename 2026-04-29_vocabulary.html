Word 1 of 5
spasticity
/spæˈstɪs.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A velocity-dependent increase in muscle tone with exaggerated tendon reflexes, resulting from hyperexcitability of the stretch reflex; a component of upper motor neurone syndrome commonly seen in cerebral palsy, stroke, and spinal cord injury.
繁體中文
痙攣（肌肉張力過高）
Common Collocations
lower limb spasticity manage spasticity spasticity assessment reduce spasticity spasticity graded on the Modified Ashworth Scale
OET Clinical Example
"The child presented with bilateral lower limb spasticity graded 2 on the Modified Ashworth Scale, significantly impacting his gait pattern and functional mobility on level surfaces."
Related Words
hypertonia (increased tone) spastic (adj) spastic diplegia Modified Ashworth Scale clonus (associated sign)
Word 2 of 5
dyskinesia
/ˌdɪs.kɪˈniː.zi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal involuntary movements such as chorea, athetosis, or dystonia; may result from basal ganglia dysfunction, prolonged dopamine-blocking medication (tardive dyskinesia), or neurological conditions such as cerebral palsy.
繁體中文
運動障礙（不自主運動）
Common Collocations
tardive dyskinesia orofacial dyskinesia drug-induced dyskinesia dyskinesia management dyskinetic movements
OET Clinical Example
"James, aged 14, was referred for physiotherapy assessment of dyskinesia characterised by repetitive writhing movements of the upper limbs, which were significantly impacting his ability to participate in classroom activities and self-care tasks."
Related Words
bradykinesia (slowness of movement) akinesia (absence of movement) dyskinetic (adj) dystonia (sustained abnormal posture) choreoathetosis (writhing + jerking)

plagiocephaly
/ˌpleɪ.dʒi.əʊˈsɛf.ə.li/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Asymmetrical distortion or flattening of the skull, most commonly a flattening of one side of the occiput in infants (positional or deformational plagiocephaly); frequently associated with congenital muscular torticollis and managed with physiotherapy and positional advice.
繁體中文
斜頭畸形（頭顱不對稱）
Common Collocations
positional plagiocephaly deformational plagiocephaly posterior plagiocephaly plagiocephaly with torticollis mild / moderate / severe plagiocephaly
OET Clinical Example
"Emma, aged 3 months, was referred to physiotherapy with a diagnosis of right-sided positional plagiocephaly and associated congenital muscular torticollis, presenting with marked occipital flattening on the right and a preference for right cervical rotation."
Related Words
torticollis (associated condition) brachycephaly (shortened skull) scaphocephaly (elongated skull) craniosynostosis (premature skull fusion) repositioning therapy
Word 4 of 5
hypermobility
/ˌhaɪ.pəˌməʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Excessive range of motion in one or more joints beyond the normal physiological limit, due to laxity of the supporting ligaments and connective tissue; may be generalised or localised and predisposes to joint instability, pain, and recurrent injury, particularly in children.
繁體中文
關節過度活動（關節鬆弛）
Common Collocations
joint hypermobility generalised hypermobility hypermobility syndrome symptomatic hypermobility hypermobility of the fingers
OET Clinical Example
"The patient, an 8-year-old female, was referred with suspected generalised hypermobility, scoring 7 out of 9 on the Beighton Scale, and presenting with recurrent ankle sprains and bilateral knee pain following participation in gymnastics."
Related Words
hypermobile (adj) Beighton Score (assessment tool) Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (associated) joint laxity (lay/clinical) instability (consequence)
Word 5 of 5
clonus
/ˈkləʊ.nəs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A series of rhythmic, involuntary muscular contractions and relaxations elicited by sudden passive stretching of a muscle group; a sign of an upper motor neurone lesion indicating hyperreflexia, most commonly assessed at the ankle.
繁體中文
陣攣
Common Collocations
ankle clonus sustained clonus unsustained clonus elicit clonus beats of clonus
OET Clinical Example
"Neurological examination revealed three beats of ankle clonus on the right, consistent with an upper motor neurone lesion; this finding was documented and referred to the treating paediatrician for further investigation."
Related Words
hyperreflexia (associated sign) spasticity (associated feature) upper motor neurone syndrome myoclonus (sudden muscle jerks) clonic (adj)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 11)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Dear Dr. Patel,

Thank you for referring Master Liam Chen, aged 6 years, to our paediatric physiotherapy service. Liam was diagnosed with spastic diplegic cerebral palsy at 18 months of age and has been reviewed at our clinic for ongoing management of his neuromotor presentation.

On assessment, Liam demonstrated bilateral lower limb spasticity graded 1+ on the Modified Ashworth Scale at the hamstrings and gastrocnemius bilaterally. Of note, three beats of clonus were elicited at the right ankle on dorsiflexion, which represents an increase from his previous assessment six months ago and warrants medical review.

Gait analysis identified a crouch gait pattern with bilateral hip and knee flexion during mid-stance. There were no features of dyskinesia observed during the assessment. Functional mobility has improved, with Liam now able to ambulate independently on level surfaces over short distances using bilateral ankle-foot orthoses.

Goals for the coming six months include progressive strengthening of the lower limb extensors, gait retraining, and review of orthotic prescription in consultation with the orthotist. I would be grateful for your review regarding the increase in right ankle clonus and to discuss whether botulinum toxin therapy may be appropriate at this stage.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In paediatric referral letters, always specify the child's age and diagnosis, use objective measurement scales (e.g. Modified Ashworth Scale, Beighton Score), describe any changes from previous assessments, and clearly state the reason for referral. Avoid vague terms like "stiff" — use precise clinical language such as "spasticity graded 1+ MAS."
