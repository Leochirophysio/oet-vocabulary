Word 1 of 5
labrum
/ˈleɪ.brəm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
A ring of fibrocartilage attached to the rim of a joint socket (glenoid in the shoulder or acetabulum in the hip) that deepens the socket, distributes load, and contributes to joint stability.
繁體中文
關節唇（纖維軟骨環）
Common Collocations
glenoid labrum acetabular labrum labral tear labral repair SLAP lesion
OET Clinical Example
"MRI of the right shoulder confirmed a superior glenoid labrum tear, consistent with the patient's reported history of anterior shoulder instability and mechanical clicking during overhead reaching."
Related Words
labral (adj) SLAP lesion (superior labrum ant–post tear) Bankart lesion (anterior labral) acetabular labrum glenoid (the socket)
Word 2 of 5
osseous
/ˈɒs.i.əs/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
Of, relating to, or resembling bone; consisting of or containing bone tissue. Used in clinical documentation to specify that a finding, lesion, or structure is bony in nature.
繁體中文
骨性的；骨質的
Common Collocations
osseous structure osseous lesion osseous defect osseous impingement osseous metastasis
OET Clinical Example
"Plain radiographs demonstrated an osseous defect at the posterior glenoid rim, and the patient was referred to the orthopaedic team for consideration of surgical intervention."
Related Words
ossify (verb — to become bone) ossification (noun) bony (lay / informal equivalent) cortical (relating to outer bone layer) trabecular (relating to inner bone structure)
Word 3 of 5
spasm
/ˈspæz.əm/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A sudden, involuntary contraction of a muscle or group of muscles, often sustained, causing pain and restricting movement. May occur in response to injury, nerve irritation, or overuse.
繁體中文
肌肉痙攣
Common Collocations
muscle spasm lumbar spasm paravertebral spasm acute spasm spasm on palpation
OET Clinical Example
"On examination, marked paravertebral spasm was noted bilaterally at the L4–L5 level, with associated restriction of all active lumbar movements and significant pain on palpation."
Related Words
spasmodic (adj — occurring in spasms) spasticity (upper motor neurone — sustained) cramp (lay term) contracture (chronic shortening) guarding (protective muscle contraction)
Word 4 of 5
intermittent
/ˌɪn.təˈmɪt.ənt/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Occurring at irregular or alternating intervals; not continuous or constant. Used to describe symptoms, pain, or conditions that come and go rather than persisting without interruption.
繁體中文
間歇性的；時斷時續的
Common Collocations
intermittent pain intermittent symptoms intermittent claudication intermittent locking intermittent swelling
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Taufa described intermittent anterior knee pain aggravated by stair descent and prolonged sitting, with episodes lasting approximately 20–30 minutes before spontaneously resolving with rest."
Related Words
constant (antonym) persistent (antonym) episodic (similar — distinct episodes) periodic (occurring at regular intervals) paroxysmal (sudden onset episodes)
Word 5 of 5
deconditioning
/ˌdiː.kənˈdɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A reversible deterioration of physical fitness, muscular strength, and cardiovascular endurance resulting from prolonged inactivity, bed rest, or immobilisation. A key target of physiotherapy rehabilitation.
繁體中文
體能衰退；去適應化
Common Collocations
physical deconditioning generalised deconditioning deconditioning secondary to bed rest address deconditioning deconditioning following surgery
OET Clinical Example
"Following a five-week inpatient admission for bilateral pneumonia, Mrs. Sione presented with significant physical deconditioning, including reduced lower limb strength and exercise tolerance, requiring a graded rehabilitation programme prior to discharge home."
Related Words
deconditioned (adj) reconditioning (antonym — process of restoring fitness) disuse atrophy (muscle wasting from inactivity) sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) detraining (loss of fitness from stopping exercise)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 54)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Daniel Park, a 34-year-old recreational tennis player, was referred to your physiotherapy department following arthroscopic repair of a right glenoid labrum tear six weeks ago. Pre-operatively, imaging had revealed both the labral injury and an associated osseous defect at the posterior glenoid rim.

At initial physiotherapy assessment, Mr. Park reported intermittent aching across the anterior shoulder, rated 3–4/10 at rest and worsening to 6/10 with active elevation beyond 90 degrees. Palpation of the posterior shoulder musculature elicited localised spasm in the infraspinatus and teres minor, with notable restriction of passive external rotation to 30 degrees compared with 75 degrees on the unaffected side.

Significant upper limb deconditioning was evident, with marked rotator cuff weakness bilaterally, consistent with the six-week period of postoperative immobilisation and restricted activity. A progressive rehabilitation programme has been commenced, focusing on pain-free range of motion restoration, rotator cuff strengthening, and scapular stabilisation, with a return-to-sport goal of 16 weeks post-operatively.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters for post-surgical patients, always document the operative procedure and date, current objective findings (range of motion in degrees, strength grades), and a clear rehabilitation goal with timeframe. Avoid vague terms — specify measurements wherever possible.
