spondylolysis
/ˌspɒn.dɪˈlɒl.ɪ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A stress fracture or defect in the pars interarticularis — a small section of bone connecting the upper and lower facet joints within a vertebra. Most common at L5, it can be unilateral or bilateral, and may progress to spondylolisthesis (forward vertebral slippage) if left unmanaged.
繁體中文

Common Collocations
lumbar spondylolysis bilateral spondylolysis L5 spondylolysis symptomatic spondylolysis spondylolysis repair
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chen, a 16-year-old competitive gymnast, was referred to physiotherapy with a confirmed diagnosis of bilateral L5 spondylolysis. He presents with activity-related low back pain and is currently managed conservatively with bracing and activity modification."
Related Words
spondylolytic (adj) spondylolisthesis (forward vertebral slip) pars defect (synonym) stress fracture (mechanism) spondylosis (degenerative — different condition)
Word 2 of 5
plexopathy
/plekˈsɒp.ə.θi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Damage or disease affecting a nerve plexus — a network of intersecting nerves. Most commonly involves the brachial plexus (supplying the arm and shoulder) or the lumbosacral plexus (supplying the lower limb). Causes include trauma, tumour compression, radiation injury, and inflammatory conditions. Presents with limb weakness, sensory loss, and pain in the distribution of the affected plexus.
繁體中文
神經叢病變
Common Collocations
brachial plexopathy lumbosacral plexopathy traumatic plexopathy radiation-induced plexopathy plexopathy secondary to
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs. Singh was referred with a diagnosis of left brachial plexopathy following a traction injury sustained during a motor vehicle accident. She presents with significant weakness of the left shoulder girdle and sensory loss along the C5–C6 dermatomal distribution."
Related Words
plexus (nerve network) brachial plexus (arm/shoulder) lumbosacral plexus (lower limb) neuropathy (broader term) radiculopathy (nerve root — different level)
Word 3 of 5
laminectomy
/ˌlæm.ɪˈnek.tə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A surgical procedure in which part or all of the lamina — the posterior arch of a vertebra — is removed to relieve pressure on the spinal cord or nerve roots. Commonly performed for lumbar spinal stenosis, herniated intervertebral discs, or spinal tumours. May be combined with spinal fusion for added stability.
繁體中文
椎板切除術
Common Collocations
lumbar laminectomy cervical laminectomy decompressive laminectomy laminectomy with fusion post-laminectomy rehabilitation
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Thompson is referred post lumbar laminectomy at L3–L4 for rehabilitation. He underwent surgery six weeks ago and is progressing well, though he continues to report residual left leg weakness and intermittent pins and needles. Please assess and commence a graded strengthening programme."
Related Words
laminotomy (partial lamina removal) discectomy (disc removal) spinal decompression (functional goal) laminoplasty (lamina reshaping) lamina (posterior vertebral arch)
Word 4 of 5
iliopsoas
/ˌɪl.i.əʊˈsəʊ.æs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The combined muscle group formed by the iliacus and psoas major muscles. It is the primary hip flexor, originating from the lumbar vertebrae and iliac fossa, inserting onto the lesser trochanter of the femur. Tightness or weakness of the iliopsoas is a common clinical finding in low back pain, hip conditions, and postural dysfunction.
繁體中文
髂腰肌
Common Collocations
iliopsoas tightness iliopsoas weakness iliopsoas tendinopathy iliopsoas stretch iliopsoas release
OET Clinical Example
"Assessment revealed marked bilateral iliopsoas tightness, consistent with a predominantly sedentary occupational posture. A structured hip flexor stretching and core stabilisation programme has been commenced to address the biomechanical imbalance contributing to his chronic low back pain."
Related Words
iliacus (component muscle) psoas major (component muscle) hip flexor (functional description) Thomas test (assessment for tightness) lesser trochanter (insertion point)
Word 5 of 5
apprehension
/ˌæp.rɪˈhen.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
In clinical practice, apprehension has two distinct uses: (1) a patient's anxiety or fear regarding a movement, procedure, or diagnosis; (2) a positive apprehension sign during physical examination, where the patient expresses pain, muscle guarding, or fear of impending dislocation — most commonly elicited during the glenohumeral apprehension test for anterior shoulder instability at 90° abduction and external rotation.
繁體中文
憂慮感；（理學檢查）恐懼徵象
Common Collocations
positive apprehension sign glenohumeral apprehension test apprehension with shoulder abduction patient apprehension apprehension regarding
OET Clinical Example
"On examination, the patient demonstrated a positive apprehension sign at 90° of shoulder abduction and external rotation, consistent with anterior glenohumeral instability. She also expressed significant apprehension regarding return to overhead sporting activities and will benefit from graded exposure as part of her rehabilitation."
Related Words
apprehensive (adj) apprehension test (clinical test) anxiety (related — patient context) glenohumeral instability (associated condition) relocation test (follow-up test)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 63)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. Liam Nguyen, a 17-year-old competitive swimmer, was referred to your physiotherapy department following surgical management of L5 spondylolysis with associated grade I spondylolisthesis. He underwent decompressive laminectomy at L4–L5 eight weeks ago and has been cleared by his surgeon to commence active rehabilitation.

On initial physiotherapy assessment, Liam demonstrated marked bilateral iliopsoas tightness and weakness of the lumbar stabilisers, consistent with deconditioning following a prolonged period of restricted activity. He expressed significant apprehension regarding trunk rotation and lumbar loading activities, and required reassurance that these movements are safe within his current post-operative precautions.

In addition, he was referred to neurology for review of an incidental finding of left-sided upper limb weakness noted on post-operative imaging, which has raised the possibility of a concurrent brachial plexopathy. Results of nerve conduction studies are pending.

Goals of physiotherapy include progressive lumbar stabilisation, hip flexor flexibility, gradual return to aquatic training, and graded desensitisation to address movement apprehension.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting post-surgical patients, always specify the procedure performed (e.g. "decompressive laminectomy at L4–L5"), the time since surgery (e.g. "eight weeks post-operatively"), and any psychological barriers (e.g. "apprehension regarding lumbar loading"). Addressing both physical and psychosocial factors demonstrates thorough clinical reasoning in OET Writing tasks.
