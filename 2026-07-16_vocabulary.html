Medical Definition

心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The non-invasive measurement of arterial oxygen saturation (SpO2) using a photoelectric sensor (pulse oximeter) applied to a peripheral site such as the fingertip or earlobe; used to guide oxygen therapy and monitor respiratory status during exercise.
繁體中文
血氧飽和度測量；脈搏血氧測定

OET Clinical Example
"Continuous pulse oximetry was commenced prior to the commencement of the exercise programme, with a predetermined threshold of SpO2 no less than 90% throughout the session; the patient was advised to cease activity immediately should desaturation occur."
Related Words
pulse oximeter (device) SpO2 (abbreviation) oxygen saturation oxygenation (process) desaturation (antonym outcome)

Word 5 of 5
hyperinflation
/ˌhaɪ.pər.ɪnˈfleɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 心肺 Cardiopulmonary
Medical Definition
(1) Pathological: abnormal over-distension of the lungs due to air trapping, commonly seen in obstructive conditions such as emphysema and severe asthma. (2) Therapeutic: a physiotherapy technique (manual hyperinflation) using a resuscitation bag to deliver supra-tidal breaths via an endotracheal tube, aiming to recruit collapsed alveoli and facilitate secretion clearance in ventilated patients.
繁體中文
肺過度充氣；（手動）過度充氣治療技術
Common Collocations
pulmonary hyperinflation dynamic hyperinflation manual hyperinflation lung hyperinflation air trapping with hyperinflation
OET Clinical Example
"Manual hyperinflation was performed via the endotracheal tube to facilitate mobilisation of tenacious bronchial secretions, in accordance with the unit protocol and following a review by the medical officer confirming the absence of contraindications."
Related Words
air trapping (mechanism) dynamic hyperinflation (exertional) manual hyperinflation / MHI (technique) barrel chest (clinical sign) emphysema (associated condition)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 69)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Nguyen, a 72-year-old male with a background of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD Gold Stage III) and type 2 diabetes mellitus, was admitted to the intensive care unit following acute decompensation secondary to aspiration pneumonia, confirmed on high-resolution CT chest.

On arrival, he was intubated and mechanically ventilated due to respiratory failure with SpO2 of 79% on supplemental oxygen. Continuous pulse oximetry was commenced, targeting SpO2 greater than 94%. Baseline chest X-ray demonstrated significant bilateral consolidation alongside pre-existing pulmonary hyperinflation consistent with his underlying COPD.

The intensive care physiotherapist commenced manual hyperinflation via the endotracheal tube to facilitate clearance of tenacious secretions, in conjunction with suction. Earlier in the admission, Mr. Nguyen had required resuscitation following a brief period of pulseless electrical activity; return of spontaneous circulation was achieved within four minutes. A tube thoracostomy was subsequently inserted on the left side to drain a parapneumonic effusion identified on ultrasound.

Goals of ICU physiotherapy include optimisation of ventilation, progressive weaning support, and early rehabilitation commencing with passive range-of-motion exercises as haemodynamic stability permits.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In ICU handover notes and referral letters, always specify the indication for each procedure (e.g. "tube thoracostomy for parapneumonic effusion"), the monitoring parameters (e.g. "oximetry targeting SpO2 >94%"), and the contraindications checked (e.g. "manual hyperinflation following medical officer review").
