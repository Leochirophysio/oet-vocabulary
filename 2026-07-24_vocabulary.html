corticospinal
/ˌkɔː.tɪ.kəʊˈspaɪ.nəl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
Relating to the corticospinal tract — the major nerve pathway that carries voluntary motor signals from the cerebral cortex down through the spinal cord to the muscles. Damage to this tract causes upper motor neurone signs.
繁體中文
皮質脊髓（束）的
Common Collocations
corticospinal tract corticospinal pathway corticospinal lesion corticospinal dysfunction bilateral corticospinal involvement
OET Clinical Example
"MRI findings confirm a left-sided infarct with involvement of the corticospinal tract at the level of the internal capsule, accounting for the patient's right-sided hemiplegia and upper motor neurone signs."
Related Words
pyramidal tract (synonym) upper motor neurone (UMN) lower motor neurone (LMN) spasticity (UMN sign) hemiplegia (UMN result)

Word 4 of 5
asterixis
/ˌæs.tərˈɪk.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A coarse, irregular, flapping tremor of the outstretched hands caused by brief lapses in sustained posture; often called a "flapping tremor." It is a sign of metabolic encephalopathy, most commonly seen in hepatic or uraemic encephalopathy.
繁體中文
撲翼性震顫（負性肌陣攣）
Common Collocations
bilateral asterixis asterixis on outstretched arms hepatic asterixis uraemic asterixis positive asterixis test
OET Clinical Example
"On neurological examination, bilateral asterixis was demonstrated with outstretched arms, consistent with the known diagnosis of hepatic encephalopathy secondary to decompensated liver cirrhosis."
Related Words
flapping tremor (lay/clinical synonym) negative myoclonus (physiological basis) encephalopathy (associated condition) tremor (broader category) hepatic encephalopathy (common context)

tenotomy
/tɪˈnɒt.ə.mi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A surgical procedure in which a tendon is partially or completely cut in order to correct a deformity, relieve spasticity, or lengthen a shortened muscle-tendon unit. Commonly performed in the treatment of talipes equinovarus, hip adductor spasticity, and congenital muscular torticollis.
繁體中文
肌腱切開術
Common Collocations
percutaneous tenotomy Achilles tenotomy adductor tenotomy tenotomy for spasticity open tenotomy
OET Clinical Example
"Following percutaneous Achilles tenotomy performed at six weeks of age, the infant was referred to physiotherapy for serial casting and subsequent bracing as part of the Ponseti protocol for bilateral talipes equinovarus."
Related Words
tendon release (lay equivalent) tenotomy scissors (instrument) tendon transfer (related procedure) fasciotomy (fascia equivalent) equinus (deformity treated)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 77)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Park, a 54-year-old male with a background of liver cirrhosis secondary to chronic alcohol use, was admitted to the medical ward following a fall at home. Neurological assessment on admission demonstrated bilateral asterixis on outstretched arms and mild confusion, consistent with hepatic encephalopathy grade II.

A subsequent MRI of the brain revealed no acute intracranial pathology. Physiotherapy was consulted to assess his functional mobility and neurological status. Motor examination revealed reduced neuromuscular control of the lower limbs, with difficulty maintaining dynamic balance during sit-to-stand transfers. There were no focal upper motor neurone signs to suggest corticospinal tract involvement on this occasion.

Additionally, he reported a longstanding history of right shoulder pain with palpable tightness of the upper trapezius and restricted cervical rotation to the right. This was attributed to chronic postural dysfunction secondary to his prolonged sedentary lifestyle.

Goals of physiotherapy include safe mobility re-education, neuromuscular re-education for dynamic balance, and a graded upper limb and cervical stretching programme targeting trapezius flexibility and shoulder range of motion.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When multiple systems are affected, use structured clinical language to distinguish each system's findings — e.g., "neurological examination revealed…" then "musculoskeletal assessment demonstrated…". This helps the receiving clinician prioritise management and avoids ambiguity in complex referrals.
