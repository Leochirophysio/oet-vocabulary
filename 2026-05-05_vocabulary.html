✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 16)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs. Chen Wei, a 68-year-old retired nurse with a known diagnosis of osteoporosis and bilateral hip osteoarthritis, was admitted following a low-energy fall at home that resulted in a Colles' fracture of the right wrist, managed conservatively with casting for six weeks.

On removal of the cast, the physiotherapy team identified residual tenosynovitis of the right wrist extensor tendons, contributing to significant functional limitation with daily tasks including dressing, meal preparation, and personal hygiene. A custom wrist orthosis was prescribed and fitted to offload the affected tendons during functional activities, with a scheduled review at two weeks to assess fit and tolerance.

Concurrently, electrotherapy comprising therapeutic ultrasound was commenced over the wrist extensor compartment to address localised pain and facilitate tendon sheath healing. A hydrotherapy programme was also initiated twice weekly to address generalised lower limb deconditioning and improve balance in a reduced weight-bearing environment, given her high risk of further fragility fractures.

Goals of the rehabilitation programme include: resolution of wrist tenosynovitis, independent management of the orthosis, fall prevention, and safe return to community ambulation.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting a rehabilitation plan in a referral letter, specify the frequency and duration of interventions (e.g. "twice weekly for six weeks"), the rationale for each modality (e.g. "reduced weight-bearing environment"), and measurable goals (e.g. "independent management of the orthosis"). Avoid generic phrases such as "physiotherapy was given."
