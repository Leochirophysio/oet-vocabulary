Word 1 of 5
hypotension
/ˌhaɪ.pəˈten.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormally low blood pressure, typically defined as systolic BP below 90 mmHg; clinically significant because it can cause dizziness, falls, and syncope — particularly when a patient moves from lying to standing (orthostatic hypotension).
繁體中文
低血壓
Common Collocations
orthostatic hypotension postural hypotension symptomatic hypotension hypotension on standing exercise-induced hypotension
OET Clinical Example
"The patient experienced symptomatic orthostatic hypotension on initial standing, with systolic blood pressure falling from 126 mmHg supine to 88 mmHg on rising, necessitating a modified mobilisation programme with graded position changes and close monitoring."
Related Words
hypertension (antonym) hypotensive (adj) postural hypotension (synonym) syncope (fainting) dizziness / lightheadedness

instability
/ˌɪn.stəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The inability of a joint to maintain normal alignment and control during movement or loading, often resulting from ligamentous laxity, muscular weakness, or structural damage to supporting structures.
繁體中文
不穩定性；關節不穩
Common Collocations
joint instability shoulder instability lumbar instability functional instability multidirectional instability
OET Clinical Example
"The patient described recurrent episodes of the right knee 'giving way' during stair descent, consistent with functional instability secondary to anterior cruciate ligament deficiency, and was referred for orthopaedic review prior to commencing strengthening rehabilitation."
Related Words
stability (antonym) unstable (adj) laxity (ligamentous looseness) giving way (patient report) proprioception (sensorimotor control)

arthroscopy
/ɑːˈθrɒs.kə.pi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A minimally invasive surgical procedure in which a small fibre-optic camera (arthroscope) is inserted into a joint through a small incision, allowing direct visualisation and treatment of intra-articular pathology without open surgery.
繁體中文
關節鏡檢查／手術
Common Collocations
diagnostic arthroscopy knee arthroscopy shoulder arthroscopy post-arthroscopy rehabilitation arthroscopy under general anaesthetic
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Patel underwent right knee arthroscopy and partial medial meniscectomy six weeks ago and has been referred to physiotherapy for post-operative rehabilitation, with current goals of restoring full range of motion and progressive quadriceps strengthening."
Related Words
arthroscope (instrument) arthroscopic (adj) arthrotomy (open joint surgery) arthroplasty (joint replacement) meniscectomy (meniscus removal)

inflammation
/ˌɪn.fləˈmeɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A localised protective tissue response to injury, infection, or irritation, characterised by the four cardinal signs: redness (rubor), swelling (tumor), heat (calor), and pain (dolor), often with loss of function (functio laesa).
繁體中文
發炎；炎症
Common Collocations
acute inflammation chronic inflammation joint inflammation local inflammation signs of inflammation
OET Clinical Example
"On assessment, localised inflammation was noted at the right Achilles tendon insertion, with erythema, warmth, and soft tissue swelling present, consistent with active insertional tendinopathy requiring load management and activity modification."
Related Words
inflammatory (adj) inflamed (adj) anti-inflammatory (treatment) erythema (redness) synovitis (joint lining inflammation)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 47)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mrs. Nguyen, a 58-year-old retired teacher, was referred to physiotherapy following right total knee replacement for end-stage osteoarthritis with a pre-operative fixed valgus deformity of 14 degrees.

On day two post-operatively, the patient experienced symptomatic orthostatic hypotension on initial mobilisation attempts, with systolic blood pressure falling from 130 mmHg supine to 86 mmHg on standing. Mobilisation was temporarily suspended and a programme of graded position changes, compression stockings, and vital sign monitoring was introduced prior to recommencing gait retraining.

At the time of physiotherapy assessment, clinical signs of localised inflammation were evident at the surgical site, including periarticular warmth, erythema, and moderate joint effusion. The patient also reported functional instability when attempting unsupported standing, attributable to residual quadriceps inhibition and pain.

Prior to her joint replacement, Mrs. Nguyen had undergone diagnostic arthroscopy of the same knee 18 months earlier, at which time extensive chondral damage and a complex medial meniscal tear were identified. Physiotherapy goals include progressive weight-bearing, resolution of post-surgical inflammation, and restoration of functional stability for safe community ambulation.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting surgical history in a referral letter, name the procedure precisely (e.g. "diagnostic arthroscopy", "total knee replacement") and include the timeframe. Avoid vague phrases like "had an operation" — specificity demonstrates professional register and aids the receiving clinician.
