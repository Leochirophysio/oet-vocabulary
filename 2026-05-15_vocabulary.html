fontanelle
/ˌfɒn.təˈnel/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A soft membranous gap between the incompletely ossified cranial bones in an infant's skull, which gradually closes as the bones fuse during early childhood; used clinically as an indicator of intracranial pressure and hydration status.
繁體中文
囟門
Common Collocations
anterior fontanelle posterior fontanelle bulging fontanelle sunken fontanelle fontanelle closure
OET Clinical Example
"On examination, the infant's anterior fontanelle was noted to be bulging and tense at rest, prompting urgent referral to the paediatric neurology team for further assessment."
Related Words
fontanel (American spelling) cranial suture ossification (bone formation) hydrocephalus (related condition) intracranial pressure

hyporeflexia
/ˌhaɪ.pəʊ.rɪˈfleks.i.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormal reduction or diminishment of deep tendon reflexes, typically indicating lower motor neurone lesion, peripheral neuropathy, or cerebellar dysfunction.
繁體中文
反射減退
Common Collocations
bilateral hyporeflexia deep tendon hyporeflexia hyporeflexia of lower limbs profound hyporeflexia hyporeflexia on testing
OET Clinical Example
"Neurological examination revealed bilateral hyporeflexia of the lower limbs with diminished patellar and Achilles tendon reflexes, consistent with a lower motor neurone lesion, and the patient was referred for electromyography."
Related Words
hyperreflexia (antonym — exaggerated reflexes) areflexia (absent reflexes) deep tendon reflex / DTR lower motor neurone lesion hyporeflexic (adj)
Word 3 of 5
febrile
/ˈfiː.braɪl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to, characterised by, or having a fever; a body temperature above the normal range (typically above 38°C in children). Used as a clinical descriptor in assessment and documentation.
繁體中文
發熱的；發燒的
Common Collocations
febrile seizure febrile convulsion febrile illness febrile episode become febrile
OET Clinical Example
"The child was noted to be febrile on initial assessment, with a tympanic temperature of 38.8°C, and physiotherapy was deferred pending medical review and clearance to proceed."
Related Words
afebrile (antonym — without fever) pyrexia (noun — fever) hyperpyrexia (very high fever) febrile convulsion febris (Latin root)
Word 4 of 5
talipes
/ˈtæl.ɪ.piːz/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A congenital deformity of the foot in which the foot is twisted out of its normal position; commonly known as clubfoot. The most prevalent form is talipes equinovarus, where the foot is turned inward and downward.
繁體中文
先天性足部畸形；馬蹄內翻足
Common Collocations
talipes equinovarus congenital talipes bilateral talipes talipes calcaneovalgus talipes correction
OET Clinical Example
"The infant was diagnosed with bilateral congenital talipes equinovarus at birth and has been referred for physiotherapy assessment to commence the Ponseti method of serial casting."
Related Words
clubfoot (lay term) equinus (plantarflexion deformity) varus (inward angulation) Ponseti method (treatment) calcaneus (heel bone)
Word 5 of 5
lethargy
/ˈleθ.ə.dʒi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A state of abnormal drowsiness, reduced responsiveness, and lack of physical or mental energy; in infants and children, a significant clinical indicator of systemic illness, neurological compromise, or deteriorating condition.
繁體中文
嗜睡；倦怠；精神萎靡
Common Collocations
profound lethargy post-ictal lethargy lethargy and irritability persistent lethargy increasing lethargy
OET Clinical Example
"The patient's carer reported increasing lethargy over the preceding 48 hours, with the child sleeping excessively and demonstrating markedly reduced responsiveness during routine physiotherapy; medical review was sought immediately."
Related Words
lethargic (adj) somnolence (clinical synonym — drowsiness) fatigue (less severe) stupor (deeper impairment) alertness (antonym)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 25)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Master Ethan Park, a six-week-old male infant, was referred to the paediatric physiotherapy service by the neonatal unit following concerns regarding neurological development and recent clinical deterioration.

On review, the infant's mother reported progressive lethargy over the preceding three days, with the child sleeping for extended periods and displaying reduced interest in feeding. On examination, the anterior fontanelle was noted to be mildly bulging and tense, prompting immediate escalation to the paediatric registrar for further assessment of intracranial pressure.

Neurological screening revealed bilateral hyporeflexia of the lower limbs, with diminished patellar reflexes bilaterally. The infant was also noted to be febrile at the time of assessment (temperature 38.4°C), and physiotherapy input was appropriately deferred pending medical stabilisation.

A separate finding on structural examination confirmed previously undiagnosed right-sided talipes equinovarus, for which orthopaedic and physiotherapy co-management via the Ponseti method has been recommended once the infant's acute presentation resolves. Goals of physiotherapy will subsequently include serial casting, family education, and developmental monitoring.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting paediatric assessments, specify the informant (parent/carer), note time course of symptoms (e.g. "over the preceding three days"), and clearly state when physiotherapy was deferred and why. Use objective measurements (e.g. temperature readings) alongside clinical descriptors.
