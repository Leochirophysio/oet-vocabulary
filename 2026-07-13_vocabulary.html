Word 1 of 5
hemineglect
/ˌhemɪnɪˈɡlekt/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A neurological condition in which a person fails to attend to, perceive, or respond to stimuli on one side of the body or environment; most commonly affecting the left side following a right-hemisphere stroke.
繁體中文
半側空間忽略
Common Collocations
left-sided hemineglect post-stroke hemineglect hemineglect syndrome hemineglect on assessment functional impact of hemineglect
OET Clinical Example
"Assessment revealed marked left-sided hemineglect, with the patient consistently failing to acknowledge stimuli presented to the left visual field during standardised cancellation tasks."
Related Words
unilateral neglect (synonym) visuospatial deficit (related) hemiplegia (common co-occurrence) right hemisphere lesion (cause) scanning training (treatment)
Word 2 of 5
ptosis
/ˈtəʊsɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Drooping of the upper eyelid due to weakness or paralysis of the levator palpebrae superioris muscle; may indicate cranial nerve III palsy, Horner syndrome, or neuromuscular disease.
繁體中文
眼瞼下垂
Common Collocations
unilateral ptosis bilateral ptosis partial ptosis congenital ptosis ptosis of the right eyelid
OET Clinical Example
"Neurological examination identified right-sided ptosis with associated pupillary dilation, prompting urgent referral for investigation of a possible third cranial nerve palsy."
Related Words
blepharoptosis (formal synonym) Horner syndrome (associated condition) cranial nerve III palsy (cause) levator palpebrae (muscle involved) diplopia (related sign)
Word 3 of 5
abulia
/əˈbjuːlɪə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A neurological symptom characterised by a marked reduction or absence of motivation, initiative, and spontaneous activity; commonly seen following frontal lobe injury, stroke, or in Parkinson's disease.
繁體中文
意志力缺乏症（缺乏動力）
Common Collocations
severe abulia post-stroke abulia abulia following TBI abulia and apathy abulic presentation
OET Clinical Example
"The patient demonstrated significant abulia following his right-hemisphere cerebrovascular accident, requiring substantial verbal cueing to initiate functional tasks throughout the rehabilitation session."
Related Words
apathy (related, less severe) akinetic mutism (severe form) executive dysfunction (related) frontal lobe syndrome (associated) motivation (antonym concept)
Word 4 of 5
paraphrase
/ˈpær.ə.freɪz/ verb / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
To restate what a patient has said in different words in order to demonstrate active listening, confirm accurate understanding of concerns, and encourage further disclosure during a clinical interview.
繁體中文
釋義、換句話說（臨床溝通技巧）
Common Collocations
paraphrase the patient's concern paraphrase to clarify paraphrase and check understanding accurate paraphrasing paraphrase and reflect
OET Clinical Example
"To confirm my understanding, I paraphrased Mrs. Tran's concern: 'It sounds as though you are worried that exercising will worsen your pain — have I understood you correctly?'"
Related Words
summarise (broader overview) reflect (emotional mirroring) clarify (related skill) active listening (context) open-ended question (related technique)
Word 5 of 5
acknowledge
/əkˈnɒlɪdʒ/ verb 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
In clinical communication, to recognise and verbally respond to a patient's concerns, emotions, or experiences in order to validate their feelings, demonstrate empathy, and build therapeutic rapport.
繁體中文
承認、認可（確認患者感受與顧慮）
Common Collocations
acknowledge the patient's concern acknowledge pain and discomfort acknowledge feelings acknowledge barriers to recovery acknowledge progress
OET Clinical Example
"I acknowledged Mr. Santos's frustration with his slow recovery by saying, 'I can hear that this has been very difficult for you, and your feelings are completely understandable.'"
Related Words
validate (close synonym) empathise (emotional response) reflect (communication skill) rapport (therapeutic outcome) dismiss (antonym)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 66)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 72-year-old male, was admitted to the stroke unit following a right middle cerebral artery infarction four weeks ago. He has been referred to your physiotherapy service for ongoing neurological rehabilitation prior to discharge planning.

On initial physiotherapy assessment, Mr. Park presented with moderate left hemiplegia and marked left-sided hemineglect, consistently failing to attend to stimuli on his left side during functional tasks such as dressing and transfers. In addition, right-sided ptosis was noted, which had been attributed to a concurrent Horner syndrome identified on admission, and had been reviewed by the neurology team.

Of particular concern to the rehabilitation team was Mr. Park's abulia, characterised by a marked reduction in spontaneous communication and self-initiated movement. He required consistent verbal cueing to begin exercises and frequently disengaged from tasks without prompting. During the assessment interview, I paraphrased his wife's description of his pre-morbid character as an active and independent man, and used this to frame goal-setting in a meaningful context for him.

I also ensured that I acknowledged both Mr. Park's and his wife's concerns regarding his prognosis, validating their anxiety whilst providing realistic but optimistic information about neurological recovery timelines.

Goals for the coming fortnight include visuospatial scanning training to address hemineglect, progressive transfer and mobility practice with adequate cueing strategies, and family education to support carryover of rehabilitation activities in the home environment.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting neurological presentations, specify the side affected (e.g. "left-sided hemineglect"), the functional impact (e.g. "failing to attend to stimuli during dressing"), and any communication strategies used (e.g. "paraphrased" and "acknowledged"). This demonstrates both clinical precision and patient-centred care.
