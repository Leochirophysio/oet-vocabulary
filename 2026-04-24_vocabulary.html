/ˌtæk.ɪpˈniːə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical

Medical Definition
An abnormally rapid respiratory rate, typically defined as more than 20 breaths per minute in adults; a common clinical sign of respiratory distress, infection, or cardiovascular compromise.

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 6)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Tautau, a 71-year-old male with a background of moderate COPD (GOLD Stage III) and ischaemic heart disease, was referred to the physiotherapy department for pulmonary rehabilitation following a 7-day hospital admission for an acute exacerbation of COPD.

On initial assessment, the patient displayed mild central cyanosis of the lips at rest, with oxygen saturations of 87% on room air, improving to 94% on 2 L/min supplemental oxygen via nasal cannula. Respiratory rate was 26 breaths per minute, consistent with moderate tachypnoea at rest.

Auscultation of the chest revealed widespread expiratory wheeze throughout all lung fields and coarse crackles at both bases. Percussion of the chest demonstrated bilateral hyperresonance, in keeping with known emphysematous changes.

The patient is currently receiving salbutamol and ipratropium bromide via nebulisation four times daily. Physiotherapy management will include active cycle of breathing technique (ACBT), pursed-lip breathing instruction, and a graduated walking programme commencing at low intensity with continuous oxygen saturation monitoring.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting respiratory assessments, always pair auscultation and percussion findings together, specify the exact respiratory rate (not just "fast breathing"), and note the oxygen flow rate and delivery device when reporting saturations. Avoid vague terms like "breathing problems."
