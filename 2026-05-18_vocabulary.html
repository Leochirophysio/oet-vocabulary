bradypnoea
/ˌbræd.ɪpˈniː.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormally slow respiratory rate, typically defined as fewer than 12 breaths per minute in adults; may indicate respiratory depression, neurological compromise, or medication side effects.
繁體中文
呼吸緩慢；緩慢呼吸
Common Collocations
opioid-induced bradypnoea bradypnoea and desaturation bradypnoea at rest symptomatic bradypnoea bradypnoea with altered consciousness
OET Clinical Example
"On arrival to the ward, the patient demonstrated bradypnoea with a respiratory rate of 8 breaths per minute and an oxygen saturation of 88%, prompting an urgent medical review prior to any physiotherapy intervention."
Related Words
tachypnoea (antonym — fast breathing) apnoea (cessation of breathing) eupnoea (normal breathing) bradycardia (slow heart rate — parallel term) respiratory depression (cause)

心肺 Cardiopulmonary
Medical Definition
The passage of blood through the capillaries of an organ or tissue, delivering oxygen and nutrients; adequate perfusion is essential for normal organ function.
繁體中文

Common Collocations

Related Words

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 27)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Park, a 74-year-old male with a background of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and type 2 diabetes, was admitted to the intensive care unit following an acute respiratory failure secondary to community-acquired pneumonia. He was commenced on non-invasive ventilation and remains under close observation.

Physiotherapy was requested to assist with respiratory management and early rehabilitation. On initial review, the patient was noted to be haemodynamically stable with a mean arterial pressure of 78 mmHg; however, his respiratory rate was 9 breaths per minute, indicating bradypnoea, with an SpO₂ of 90% on supplemental oxygen at 4 L/min.

There was a documented risk of aspiration associated with reduced consciousness and difficulty managing oral secretions. The treating team had already implemented aspiration precautions and elevated the head of the bed to 30 degrees. Assessment of peripheral perfusion revealed delayed capillary refill at 3.5 seconds and cool extremities to the mid-calf bilaterally.

Physiotherapy management included therapeutic positioning — alternating between semi-recumbent and lateral positions every two hours to optimise ventilation–perfusion matching and reduce the risk of pressure injury. Airway clearance techniques and breathing exercises were also initiated in keeping with the patient's tolerance.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting respiratory status in referral letters, always include the respiratory rate (with reference range), oxygen saturation, and supplemental oxygen requirements. Use precise clinical terms such as "bradypnoea" rather than "breathing slowly" to demonstrate professional register.
