Word 1 of 5
aponeurosis
/ˌæp.ə.njʊˈrəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A flat, sheet-like layer of fibrous connective tissue that functions as a broad tendon, attaching muscle to bone or joining muscles to adjacent structures; commonly found on the plantar surface of the foot (plantar aponeurosis) and the palm of the hand (palmar aponeurosis).
繁體中文
腱膜（扁平的纖維結締組織，功能如肌腱，連接肌肉與骨骼）
Common Collocations
plantar aponeurosis palmar aponeurosis aponeurosis attachment aponeurosis tear aponeurosis contracture
OET Clinical Example
"MRI findings confirmed a partial tear of the plantar aponeurosis at its calcaneal attachment, consistent with the patient's complaint of sharp, weight-bearing heel pain that was most severe upon taking the first steps in the morning."
Related Words
plantar fascia (clinical synonym) aponeurotic (adj) fasciitis (inflammation) fasciotomy (surgical release) tendon (similar structure)
Word 2 of 5
arthrocentesis
/ˌɑː.θrəʊ.senˈtiː.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A clinical procedure in which a sterile needle is inserted into a joint space to aspirate (withdraw) synovial fluid; performed for diagnostic analysis of the fluid (e.g. to exclude infection or gout) or for therapeutic relief of a painful, distended joint effusion.
繁體中文
關節穿刺術（用針穿入關節腔抽取滑液，用於診斷或治療）
Common Collocations
perform arthrocentesis diagnostic arthrocentesis therapeutic arthrocentesis arthrocentesis of the knee ultrasound-guided arthrocentesis
OET Clinical Example
"Following arthrocentesis of the left knee, approximately 35 mL of straw-coloured synovial fluid was aspirated; this significantly reduced the patient's reported pain and restored terminal knee extension, enabling commencement of active rehabilitation."
Related Words
joint aspiration (clinical synonym) synovial fluid (aspirated substance) effusion (joint fluid accumulation) arthroscopy (visual joint examination) thoracocentesis (analogous chest procedure)
Word 3 of 5
metatarsal
/ˌmet.əˈtɑː.səl/ adjective / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to or denoting the five long bones (metatarsals, numbered I–V from medial to lateral) of the forefoot, situated between the tarsal bones of the ankle and the proximal phalanges of the toes; a common site of stress fractures in runners and dancers.
繁體中文
蹠骨（足部前段連接踝骨與趾骨的五根長骨）
Common Collocations
metatarsal stress fracture fifth metatarsal metatarsal head metatarsal pain metatarsophalangeal joint
OET Clinical Example
"Plain X-ray revealed a non-displaced stress fracture of the second metatarsal shaft, consistent with the patient's reported increase in training load and localised dorsal foot pain worsened by weight-bearing activity."
Related Words
metatarsalgia (forefoot pain syndrome) phalanges (toe bones) tarsals (ankle bones) forefoot (anatomical region) stress fracture (common pathology)
Word 4 of 5
supination
/ˌsuː.pɪˈneɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
In the forearm: rotation at the radioulnar joint so that the palm faces anteriorly (upward when the arm is at the side), performed by the supinator and biceps brachii muscles. In the foot: a combined movement of inversion, plantarflexion, and adduction, raising the medial arch and directing the sole laterally. The opposite of pronation.
繁體中文
旋後（前臂旋轉使掌心向上；足部向外側翻轉，使足底朝外）
Common Collocations
forearm supination restricted supination pain on supination supination range of motion supination deformity
OET Clinical Example
"Active range of motion assessment revealed significantly restricted forearm supination on the right, measuring 40° compared with 85° on the left, and this limitation was attributed to adhesions at the distal radioulnar joint following a previous Colles' fracture."
Related Words
pronation (antonym) supinate (verb) supinator (muscle) radioulnar joint (site of movement) range of motion / ROM (assessment)
Word 5 of 5
thermotherapy
/ˌθɜː.məʊˈθer.ə.pi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The therapeutic application of heat to body tissues using superficial modalities (e.g. moist hot packs, paraffin wax baths, infrared lamps) or deep-heating agents (e.g. therapeutic ultrasound, shortwave diathermy), with the aim of reducing pain, increasing collagen extensibility, decreasing muscle guarding, and promoting local circulation.
繁體中文
熱療法（以熱能治療疼痛、改善組織伸展性及促進血液循環）
Common Collocations
apply thermotherapy superficial thermotherapy moist heat thermotherapy thermotherapy modalities thermotherapy contraindications
OET Clinical Example
"Superficial thermotherapy via a moist hot pack was applied to the lumbar region for 20 minutes prior to manual therapy, with the aim of increasing soft tissue extensibility, reducing muscle guarding, and preparing the tissues for subsequent joint mobilisation."
Related Words
cryotherapy (cold therapy — antonym) diathermy (deep thermotherapy) paraffin wax bath (modality) heat pack / hot pack (lay term) vasodilation (physiological effect)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 58)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Tran, a 34-year-old recreational distance runner, was referred to the physiotherapy department by his orthopaedic surgeon following surgical repair of a ruptured plantar aponeurosis of the right foot, sustained during a competitive marathon six weeks ago.

Intraoperatively, the surgeon also identified a concurrent non-displaced stress fracture of the third metatarsal shaft, which was managed conservatively with a cam boot for four weeks post-operatively. X-ray review at the six-week mark confirmed satisfactory callus formation at the fracture site, and the patient has since been cleared for progressive weight-bearing.

On initial physiotherapy assessment, Mr. Tran demonstrated reduced ankle dorsiflexion and restricted forearm supination on the right (55°, compared with 90° on the left), the latter attributed to altered load distribution during crutch use over the preceding weeks. A moderate effusion of the right ankle was noted, and following orthopaedic review, diagnostic arthrocentesis was performed, yielding 12 mL of clear synovial fluid with no evidence of infection or crystal deposition.

Treatment commenced with thermotherapy to the plantar surface of the foot and distal forearm prior to stretching and joint mobilisation, followed by a graded weight-bearing exercise programme. Goals include restoring full range of motion, improving proprioception, and enabling a safe return to running within twelve weeks.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting surgical or procedural history, specify the structure involved (e.g. "plantar aponeurosis"), the mechanism (e.g. "during a competitive marathon"), and the current functional status. This gives the receiving clinician a clear, actionable picture without requiring them to review the full medical record.
