quadriceps
/ˈkwɒd.rɪ.seps/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The large four-part muscle group on the anterior surface of the thigh, comprising the rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, and vastus intermedius; the primary muscle responsible for knee extension and hip flexion (rectus femoris only).
繁體中文
股四頭肌
Common Collocations
quadriceps weakness quadriceps strengthening quadriceps inhibition quadriceps lag quadriceps-to-hamstring ratio
OET Clinical Example
"Manual muscle testing revealed significant quadriceps weakness on the right (MRC grade 3/5), with a notable quadriceps lag of approximately 15 degrees during active straight leg raise, indicating incomplete active knee extension."
Related Words
vastus medialis oblique / VMO (component) rectus femoris (component) hamstrings (antagonist) knee extension (action) quadriceps tendon

tenderness
/ˈten.də.nəs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Pain or discomfort elicited when pressure is applied to a body part during examination; a key clinical sign used to localise pathology, assess severity, and guide differential diagnosis.
繁體中文
壓痛
Common Collocations

Related Words

Word 3 of 5
haemarthrosis
/hɪˌmɑːˈθrəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The accumulation of blood within a joint cavity, typically resulting from trauma (e.g., ligament rupture, intra-articular fracture), haemophilia, or anticoagulant therapy. It presents as a rapidly developing, tense, warm, and painful joint swelling.
繁體中文
關節積血
Common Collocations
acute haemarthrosis traumatic haemarthrosis haemarthrosis of the knee aspirate a haemarthrosis haemarthrosis following injury
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented to the emergency department with an acute haemarthrosis of the left knee following a twisting injury during football, with marked swelling developing within one hour of the incident, raising concern for anterior cruciate ligament rupture."
Related Words
effusion (fluid in joint — not blood) lipohaemarthrosis (fat and blood in joint) haemophilia (common cause) arthrocentesis (drainage procedure) haemarthrotic (adjective)
Word 4 of 5
contralateral
/ˌkɒn.trəˈlæt.ər.əl/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Pertaining to or situated on the opposite side of the body from a reference point, injury, or pathology; the antonym of ipsilateral. Commonly used in neurological, orthopaedic, and rehabilitation documentation.
繁體中文
對側的
Common Collocations
contralateral limb contralateral side contralateral weakness contralateral trunk lean contralateral hip drop
OET Clinical Example
"Gait analysis revealed a contralateral hip drop during single-leg stance on the right, indicative of gluteus medius weakness, which was further confirmed by a positive Trendelenburg sign on the ipsilateral side."
Related Words
ipsilateral (antonym — same side) bilateral (both sides) unilateral (one side) contralaterally (adverb) crossed (synonym in some contexts)

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 61)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Holt, a 24-year-old male recreational footballer, sustained a significant twisting injury to his right knee during a match three days ago. He was initially assessed in the emergency department, where imaging confirmed an acute haemarthrosis and a complete anterior cruciate ligament rupture. He has been referred to your physiotherapy department for pre-operative rehabilitation prior to planned ACL reconstruction.

On initial assessment, Mr. Holt presented with marked swelling and point tenderness along the medial joint line bilaterally, with end-range flexion restricted to 85 degrees passively. Strength assessment revealed significant quadriceps inhibition (MRC grade 3/5) on the right in comparison to the contralateral limb (MRC grade 5/5), consistent with arthrogenic muscle inhibition secondary to the acute joint effusion.

Short-term physiotherapy goals include reduction of swelling, restoration of full passive range of motion, and recovery of quadriceps activation prior to surgical intervention. The patient has been counselled regarding realistic expectations for post-operative rehabilitation and has expressed strong motivation to return to competitive sport within nine months.

📌 OET Writing Tip: In referral letters for post-injury or pre-operative patients, always document the mechanism of injury, imaging findings, objective strength and range of motion data, and short-term goals. Compare the affected side to the contralateral limb to contextualise functional deficits.
