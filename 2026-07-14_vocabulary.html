Word 1 of 5
kernicterus
/ˌkɜː.nɪkˈtɛ.rəs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric 神經 Neurological
Medical Definition
Severe neurological damage in neonates caused by excessive bilirubin crossing the blood-brain barrier, resulting in brain injury and potentially permanent disability including cerebral palsy, hearing loss, and abnormal eye movements.
繁體中文
核黃疸（膽紅素腦病）
Common Collocations
acute kernicterus risk of kernicterus kernicterus secondary to hyperbilirubinaemia prevent kernicterus chronic kernicterus
OET Clinical Example
"The neonate was urgently referred to the neonatal intensive care unit following documentation of a total serum bilirubin of 425 µmol/L, raising significant concerns regarding the risk of kernicterus and irreversible neurological injury."
Related Words
hyperbilirubinaemia (cause) jaundice (clinical sign) bilirubin encephalopathy (synonym) phototherapy (treatment) exchange transfusion (severe cases)
Word 2 of 5
craniosynostosis
/ˌkreɪ.ni.əʊˌsɪn.ɒˈstəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Premature fusion of one or more fibrous joints (sutures) between the skull bones of an infant, restricting normal brain growth and causing abnormal skull shape; may be isolated or part of a genetic syndrome.
繁體中文
顱縫早閉症
Common Collocations
sagittal craniosynostosis metopic craniosynostosis coronal craniosynostosis non-syndromic craniosynostosis surgical correction of craniosynostosis
OET Clinical Example
"This four-month-old male presents with a palpable ridge along the sagittal suture and an elongated anterior-posterior skull shape consistent with sagittal craniosynostosis, confirmed on CT imaging; referral to neurosurgery is pending."
Related Words
plagiocephaly (asymmetric head shape) scaphocephaly (boat-shaped skull) trigonocephaly (triangular forehead) fontanelle (suture junction) craniotomy (surgical treatment)

小兒 Paediatric Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Present at or existing from birth; used to describe conditions, structural abnormalities, or diseases that develop during fetal development, whether genetic or caused by environmental factors during pregnancy.
繁體中文
先天性的
Common Collocations
congenital heart defect congenital abnormality congenital muscular torticollis congenital hip dysplasia congenital disorder

Related Words

asthenia
/æsˈθiː.ni.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Abnormal physical weakness or lack of bodily strength; a generalised loss of energy and muscular power that may result from serious illness, prolonged inactivity, malnutrition, or systemic disease.
繁體中文
虛弱無力症
Common Collocations
generalised asthenia profound asthenia asthenia secondary to deconditioning asthenia following chemotherapy marked asthenia
OET Clinical Example
"Mr Thompson presents with marked asthenia following his third cycle of chemotherapy, reporting an inability to perform basic activities of daily living independently and requiring physiotherapy input to address his significant functional decline."
Related Words
weakness (lay term) fatigue (subjective tiredness) myasthenia (muscle-specific weakness) debility (general frailty) neurasthenia (nervous exhaustion)
Word 5 of 5
nocturia
/nɒkˈtjʊə.ri.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The need to wake from sleep during the night to urinate; excessive nighttime urinary frequency that disrupts sleep and may indicate an underlying condition such as cardiac failure, diabetes, or prostatic hypertrophy.
繁體中文
夜尿症
Common Collocations
nocturia two or more times nightly nocturia secondary to cardiac failure bothersome nocturia nocturia associated with sleep disturbance nocturia on diuretic therapy
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs Patel reports nocturia three to four times nightly, which she attributes to her poorly controlled chronic heart failure; the resulting sleep disruption contributes to her daytime fatigue and reduced participation in her rehabilitation programme."
Related Words
polyuria (excessive urine production) urinary frequency (daytime equivalent) nocturnal enuresis (involuntary bedwetting) diuretic (medication causing increased urination) oliguria (antonym — reduced urine output)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 67)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Joshua Williams is a 10-week-old male infant born at 37+2 weeks gestation. On day three of life, he presented with a total serum bilirubin of 435 µmol/L. Despite intensive phototherapy, his bilirubin continued to rise, and he subsequently developed signs consistent with acute bilirubin encephalopathy (kernicterus), including high-pitched cry, opisthotonus, and impaired visual tracking.

Joshua is known to have a congenital G6PD deficiency, which was identified as the precipitating cause of his severe hyperbilirubinaemia. He was managed with exchange transfusion and has since been discharged from NICU. He is now referred to the paediatric physiotherapy team for neurodevelopmental surveillance and early intervention.

On separate review, a ridge was noted along his metopic suture on routine head circumference measurement, and craniosynostosis has been queried. CT head has been requested; pending neurosurgical review. Physiotherapy is asked to monitor developmental milestones, provide family education on tummy time and positioning, and commence early sensorimotor stimulation.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing paediatric referral letters, always document gestational age at birth, corrected age (if preterm), and relevant perinatal history. Use precise clinical terms (e.g. "bilirubin encephalopathy" rather than "brain damage from jaundice") and specify which suture is affected in craniosynostosis rather than describing it vaguely.
