Medical Definition
Partial or complete collapse of lung tissue, resulting in reduced or absent air entry to the affected area; commonly seen postoperatively or in patients with reduced respiratory effort.

Common Collocations

Related Words

Medical Definition

✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 13)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr James Tahi, a 54-year-old male with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and type 2 diabetes mellitus, was admitted to the intensive care unit following an acute respiratory failure secondary to severe community-acquired pneumonia.

Chest computed tomography confirmed extensive right lower lobe consolidation with associated atelectasis of the right middle lobe. He developed progressive hypoxia refractory to high-flow nasal oxygen, with SpO₂ maintaining at 88–90% on 15 L/min, necessitating intubation and mechanical ventilation.

Following ten days of ventilatory support, a percutaneous tracheostomy was inserted to facilitate weaning and airway management. A diagnostic thoracocentesis was also performed to sample a moderate left pleural effusion identified on imaging, with results pending.

Mr Tahi has been referred to physiotherapy for respiratory management, including airway clearance techniques, progressive ventilator weaning support, and planning for early mobilisation as his clinical condition permits.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing referral letters involving intensive care patients, specify the indication for each intervention (e.g. "hypoxia refractory to high-flow oxygen"), the current clinical status (e.g. "ventilator-dependent via tracheostomy"), and the specific physiotherapy goals (e.g. "airway clearance, weaning support, early mobilisation"). Avoid passive constructions — use active, precise language.
