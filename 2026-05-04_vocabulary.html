tachycardia
/ˌtæk.ɪˈkɑː.di.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
An abnormally rapid heart rate, typically defined as exceeding 100 beats per minute at rest in adults; may be a normal physiological response to exercise or stress, or indicative of an underlying pathological condition.
繁體中文
心搏過速；心動過速
Common Collocations
sinus tachycardia resting tachycardia exertional tachycardia compensatory tachycardia sustained tachycardia
OET Clinical Example
"On pre-treatment assessment, the patient demonstrated a resting tachycardia of 114 bpm with associated diaphoresis; physiotherapy was deferred and the treating medical officer notified immediately."
Related Words
bradycardia (antonym — slow heart rate) tachycardic (adj) arrhythmia (irregular rhythm) palpitations (subjective awareness) supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)

pneumothorax
/ˌnjuː.məʊˈθɔː.ræks/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
The abnormal presence of air or gas within the pleural cavity — the space between the lung and the chest wall — causing partial or complete collapse of the affected lung.
繁體中文
氣胸
Common Collocations
spontaneous pneumothorax tension pneumothorax traumatic pneumothorax suspected pneumothorax pneumothorax resolution
OET Clinical Example
"Mr Park was admitted following a spontaneous pneumothorax confirmed on chest X-ray; physiotherapy was deferred pending chest drain insertion and radiological evidence of lung re-expansion."
Related Words
haemothorax (blood in pleural space) haemopneumothorax (both blood and air) pleural effusion (fluid in pleural space) chest drain / intercostal drain pleurodesis (procedure to prevent recurrence)

syncope
/ˈsɪŋ.kə.pi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
A transient, self-limited loss of consciousness due to a sudden reduction in cerebral perfusion, commonly referred to as fainting; typically preceded by prodromal symptoms such as lightheadedness, nausea, or visual disturbance.
繁體中文
昏厥；暈厥
Common Collocations
vasovagal syncope exertional syncope syncopal episode pre-syncope (near-fainting) recurrent syncope
OET Clinical Example
"The patient reported a syncopal episode during the previous therapy session, preceded by lightheadedness and diaphoresis; exercise was suspended and the treating physician was notified to review prior to resuming physiotherapy."
Related Words
syncopal (adj) pre-syncope / near-syncope vasovagal (common mechanism) fainting (lay term) loss of consciousness (LOC)

pleurisy
/ˈplʊər.ɪ.si/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inflammation of the pleura — the two-layered membrane that surrounds the lungs and lines the chest cavity — characterised by sharp, stabbing chest pain that typically worsens with breathing, coughing, or movement.
繁體中文
胸膜炎；肋膜炎
Common Collocations
pleuritic chest pain acute pleurisy pleurisy secondary to pneumonia infectious pleurisy pleurisy management
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs Nguyen was referred with a diagnosis of pleurisy secondary to community-acquired pneumonia, presenting with sharp, pleuritic chest pain rated 8/10, which significantly restricted deep breathing and impaired effective cough."
Related Words
pleuritic (adj — describes pain quality) pleura (the membrane itself) pleuritis (medical synonym) pneumothorax (related condition) pleurodesis (surgical procedure)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 15)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr James Tui, a 34-year-old male with a history of spontaneous pneumothorax three years prior, presented to the emergency department with sudden-onset right-sided chest pain and breathlessness. A chest X-ray confirmed a recurrent right-sided pneumothorax with 40% lung collapse. An intercostal chest drain was inserted and the patient was admitted to the respiratory ward.

During his inpatient admission, Mr Tui was referred to physiotherapy for airway clearance management. He was producing moderate amounts of thick, cream-coloured sputum, and reported associated sharp, pleuritic pain consistent with pleurisy around the drain insertion site, which was limiting his ability to perform an effective cough.

On assessment, vital signs revealed a resting tachycardia of 108 bpm, which settled to 88 bpm following adequate analgesia. The patient also reported a syncopal episode on the previous day when mobilising to the bathroom, preceded by dizziness and nausea; bed rest was reinstated pending further cardiac review.

Goals of physiotherapy include effective sputum clearance via active cycle of breathing techniques, pain management strategies to facilitate deep breathing, and cautious progressive mobilisation once cardiac and respiratory parameters are stabilised.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting safety concerns in referrals, always state the specific vital sign values (e.g. "resting tachycardia of 108 bpm"), the action taken (e.g. "bed rest reinstated"), and the condition for resumption (e.g. "once cardiac parameters are stabilised"). This demonstrates professional clinical reasoning to the receiving practitioner.
