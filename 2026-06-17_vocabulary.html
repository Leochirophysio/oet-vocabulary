Medical Definition
Persistently elevated arterial blood pressure, defined as a systolic reading of ≥140 mmHg and/or a diastolic reading of ≥90 mmHg; a major modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular and cerebrovascular disease.

Medical Definition
Chest pain or pressure caused by insufficient blood supply to the myocardium (cardiac ischaemia), typically triggered by physical exertion or emotional stress and relieved by rest or nitrates.

crackles
/ˈkræk.əlz/ noun (plural) 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Discontinuous, non-musical adventitious breath sounds heard on auscultation; classified as fine (high-pitched, associated with pulmonary fibrosis or oedema) or coarse (low-pitched, associated with retained secretions or bronchiectasis).
繁體中文
爆裂音（肺部聽診異常音）
Common Collocations
fine crackles coarse crackles basal crackles inspiratory crackles bilateral crackles
OET Clinical Example
"On auscultation, bilateral fine crackles were noted at the lung bases, consistent with pulmonary oedema; the treating respiratory physician was notified and chest physiotherapy was deferred pending medical review."
Related Words
crepitations (British synonym) rales (older/American term) wheeze (continuous sound) rhonchi (low continuous) adventitious sounds (category)
Word 4 of 5
vasodilatation
/ˌveɪ.zəʊˌdaɪ.ləˈteɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
The widening of blood vessels resulting from relaxation of smooth muscle in the vessel wall, leading to increased blood flow, reduced vascular resistance, and lower blood pressure; can be induced therapeutically or occur as a physiological response.
繁體中文
血管擴張
Common Collocations
peripheral vasodilatation reactive vasodilatation heat-induced vasodilatation vasodilatation response cutaneous vasodilatation
OET Clinical Example
"Thermotherapy was applied to the lumbar region for fifteen minutes prior to manual therapy; the resultant local vasodilatation increased tissue extensibility and facilitated a more effective treatment response."
Related Words
vasoconstriction (antonym) vasodilator (agent) vasodilatory (adj) perfusion (increased blood flow) vascular resistance (reduced)
Word 5 of 5
orthostatic
/ˌɔː.θəˈstæt.ɪk/ adjective 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
心肺 Cardiopulmonary Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to or caused by the assumption of an upright posture; used clinically to describe haemodynamic changes (particularly a drop in blood pressure) that occur when a patient moves from lying or sitting to standing.
繁體中文
體位性的；直立性的
Common Collocations
orthostatic hypotension orthostatic dizziness orthostatic intolerance orthostatic vital signs orthostatic challenge
OET Clinical Example
"Orthostatic hypotension was confirmed on assessment, with a systolic drop of 24 mmHg upon standing from supine; early mobilisation was progressed cautiously with a head-up tilt table prior to full weight-bearing."
Related Words
postural hypotension (synonym) orthostasis (noun form) syncope (consequence) tilt table (assessment tool) haemodynamic (broader term)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 57)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr David Park, a 67-year-old male with a background of ischaemic heart disease and long-standing hypertension (currently managed with ramipril 10 mg and amlodipine 5 mg daily), was referred to the cardiac rehabilitation programme following elective percutaneous coronary intervention three weeks ago.

On initial physiotherapy assessment, Mr Park reported stable angina precipitated by moderate exertion (walking uphill or climbing more than one flight of stairs), typically resolving within four minutes of rest. His resting blood pressure was 152/88 mmHg. Orthostatic hypotension was confirmed on standing assessment, with a systolic drop of 20 mmHg, necessitating a seated warm-up protocol and gradual positional transitions prior to upright exercise.

Auscultation of the lung fields revealed bilateral fine crackles at the bases; the treating cardiologist was notified and the session was modified accordingly. Thermotherapy was applied to the lower limbs post-exercise to promote local vasodilatation and facilitate recovery of peripheral circulation.

Goals of rehabilitation include progressive aerobic conditioning, blood pressure optimisation during exercise, patient education regarding angina recognition and appropriate rest strategies, and safe return to independent community ambulation.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting cardiovascular findings in referral letters, always include specific values (e.g. "systolic drop of 20 mmHg"), triggers and relieving factors for angina, and the clinical action taken in response to abnormal findings (e.g. "cardiologist was notified"). This demonstrates sound clinical reasoning.
