piriformis
/ˌpɪr.ɪˈfɔː.mɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical

Medical Definition
A small, pear-shaped deep gluteal muscle originating from the anterior sacrum and inserting into the greater trochanter of the femur. Functions as an external rotator and abductor of the hip; its proximity to the sciatic nerve makes it clinically significant in posterior hip and buttock pain.

OET Clinical Example

Related Words
sciatic nerve (adjacent structure) deep gluteal syndrome hip external rotators sacrum (origin) greater trochanter (insertion)

sacroiliac
/ˌsæk.rəʊˈɪl.i.æk/ adjective / noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Relating to, or describing, the joint formed between the sacrum and the ilium at the posterior pelvis. The sacroiliac (SI) joint transmits load between the spine and lower limbs and is a common source of low back and posterior pelvic pain.

Related Words
sacrum (medial bone) ilium (lateral bone) SI joint (abbreviation) posterior pelvic pain FABER test (provocation test)

manipulation
/məˌnɪp.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
骨科 Musculoskeletal Procedures & Interventions
Medical Definition
A high-velocity, low-amplitude (HVLA) thrust technique applied to a joint by a physiotherapist to restore joint mobility, reduce pain, or address mechanical restriction. Distinguished from mobilisation by the speed and amplitude of the applied force.
繁體中文
推拿手法；關節整復術
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Thoracic manipulation was performed at the T4–T6 levels with the patient in the supine position, producing an audible cavitation and an immediate improvement in thoracic extension range of motion from 15° to 30°."
Related Words
mobilisation (lower-velocity technique) HVLA thrust cavitation (audible pop) manipulate (verb) manual therapy (broader term)
Word 5 of 5
cryotherapy
/ˌkraɪ.əʊˈθer.ə.pi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Procedures & Interventions 骨科 Musculoskeletal
Medical Definition
The therapeutic application of cold (ice packs, cold water immersion, or cryogenic sprays) to reduce pain, oedema, and tissue metabolism following acute injury, post-operatively, or after exercise. Works by inducing vasoconstriction and reducing nerve conduction velocity.
繁體中文
冷凍療法；冰療
Common Collocations

OET Clinical Example
"Local cryotherapy was applied to the right knee for 20 minutes following therapeutic exercise to manage post-exercise oedema and provide short-term analgesia prior to discharge."
Related Words
thermotherapy (antonym — heat application) ice therapy / cold therapy (lay terms) vasoconstriction (mechanism) RICE protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) analgesia (pain relief effect)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 30)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. James Mitchell, a 45-year-old male tradesman, was referred to physiotherapy by his general practitioner for management of right-sided low back and posterior pelvic pain of three weeks' duration following a heavy lifting incident at work.

Observational gait analysis on initial assessment revealed a mild antalgic pattern with increased ipsilateral trunk lean during the right stance phase. Lumbar and sacroiliac joint assessment demonstrated significant tenderness on palpation over the right posterior superior iliac spine, with positive provocation tests (FABER, Gaenslen's, and posterior pelvic pain provocation test), confirming right sacroiliac joint dysfunction as the primary diagnosis.

Secondary assessment identified hypertonic piriformis bilaterally, with restricted hip internal rotation and reproduction of posterior thigh pain on resisted external rotation of the right hip. Treatment during the initial session included lumbar manipulation at the L5–S1 segment (with patient consent and contraindications excluded), followed by soft tissue release of the right piriformis and instruction in a home stretching programme. Cryotherapy was applied to the right sacroiliac region for 20 minutes post-treatment to manage acute-on-chronic pain.

The patient was reviewed at 48 hours, reporting a 40% reduction in pain and improved gait mechanics. A progressive home exercise programme targeting sacroiliac joint stabilisation and hip rotator strengthening was commenced.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When documenting manual therapy techniques such as manipulation, always record the spinal level treated (e.g. "L5–S1"), the patient position, outcome measures (e.g. range of motion in degrees), and that consent was obtained. This demonstrates clinical reasoning and meets medico-legal documentation standards expected in OET Writing Task B.
