ataxia
/əˈtæk.si.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Inability to coordinate voluntary muscle movements smoothly; characterised by an unsteady, wide-based gait, impaired balance, and poor fine motor control, typically resulting from cerebellar or sensory pathway dysfunction.
繁體中文
共濟失調
Common Collocations
cerebellar ataxia gait ataxia truncal ataxia sensory ataxia progressive ataxia
OET Clinical Example
"The patient presented with marked cerebellar ataxia, demonstrating a wide-based unsteady gait and an inability to perform tandem walking without loss of balance on formal assessment."
Related Words
ataxic (adj) dysmetria (inaccurate limb movement) incoordination (lay term) cerebellar dysfunction dysdiadochokinesia (rapid alternating movement failure)

hemiplegia
/ˌhem.ɪˈpliː.dʒi.ə/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Complete paralysis of one entire side of the body (arm, leg, and sometimes the face), typically caused by a stroke, brain tumour, or traumatic brain injury affecting the contralateral motor cortex or corticospinal tract.
繁體中文
偏癱 / 半身不遂
Common Collocations
left-sided hemiplegia right-sided hemiplegia spastic hemiplegia post-stroke hemiplegia flaccid hemiplegia
OET Clinical Example
"Mr. Chen presents with left-sided hemiplegia following a right MCA territory ischaemic stroke three weeks ago, requiring maximum assistance for all transfers and activities of daily living."
Related Words
hemiplegic (adj) hemiparesis (partial one-sided weakness) paraplegia (both lower limbs) tetraplegia / quadriplegia (all four limbs) plegia (suffix: complete paralysis)
Word 3 of 5
neuropathy
/njʊˈrɒp.ə.θi/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
神經 Neurological Signs & Symptoms
Medical Definition
Disease, damage, or dysfunction of one or more peripheral nerves, causing weakness, numbness, tingling, or burning pain, most commonly in the hands and feet; may be caused by diabetes, toxins, trauma, or systemic illness.
繁體中文
神經病變
Common Collocations
peripheral neuropathy diabetic neuropathy sensorimotor neuropathy chemotherapy-induced neuropathy compressive neuropathy
OET Clinical Example
"Mrs. Patel was referred with a background of diabetic peripheral neuropathy, reporting bilateral foot numbness and recurrent falls attributed to significantly reduced proprioceptive feedback in both lower limbs."
Related Words
neuropathic (adj) neuropathic pain polyneuropathy (multiple nerves) mononeuropathy (single nerve) radiculopathy (nerve root)
Word 4 of 5
prognosis
/prɒɡˈnəʊ.sɪs/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
A clinical forecast of the likely course, duration, and outcome of a disease or injury, based on assessment findings, the nature of the condition, and evidence-based research; communicated to the patient to guide realistic goal-setting.
繁體中文
預後
Common Collocations
guarded prognosis favourable prognosis poor prognosis functional prognosis long-term prognosis
OET Clinical Example
"In view of the extent of the spinal cord injury and the absence of motor function below T6, the team discussed a guarded prognosis for independent ambulation with the patient and his family, focusing goals on functional wheelchair mobility and upper limb independence."
Related Words
prognostic (adj) prognostic indicators / factors diagnosis (identifying the condition) outcome (result of treatment) prognoses (plural)
Word 5 of 5
concordance
/kənˈkɔː.dəns/ noun 🔊 Cambridge 📖 M-W Medical
Patient Communication
Medical Definition
A collaborative agreement between the patient and clinician regarding treatment goals, management plans, and self-care strategies; reflects the patient's understanding, acceptance, and active participation in their own care. The preferred modern clinical term over 'compliance'.
繁體中文
治療共識 / 患者配合度
Common Collocations
patient concordance concordance with home exercises good concordance poor concordance achieve concordance
OET Clinical Example
"Excellent concordance with the prescribed home exercise programme was reported at the four-week review, with the patient demonstrating correct technique and consistent adherence to the recommended frequency of twice daily."
Related Words
compliance (older term) adherence (following treatment) non-concordance (antonym) patient engagement self-efficacy (belief in own ability)
✍️ OET Writing Task — Clinical Scenario (Day 10)

Read the scenario below. Notice how today's vocabulary is used in a realistic OET-style referral context. Highlighted words are from today's list.

Mr. David Tran, a 54-year-old male, was admitted to the neurology ward following a right-sided ischaemic stroke resulting in left-sided hemiplegia and moderate cognitive impairment. He was referred to the physiotherapy team on day three post-admission for neurological rehabilitation.

Initial assessment revealed dense left upper and lower limb paralysis with no active voluntary movement below the shoulder. Balance and postural control were severely compromised, with marked truncal ataxia noted in supported sitting. A background history of type 2 diabetes was significant, as pre-existing peripheral neuropathy in both feet was likely to complicate the rehabilitation trajectory and sensory re-education process.

The treating team communicated a guarded prognosis for independent functional mobility to both Mr. Tran and his family, acknowledging that recovery would be prolonged and dependent on neurological plasticity over the coming months. Initial rehabilitation goals were centred on supported standing practice, transfer training, and upper limb positioning to prevent secondary complications.

At the two-week review, Mr. Tran demonstrated good concordance with his prescribed bedside exercise programme and reported feeling motivated to engage actively in all therapy sessions. This positive engagement was documented as a favourable prognostic indicator.

📌 OET Writing Tip: When writing neurological referral letters, always specify the affected side (left/right), the degree of impairment (dense, moderate, mild), and any complicating factors (e.g. pre-existing neuropathy). Use prognosis with a qualifying adjective (guarded, favourable, poor) and document concordance to reflect patient engagement — this is preferred over "compliance" in modern clinical writing.
